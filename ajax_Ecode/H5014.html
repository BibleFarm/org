H5014. nabab

[נָבַב‎] verb hollow out (compare Assyrian imbubu, flute, DlHWB 443, Late Hebrew אַבּוּב‎ id., Aramaic אַבּוּבָא‎ id.; hence also Arabic knots in reed, part of reed between knots, reed; see HoffmLCB 1882, 321 Frä23 Fl in LevyTW i. 417); —

Qal Passive participle נָבוּב‎ Job 11:12; Jer 52:21; נְבוּב‎ Exod 27:8; 38:7; — hollowed, hollow: of altar of tabernacle לֻחוֺת נְבוּב‎ 27:8; 38:7 (P); of pillar in temple Jer 52:21; figurative נָבוּב אִישׁ‎ Job 11:12, i.e. empty, hollow-minded man.
