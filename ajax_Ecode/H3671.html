1 wing,

a. of birds Isa 10:14 (in figurative), Exod 19:4 (E ? R ? figurative) Deut 32:11 (in simile), Zech 5:9; Lev 1:17 (P), Job 39:13, 26; Psa 68:14; in figure of invading king Jer 48:40; 49:22; Ezek 17:3, 7; so apparently Isa 8:8 of invader as overflowing river (but Du Che separate כְּנָפָיו מֻטּוֺת וְהָיָה‎ from preceding); of riches, as flying away Prov 23:5; in phrase כָּנָף צִמּוֺר‎ = winged birds Deut 4:17; Psa 148:10, כָּלכֿ ׳(עֵיט)צִמּוֺר‎ Ezek 39:4, 17, כָּלכֿ צִמּוֺר ׳כֹּל‎ Gen 7:14 (P), Ezek 17:23, כ ׳עוֺף‎ Gen 1:21 (P), Psa 78:27; also כ ׳בַּעַל‎ Prov 1:17 = winged thing, compare בַּעַלהֿ֯כְּנָפַיִם‎ Eccl 10:20.

b. of insects (probably), כְּנָפָ֑יִם צִלְצַל אֶרֶץ‎ Isa 18:1 buzzing, or humming of wings, i.e. Ethiopia, so called from its swarms of flies, with especial reference possibly to the tsetse-fly (so Du, compare Che; id., in General, Hi De; > Kn Di of the tropical shadow falling both ways; Thes1167b of noise of the wings of an army; reference to sails as wings — compare 18:2 — would be suitable, but צִלְצַל‎ does not favour this).

c. of cherubim 1Kin 6:24 (4 t. in verse) + 8 t. Kings, compare 2Chr 3:11 (4 t. in verse) + 6 t. Chronicles; Ezek 1:6 17t. Ezekiel; Exod 25:20 (twice in verse); 39:9 (twice in verse) (all P).

d. of seraphim Isa 6:2 (twice in verse).

e. of women in Zechariah's vision Zech 5:9 (twice in verse).

f. figurative of the wind, Hosea 4:19; Psa 18:11 = 2Sam 22:11; Psa 104:3.

g. figurative of the dawn (winged sun-disc ?) Psa 139:9, compare of sun of righteousness Malachi 3:20.

h. figurative of ׳י‎, as protector of his people Psa 17:8; 36:8; 57:2; 61:5; 63:3; 91:4; Ruth 2:12.

2 extremity: —

a. of garment = skirt, corner, or loose flowing end, 1Sam 15:27; 24:5; 24:6; 24:12 (twice in verse); Deut 22:12; 23:1; 27:20; Jer 2:34 (figurative), Ezek 5:3; 16:8 (in figurative), Hag 2:12 (twice in verse); Zech 8:23; Num 15:38 (twice in verse) (P; compare RSSem, i. 416, 2nd ed. 437).

b. of the earth, הָאָרֶץ מִכְּנַף‎ Isa 24:16 from the end of the earth; elsewhere plural הָא ׳כַּנְפוֺת‎ Job 37:3; 38:13, and, def. כ ׳אַרְבַּע ׳הא‎ Isa 11:12 the four corners of the earth; so of the holy land Ezek 7:2. — מְשׁוֺמֵם שִׁקּוּצִים עַלכְּֿנַף‎ Dan 9:27 is obscure; Meinh reads, after Vrss., כָּנָף‎, on the corner (of the altar) is a devastating abomination; < KueOnderzoek 2, ii. 472 Bev, who read עַלכַּֿנּוֺ‎ in its place, instead of it.
