נָמֵר‎ (nâmêr｜naw-mare'｜noun｜masculine｜leopard)
[Heb] נָמֵר‎ ETCBC: נָמֵר‎ (subs｜leopard) OSHL: n.dr.ab TWOT: 1372a GK: H5807 Greek: πάρδαλις
Derivation: from an unused root meaning properly, to filtrate, i.e. be limpid (comp נִמְרָה‎ and נִמְרִים‎); and thus to spot or stain as if by dripping;
Strong's: a leopard (from its stripes)
KJV: leopard.

H5246. namer

נָמֵר‎ noun masculineIsa 11:6 leopard; — absolute ׳נ‎ Hosea 13:7 3t.; plural נְמֵרִים‎ Hab 1:8; Song 4:8; — leopard, as spotted Jer 13:23, swift Hab 1:8, keen-eyed Jer 5:6 (symbol of calamities of Israel), so Hosea 13:7 (simile of ׳י‎); ׳נ יִרְבָּ֑ץ עִםגְּֿדִי‎ Isa 11:6 (predict); נְמֵרִים חַרֲרֵי‎ Song 4:8 (|| אֲרָיוֺת מְעֹנוֺת‎).
