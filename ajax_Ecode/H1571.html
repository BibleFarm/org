גַּם‎768 adverb denoting addition, also, moreover, yea (Moabite id., also Zinjirli (Lzb250 Cooke166); probably akin to √ ; compare (accusative) in a mass, altogether) —

1 also, moreover, emphasizing sometimes the thought of an entire sentence, but more usually the word immediately following, as Gen 3:6 לְאִישָׁהּ גַּם וַתִּתֵּן‎ and gave also to her husband, Genesis 3:27 and take also of the tree of life, 7:3; 19:21; 24:19; 26:21; 29:27; 30:15; 32:21 (֗֗֗ הִגֵּה גַּם‎: so Est 7:9) Gen 48:11; Exod 8:28; 12:32b Deut 1:37; 1Sam 28:20; 2Sam 11:12; 2Kin 9:27; Isa 7:13 +. Often before pronouns, Gen 4:4 הוּא גַם הֵבִיא וְהֶבֶל‎ and Abel, he also brought, 20:5 וְהִיאגַֿםהִֿוא‎ (so only here) and she herself also, 20:6; 27:31 הוּא גַּם וַיַּעַשׂ‎, 30:8; Deut 3:20; Judg 3:31; 6:35; 9:19; 1Sam 19:20-24 Jer 12:6; 48:26 etc. (compare MI6 הא גם ויאמר‎): especially in Genealogies of J (BuUrg. 220) Gen 4:22, 26; 10:21; 19:38; 22:20, 24 compare Judg 8:31. גַּם‎ followed by pronoun also begins a sentence with emphasis in an elevated style, thou (they) also, Isa 14:10; Jer 12:6 (twice in verse); 48:7; Ezek 16:52; Nahum 3:11 (twice in verse). After a pronoun in an oblique case (Ges§ 135, 2) Gen 27:34; 1Sam 19:23; 2Sam 17:5; Jer 25:14; 27:7 +. וְגַם‎ and also (more often than גַּם‎ alone attaching a sentence) Gen 6:4; 14:16; 15:14; 17:16; 20:12; 24:14, 46; 30:6; 37:7; 38:24; 42:28; Exod 2:19; 3:9; 4:14; Josh 7:11 (5 t.) 1Sam 4:17; 1Kin 21:19 + often; with a negative = neither Exod 5:2; 34:3 and elsewhere — גַּם ֗֗֗ גַּם‎ (like et ... et) both ... and Gen 44:16; 47:3, 19; Num 18:3; Jer 51:12; Psa 49:3 +: גַּם ֗֗֗ גַּם ֗֗֗ גַּם‎ Gen 24:25; Judg 8:22; Eccl 9:6: with a negative neither ... nor Num 23:25; 1Sam 20:27; 1Kin 3:26, and (3t.) Gen 43:8; Exod 4:10; 1Sam 28:6. So (but seldom) גַּםוְ֗֗֗גַם‎ Gen 24:44; Exod 10:25f.; 1Sam 2:26; 12:14; 26:25: with negative 21:9. — N.B. In poetry independence and emphasis is sometimes given by גַּם‎ to a new idea, where in English we should be satisfied with and: Psa 107:5; 137:1; Job 24:19; Cant 7:14; Lam 4:15.

2 with stress on a particular word, even, Exod 4:9; Num 22:33 אוֺתְךָ גַּם‎ even thee I had slain, and kept her alive, 2Sam 17:10; Jer 2:33; Psa 132:12; Prov 14:13 even in laughter the heart is sorrowful, 14:20; 17:28; 20:11; Ruth 2:15; Nehemiah 3:35; and so often after כִּי‎, Deut 12:31 for even their sons they burn in the fire to their gods, 1Sam 22:17; Isa 26:12; Jer 6:11; 12:6; 14:5; Hosea 9:12 לָהֶם כִּיגַֿםאֿוֺי‎ for even woe is it to them when I depart from them; הֲגַם‎ Gen 16:13; 1Sam 10:11-12, = 19:24; 1Kin 17:20; Psa 78:20; Job 41:1; Est 7:8. Other cases: — 1Sam 24:12 רְאֵה גַּם רְאֵה‎ see, yea see ! (but HupQu. Job vi. רָאֹה גַּם‎: compare below) Psa 118:11; Job 2:10 נְקַבֵּל אֶתהַֿטּוֺב גַּם‎ shall we receive good (emphatic) from God and not evil ? 21:7; interposed once between a substantive and adjective Gen 20:4 Gentemne etiam justam interficies? emphasizing כֹּל‎, Deut 28:61; Judg 9:49b 1Sam 22:7 לְכֻלְּכֶם גַּםֿ‎ to all (emphatic) of you will he give ...? 2Sam 19:31 יִקָּ֑ח אֶתהַֿכֹּל גַּם‎ yea, the whole let him take! Isa 26:12; אֶחָד‎ 2Sam 17:12b and we will not leave ֗֗֗ אֶחָד גַּם‎ even one (similarly 17:13 b), Psa 14:3 (= 53:4) אֶחָד גַּם אֵין‎; an infinitive or cognate accusative attached to a verb, Gen 31:15; 46:4; Num 11:15; 16:13; 1Sam 1:6. שְׁנֵיהֶם גַּם‎ literally even both i.e. the one as well as the other (German alle beide) is said idiomatically, Gen 27:45 (שְׁנֵיכֶם‎), Deut 22:22; 23:19; 1Sam 25:43 (שְׁתֵּיהֶן‎), Prov 17:15; 20:10, 12; Ruth 1:5.

3 introducing a climax, yea, especially in a rhetorical style, Gen 27:33; Deut 23:3; 23:4; Judg 5:4 (twice in verse); Isa 13:3; 14:8; 43:13 yea, from to-day I am the same, 44:12 רָעֵב גַּם‎ yea, he is hungry, and has no strength, 47:3; 48:8 (3 t. in verse); 57:7; Jer 46:16; 48:2; 51:44; Hosea 7:9; Psa 41:10; 84:7; 139:12; Isa 66:8 אֶתבָּֿנֶיהָ צִיּוֺן גַּםיָָֽֿלְדָה, כִּיחָֿ֫לָה‎, Jer 5:28; 12:2; Ezek 24:5; Job 21:7; Song 8:1: emphasizing an extreme, or aggravated, case, yea, even, Isa 49:15 yea, these may forgot, Jer 8:17 yea, the stork knoweth, etc., Isa 23:12 שָׁם גַּם‎ even there no rest shall be for thee (so Psa 139:10), Isa 49:25; 57:6; Jer 23:11b; Mal 3:15; Psa 84:4.

4 expressing correspondence, especially in the matter of retribution (the גַּם‎ correlativum), so frequently אָנֹכִי גַּם אֲנִי, גַּם‎ I also (on my part): — Gen 20:6 I also [as well as thyself] know that thou hast done this innocently, Josh 24:18; Judg 2:21; 1Sam 1:28 (compare Dr) 28:22 אַתָּה גַּם‎ thou also (as I have done 28:21), 2Sam 12:13 ׳י‎ also [responding to thy confession] hath removed thy sin, 2Kin 2:3, 5; Isa 31:2; 66:3-4 (גם ֗֗֗ גם‎, emphasizing the action of both parties), Jer 2:36; 4:12 now will I also [in correspondence with their deeds] speak judgments with them, 7:11; 51:49 (גם ֗֗֗ גם‎), Ezek 5:8, 11; 16:43 (וְגַם‎ = therefore also), 23:35; Hosea 4:6 because thou hast forgotten the direction of thy God, I also (on my part) will forget thy children, Psa 52:7 (thou lovest evil, etc.) יִתָּצְךָ אֵל גַּם‎ God also (on his part) will pluck thee up, 71:22; 133:1; Prov 1:26; Job 7:11; 12:3; 16:4; so וְגַם‎ Judg 2:3; Amos 4:6-7, Micah 6:13; Mal 2:9. In the apodosis (uncommon): Gen 13:16 if a man could number the dust of the earth, יִמָּנֶה זַרְעֲךָ גַּם‎ thy seed also should be numbered, Jer 31:36-37, 33:21, 26; Zech 8:6. The correspondence is sometimes of the nature of a climax: Gen 27:33; 30:8 I have wrestled, יָכֹלְתִּי גַּם‎ (compare 1Kin 22:22 תּוּכָ֔ל וְגַם‎, Jer 50:24), Isa 66:8.

5 connecting two ideas which express (or imply) a contradiction, גַּם‎ acquires sometimes an adversative force (compare אַף‎ 1 end), yet, but, though: Psa 95:9 they tried me, but also (Che) saw my work (namely of judgment), 129:2; Jer 6:15 = 8:12; Ezek 20:23; Eccl 4:8, 16; 5:18; Neh 6:1. So וְגַם‎ Ezek 16:28; 20:15; Eccl 3:13; 6:7; Neh 5:8.

6 כִּי גַּם‎ (with imperfect) yea, when Lam 3:8, even when Prov 22:6; yea though (stating an imagined case) Isa 1:15; Hosea 8:10; 9:16; Psa 23:4 (so אִם גַּם‎ Eccl 8:17); also (is it) that Ruth 2:21 (see כִּי‎ 1d). גַם כִּי‎ = for even (see above 2); = though even, although (conceding a fact) Eccl 4:14 (see De) 8:12.
