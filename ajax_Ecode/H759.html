אַרְמֹון‎ (ʼarmôwn｜ar-mone'｜noun｜masculine｜citadel)
[Heb] אַרְמֹון‎ ETCBC: אַרְמֹון‎ (subs｜dwelling tower) OSHL: a.fu.ab TWOT: 164a GK: H810 Greek: ἄμφοδον, θεμέλιος, οἶκος, πόλις
Derivation: from an unused root (meaning to be elevated);
Strong's: a citadel (from its height)
KJV: castle, palace. Compare הַרְמֹון‎.

אַרְמוֺן‎ noun masculineIsa 32:14 citadel — ׳א‎ absolute Jer 30:18 +; construct Isa 25:2; plural אַרְמְנוֺת‎ absolute Amos 3:9 (twice in verse); construct 1:4 +; אַרְמְנוֺתֵינוּ‎ Micah 5:4 +, etc. — citadel, castle, palace, not used before royal period, mostly in prophets, especially common in Amos & Jeremiah; citadel as securely barred (in simile) Prov 18:19; ׳א הַמֶּלֶךְ בֵּית‎ i.e. the citadel, stronghold 1Kin 16:18, compare 2Kin 15:25; usually more General, of castle, palaces, prominent buildings; especially used in speaking of conquest, because the fine buildings would be especially object of attack & plunder; palaces of Israel Amos 6:8; of Jerusalem Isa 32:14 (singular collective) Lam 2:5, 7; 2Chr 36:19; Psa 48:4; 48:14; 122:7 compare Hosea 8:14; Micah 5:4; Amos 2:5; Jer 17:27; of Samaria Amos 3:10-11, also Jer 6:5; 9:20 belonging to Benhadad (i.e. Aram) Amos 1:4; Jer 49:27; of Tyre Isa 23:13; Amos 1:10; of Babylon Isa 25:2 (singular collective), compare 13:22 where read אַרְמְנוֺתָיו‎ for אלמנותיו‎ so ᵑ9 ᵑ7 ᵑ6‎ Che Di (|| היכלים‎; compare Ezek 19:7 according to ᵑ7‎ and others, but see rather מעון‎; DlBD xi defends ᵑ0‎ in 19:7 & compare Assyrian almattu, fortress); of Edom Isa 34:13, of Gaza Amos 1:7, of Rabbah 1:14, of Bozrah 1:12, of Kerioth 2:2, of Ashdod 3:9, of Egypt 3:9.

אַרְמֹון‎
原文音譯：!Amr>a; ar-mone’

詞類次數：名詞（32）

原文字根：高；相當於G296（ἄμφοδον） G2310（θεμέλιον / θεμέλιος）

字義溯源：城堡*，堡壘，宮殿，宮，衛所，住所，家宅，堅寨；先知耶利米和阿摩司的書中，此字用得很多，一般都繙作宮殿。這字出自一未經編號的字根，意為：被升高。比較（H2038（הַרְמֹון‎）=哈門，地名，意為城堡）
