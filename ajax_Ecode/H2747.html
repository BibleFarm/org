חֶרֶט‎ (chereṭ｜kheh'-ret｜noun｜masculine｜graving-tool)
[Heb] חֶרֶט‎ ETCBC: חֶרֶט‎ (subs｜stylus) OSHL: h.fq.ab TWOT: 738a GK: H3032
Derivation: from a primitive root meaning to engrave;
Strong's: a chisel or graver; also a style forwriting
KJV: graving tool, pen.

H2747. cheret

חֶ֫רֶט‎ noun [masculine] graving-tool, stylus —

1 graving-tool, with which Aaron fashioned (וַיָּצַר‎) the molten calf Exod 32:4 (E).

2 stylus, for writing on tablet (גִּלָּיוֺן‎): אֱנוֺשׁ בְּחֶרֶט עָלָיו כְּתֹב‎ Isa 8:1 write on it with a man's (i.e. an ordinary) stylus = in common characters, intelligible to all (see BenzArchaeology 290).
