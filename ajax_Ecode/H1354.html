[Heb] גַב‎, גַּב‎ ETCBC: גַּב‎ (subs｜curve) OSHL: c.af.ab TWOT: 303a GK: H1461, H1462 Greek: νῶτος, οἴκημα, ὀφρῦς
Derivation: from an unused root meaning to hollow or curve; (compare גֵּו‎ and גּוּף‎)
Strong's: the back (as rounded); by analogy, the top or rim, a boss, a vault, arch of eye, bulwarks, etc.
KJV: back, body, boss, eminent (higher) place, (eye) brows, nave, ring.

גַּב‎ back, etc., see below גבב‎

[גָּב‎], גָּבִים‎ see גוב‎.

גַּב‎ noun masculine:? compare Ezek 43:13 (feminine1:18) anything convex, curved, gibbous, e.g. back, chiefly late; — absolute גָּ֑ב‎ 16:24; construct גַּב‎ 43:13 (read ᵐ5 גֹּבַהּ‎ Ew Sm Co); suffix גַּבִּי‎ Psa 129:3, גַּבֵךְ‎ Ezek 16:31, 39; plural construct גַּבֹּת‎ Lev 14:9, גַּבֵּי‎ Job 13:12; 15:26; suffix גַּבֵּיהֶם‎ 1Kin 7:33, גַּבֵּהֶם‎ Ezek 10:12, גַּבֵּיהֶן‎ 1:18, גַֹּבֹּתָם‎ 1:18; —

1 back, of man (figurative of Israel) Psa 129:3; apparently of cherubim Ezek 10:12, but Hi Sm emend v. so that ׳גּ‎ refers to wheels, rim see 6 below

2 mound, for illicit worship Ezek 16:24, 31, 39 (all || רמה‎); ᵑ9 ᵐ5‎ lupanar, brothel, after analogy of fornix, but this without sufficient proof, & needless.

3 boss, or convex projection, of shield Job 15:26 (figurative) he runneth against him... with the stout bosses of his shields (i.e. wicked against ׳י‎); so in Arabic in similar phrase; compare also Arabic , shield; also French bouclier from boucle.

4 bulwarks, breastworks, figurative for arguments גַּבֵּיכֶם לְגַבֵּיחֹֿמֶר‎ Job 13:12 breastworks of clay are your breastworks.

5 brow, only עֵינָיו גַּבֹּת‎ Lev 14:9 his eyebrows.

6 rim of wheel, felloe 1Kin 7:33; Ezek 1:18 (twice in verse); so perhaps 10:12 see 1 above — 43:13; elevation, i.e. basement of altar, Da after MT., but see גֹּבַהּ‎, compare above
