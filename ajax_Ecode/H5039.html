H5039. nebalah

נְבָלָה‎ noun feminine senselessness (as shewn in disregard of moral and religious claims), especially of disgraceful sins; also disgrace; — always absolute ׳נ‎; —

1 disgraceful folly, especially of sins of un-chastity Judg 19:23 (wanton deed GFM), so ׳הַנּ הַזּאֹת דְּבַר‎ 19:24, 2Sam 13:12; often נ ׳עָשָׂה בְּיִשְׂרָאֵל‎ i.e. do a thing disgraceful according to Israel's standard: Gen 34:7 (J), Deut 22:21 (compare Dr on meaning of word; only here of woman), Judg 20:26 (+ זִמָּה‎), 20:10; Jer 29:23 (compare בְּיִשְׂרָאֵלּ כֵּן יֵעָשֶׂה לֹא‎ 2Sam 13:12); also of inhospitable churlishness 1Sam 25:25 (see I, II. נָבָל‎, and especially Isa 32:6); of profane action Josh 7:15 (Achan; בְּיִשְׂרָאֵל נ ׳עָשָׂה‎); of senseless and irreligious language Isa 9:16 דֹּבֵר‎ 32:6 (יְדַבֵּר‎).

2 contumely, disgrace נ עִמָּכֶם ׳עֲשׂוֺת‎ Job 42:8 (of ׳י‎), i.e. deal out to you disgrace, expose you and punish you as נְבָלִים‎ (compare 2Sam 3:33, below נָבָל‎), because of your utterances about me.
