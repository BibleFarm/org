עָפָר‎ (ʻâphâr｜aw-fawr'｜noun｜masculine｜dry earth)
[Heb] עָפָר‎ ETCBC: עָפָר‎ (subs｜dust) OSHL: p.eg.ab TWOT: 1664a GK: H6760 Greek: ἄμμος, γῆ, ἔδαφος, κονιορτός, πηλός, Χουζᾶς
Derivation: from עָפַר‎;
Strong's: dust (as powdered or gray); hence, clay, earth, mud
KJV: ashes, dust, earth, ground, morter, powder, rubbish.

H6083. aphar

עָפָר‎ noun masculineLev 14:42 dry earth, dust; — absolute ׳ע‎ Gen 2:7 +, construct עֲפַר‎ 13:16 +; suffix עֲפָרֵךְ‎ Ezek 26:12, עֲפָרוֺ‎ Deut 9:21, etc.; plural construct עַפְרֹת‎ Job 28:6; Prov 8:26; —

1 literally:

a. dry, loose earth, thrown (in malice) 2Sam 16:13 (|| אֲבָנִים‎); ע ׳וַיַעֲלוּ עַלרֿאֹשׁ‎ Josh 7:6 (J E; sign of grief), so Ezek 27:30; Lam 2:10, and (with זָרַק‎) Job 2:12; loose earth (on surface of ground) 1Kin 18:38; Amos 2:7; Micah 1:10; Isa 34:7, 9; Lev 17:13 (H) Ezek 24:7 (in figurative), Num 5:17; Job 14:19; 38:38; 39:14; Gen 26:15 (R), made into siege works Hab 1:10; of sand-storm Deut 28:24 (+ אָבָק‎); as serpent's food, token of punishment and humiliation, Gen 3:14 (J) Isa 65:25 (compare Micah 7:17 2e below); ע ׳זֹחֲלֵי‎ Deut 32:24 (poem; compare 2e).

b. specifically as material of human body Gen 2:7; 3:19 (J), Eccl 3:20, compare Job 4:19; 8:19; Psa 103:14, to which it returns (אֶלֿ שׁוּב‎) Gen 3:19; Job 10:9; Psa 104:29; Eccl 3:20; so (with עַל‎) Job 34:15; Eccl 12:7.

c. = surface of ground Exod 8:12; 8:13 (twice in verse); (P; ׳ע הָאָרַץ‎), Job 19:25; Isa 25:12; 26:5 (both || אֶרֶץ‎), surface of (whole) earth Job 41:25, ע ׳מְחִלוֺת‎ Isa 2:19 (for hiding), so ׳ע‎ alone 2:10, compare ע ׳חֹרֵי‎ Job 30:6; = soil, 5:6; 14:8.

d. powder of anything pulverized Deut 9:21 (twice in verse) (compare דָּ֑ק‎ || Exod 32:20), 2Kin 23:6 (twice in verse); 23:12, 15, so in simile 2Sam 22:43 = Psa 18:43, hence = ashes 18:4; Num 19:17.

e. debris, of ruined city 1Kin 20:10; Ezek 26:4, 12; Nehemiah 3:34; Neh 4:4; Psa 102:15. The following are late:

f. earth of the grave: ׳לֶע אֶשְׁכַּב‎ Job 7:21, so (with עַל‎) 20:11; 21:26, compare 17:16; ע ׳יוֺרְדֵי‎ Psa 22:30, ע ׳שֹׁכְנֵי‎ Isa 26:19, אַדְמַתעֿ ׳יְשֵׁנֵי‎ Dan 12:2; עֲפַרמָֿוֶת‎ Psa 22:16; ׳ע‎ in figure 30:10.

g. mortar (i.e. dried mud) for plastering houses Lev 14:41-42, 45 (P).

h. (iron-) ore Job 28:2; זָהָב עַפְרֹת‎ 28:6 gold-dust (but see סַמִּיר‎).

i. material of earth Isa 40:12, compare תֵּבֵל עַפְרֹת ראֹשׁ‎ Prov 8:26 the first of the earth particles of the world.

2 figurative:

a. of abundance Gen 13:16 (twice in verse); 28:14 (all J) 2Chr 1:9; Job 27:16; Psa 78:27 (|| יַמִּים חוֺל‎), compare יַעֲקֹב עֲפַר‎ Num 23:10 (JE); with added idea of commonness, worthlessness Zeph 1:7; Zech 9:3.

b. of worthlessness (alone) Job 22:24.

c. simile of the scattered, dispersed 2Kin 13:7; ׳כֶּע‎ Isa 41:2 (so van d. H. Ginsb; Baer ׳כֵּע‎; on meaning see Du Che, reading תְּשִׂימֵם‎ for יִתֵּן‎).

d. of self-abasement Gen 18:27 (+ אֵפַר‎), וָאֵפֶר עַלעֿ ׳וְנִחַמְתִּי‎ Job 42:6, בֶּע ׳יִתֵּן מִּיהוּ‎ Lam 3:29.

e. of humiliation (sitting or lying in dust) Isa 47:1; compare 29:4 (twice in verse), Job 16:15; 40:13; Psa 7:6; 44:26; 119:25, simile Job 30:19; licking dust Psa 72:9; Isa 49:23; Micah 7:17, compare Tel Amarna tikalu ipra (of enemies, see WklAltor. Forsch. iii. 291; also of serpent 1a above); of ׳י‎ raising from the dust 1Sam 2:8 = Psa 113:7, 1Kin 16:2; of lifting oneself Isa 52:2.
