עָשַׂר‎ (ʻâsar｜aw-sar'｜verb｜tale the tenth of)
[Heb] עָשַׂר‎ ETCBC: עשׂר‎ (verb｜take tenth) OSHL: p.gb.ad TWOT: 1711c GK: H6923 Greek: δεκατόω
Derivation: a primitive root (identical with עָשַׁר‎); to accumulate; but used only as denominative from עֶשֶׂר‎;
Strong's: to tithe, i.e. to take or give atenth
KJV: [idiom] surely, give (take) the tenth, (have, take) tithe(-ing, -s), [idiom] truly.

H6237. asar

[עָשַׂר‎] verb denominative take the tenth of, tithe; —

Qal Imperfect3masculine singular יַעְשׂר‎ 1Sam 8:15, 17 (with accusative of thing tithed).

Pi`el Imperfect + Infinitive absolute give a tenth of, with accusative of thing: 2 masculine singular תְּעַשֵּׂר עַשֵּׂר‎ Deut 14:22; 1singular לָ֑ח אֲעַשְּׂרֶנּוּ עַשֵּׂר‎ Gen 28:22 (P); Participle taking the tenth, הַמְעַשְּׂרִים‎ Neh 10:38.

Hiph`il (so usually explained) Infinitive לַעְשֵׂר‎ Deut 26:12 with accusative of congnate meaning with verb i.e. taking, reckoning, tithe, but Infinitive construct with ֵ֯‎ improbable, < read לְעַשֵּׂר‎

Pi`el; בַּעְשֵׂר‎ Neh 10:39 absolute, i.e. receiving tithes, but read probably בַּעְשׂר‎
