1 be able, to do a thing, whether ability be physical, moral, constitutional, or dependent on external authority; usually of man Gen 13:16 (J) +, but also of gods 2Chr 32:13, 15, and of ׳י‎ Num 14:16 (JE), Deut 9:28; Jer 44:22; occasionally of inanimate things Amos 7:10; Gen 36:7 (P), Song 8:7; Eccl 1:15 (twice in verse), etc.:

b. followed by infinitive without לְ‎ (27 t.; not P): Gen 37:4; Exod 2:3; 18:18, 23 (all E), Gen 24:50; 44:1 (J), Num 22:37-38, (JE), Deut 1:9; 7:22; 14:24; 22:29; Judg 8:3; Jer 49:10, 23; Hab 1:13; Isa 46:2; 47:11-12, 57:20; Lam 1:14; Psa 18:39; Psalm 36:13; 78:20; Prov 30:21; Job 4:2; 33:5.

c. with infinitive implicit, always negative, Gen 29:8 go and feed them; and they said, We are not able, cannot (J), Exod 8:14 (P), Isa 29:11; Jer 20:9; Psa 21:12; Jonah 1:13.

d. with negative = may not (of moral inability): Gen 43:32 (J) the Egyptians might not eat bread with the Hebrews; Judg 21:18 we may not give them wives; especially D, Deut 7:22; 12:17; 14:24; 16:5; 17:15; 22:3; 28:27, 35.

e. be able, followed by imperfect: נַכֶּה אוּכַל אוּלַי‎ Num 22:6 (JE) perchance I am able (so that) we smite (see Di; read perhaps נוּכַל‎ for אוּכַל‎ Ges§ 120, 1 b); יִגְּעוּ יוּכְלוּ לֹא‎ Lam 4:14 they are unable, they touch = are unable to touch (Dr§ 163, Obs.)

f. be able, followed by perfect consecutive, only very late, וְרָאִיתִי אוּכַל‎ Est 8:6 (twice in verse), literally how shall I be able and see? — Also in various combinations where English idiom would make it an auxiliary to another verb:

g. able to gain, accomplish: followed by acc נִקָּיוֺן יוּכְלוּ לֹא‎ Hosea 8:5 how long will they be unable (to gain) innocence? תּוּכָ֑ל כִּיכֹֿל יָדַעְתִּ֯‎ Job 42:2 I know that thou art able (to do) all things; דָּבָר אֶתְכֶם יוּכַל הַמֶּלֶךְ אֵין‎ Jer 38:5 the king is not (one who is) able (to do) anything with (אֶתְכם‎ for אִתְּכֶם‎ see Gf, i.e. against) you.

h. able to endure: וַעֲצָרָה אָוֶן אוּכַל לֹא‎ Isa 1:13 I cannot endure iniquity and (with) a solemn assembly, Psa 101:5.

i. able to reach, followed by לְ‎, Psa 139:6 it is high, לָהּ אוּכַל לֹא‎ I cannot (reach) to it.

2 prevail:

a. absolute prevail, overcome, be victor Gen 30:8 (E), 32:29 (J), Hosea 12:5; 1Sam 26:25; 1Kin 22:22 2Chr 18:21; Jer 20:7, 11; of waves 5:22; succeed Isa 16:12; Jer 3:5 (VB hast had they way).

b. followed by לְ‎ person, prevail against, over Gen 32:26 (J), Num 13:30 (JE), Judg 16:5; 1Sam 17:9; Jer 1:19; 15:20; 20:10; 38:22; Obadiah 7; Psa 129:2; Est 6:13.

c. once with suffix, יְכָלְתִּיו‎ Psa 13:5 I have prevailed over him, + Zech 9:15 (where read וְיָכְלוּם‎ for וְאָֽכְלוּ‎ with ᵑ7 ᵐ5‎ KloThLZ 1879. 564 StaZAW 1881, 18).

3 absolute have ability, strength, only negative אוּכָ֑ל לֹא מִשְּׂאֵתוֺ‎ Job 31:23 because of his loftiness I have no ability, am inadequate (to anything).
