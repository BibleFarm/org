H5273. na

'iym I. נָעִים‎ adjective pleasant, delightful (poetry); — absolute ׳נ‎ Prov 22:18 +; construct נְעִים‎ 2Sam 23:1 plural נְעִמִים‎ Psa 16:6, נְעִימִם‎ 2Sam 1:23, נְעִימִים‎ Prov 23:8; feminine נְעִמוֺת‎ Psa 16:11; —

1 delightful וְהַנּ ׳הַנֶּאֱהָבִים בְּחַיֵּיהֶם‎ 2Sam 1:23 (of Saul and Jonath.); of wealth, וְנ יָקָר ׳הוֺן‎ Prov 24:4; words 23:8; of acts, etc.: גַּםיָֿחַ֑ד אַחִים שֶׁבֶת וּמַהנּֿ ׳מַהטּֿוֺב‎ Psa 133:1; singing praises to ׳י‎, 135:3; 147:1; keeping wise teachings Prov 22:18; as substantive = delight בַּנְּעִימִים וּשְׁנֵיהֶם בַּטּוֺב יְמֵיהֶם יְכַלּוּ‎ Job 36:11 (of earthly prosperity); of spiritual delights Psa 16:6; 16:11.

2 lovely, beautiful (physically), Song 1:16 (|| יָפֶה‎). — Psa 81:3; 2Sam 23:1 see II. נָעִים‎ below II. נעם‎.

II. נָעִים‎ adjective perhaps singing, sweetly sounding, musical; — absolute נ ׳כִּנּוֺר‎ Psa 81:3 sweetly-sounding lyre; construct, as substantive, יִשׂ זְמִרוֺת ׳נְעִים‎ 2Sam 23:1 Israel's sweet singer of songs (compare Dr8:10 Löhr; ᵑ9‎ egregius psaltes Israel, and so Ges Ew and others, from I. נעם‎; joy of the songs of Israel HPS, compare Klo Kit).

נעץ‎ (√ of following; Late Hebrew נָעַץ‎ prick, stick or thrust in, wedge in; נְעַץ ᵑ7‎ id.; Late Hebrew נַעֲצוּץ‎ figurative of wicked person; נַעֲצוּצָא ᵑ7‎ = Biblical Hebrew).
