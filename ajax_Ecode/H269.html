אָחוֺת‎114 noun feminine sister (Phoenician אחת‎, Aramaic אֲחָת‎ , Arabic , Assyrian a—âtu, Dlw, Ethiopic Sabean in proper name אחֿתאמהו‎ OsZMG 1865, 273) — absolute ׳אָ‎ 2Sam 13:1 + (never with article); construct אֲחוֺת‎ Gen 4:22 +; suffix אֲחֹתִי‎ (אֲחוֺתִי‎) 12:13 +, etc.; plural suffix אַחְוֺתַי‎ (Qr אַחְיוֺתַי‎) Josh 2:13; אֲחוֺתַיִח‎ Ezek 16:51 (Qr; Kt אחותך‎) + 2 t. (Co all ׳אחיו‎); אַחֲיוֺתֵח‎ 16:52 (Co תֿיך‎); אֲחוֺתֵח‎ 16:52 (must be plural, but strike out Co); אַחְיֹתָיו‎ Job 42:11; אֲחוֺתֵיכֶם‎ Hosea 2:3; אַחְיֹתֵיהֶם‎ 1Chr 2:16; Job 1:4 —

1 sister Gen 4:22; 12:13, 19; 20:2, 5, 12 (same father, different mother) so Lev 18:11 compare Ezek 22:11; Gen 24:30 (twice in verse) +, Exod 2:4, 7; Lev 18:9 (either parent same) so Deut 27:22; Num 6:7; 2Sam 13:1-2, 4 +, Song 8:8 (twice in verse); called upon, in mourning for dead Jer 22:18; = near relative Gen 24:59-60, (or because Laban prominent ? so Di); /man of same nationality Num 25:18 compare Hosea 2:3.

2 = beloved Song 4:9-10, 12; 5:12 (4 t. || כַּלָּה‎ bride; phrase originally implying that marriage with half-sister — of same father — was allowed? compare NöZMG 1886, 150 & Gen 20:12).

3 symbolic of Judah, Samaria, Sodom & Jerusalem Jer 3:7-8, 10; Ezek 16:45, 52 (twice in verse) (strike out Co) +, 23:4, 11 +.

4 figurative of intimate connection לָרִמָּה וַאֲחֹתִי אִמִּי ֗֗֗ קָרָאתִי‎ Job 17:14; אַתְּ אֲחֹתִי לַחָכְמָה אֱמֹר‎ Prov 7:4.

5. another, אֶלאֲֿחֹתָהּ אִשָּׁה‎, of curtains of tabernacle Exod 26:3 (twice in verse); 26:6; loops 26:5, tenons 26:17; of wings of living creatures, Ezekiel's vision Ezek 1:9, 23; 3:13; not of persons, but see רְעוּת‎.

אָחֹות‎
原文音譯：tAxa' aw-khoth’

詞類次數：名詞（119）

原文字根：弟兄；相當於G79（ἀδελφή）

字義溯源：姐妹，妹子，妹妹，姐姐，姐，妹；為（H251（אָח‎）*=弟兄）的陰性，有廣範的使用，如：彼此，共同；又如：猶大與以色列被稱為姐妹（耶3:6,7）；耶路撒冷和撒瑪利亞也被稱為姐妹（結16:2,46）在隱喻上，智慧與聰明是姐妹（箴7:4）；約伯對蟲說，你是我的姐妹（伯17:14）；所羅門在雅歌中，稱愛情為妹子（歌4:9,10）在製造會幕的幔子時，姐妹這字繙作：相連，相對（出26:6,17）
