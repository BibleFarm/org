פֹּה‎ (pôh｜po｜adverb｜here)
[Heb] פֹּה‎ ETCBC: פֹּה‎ (advb｜here) OSHL: q.am.aa TWOT: 1739 GK: H7024 Greek: αὐτοῦ, ἐντεῦθεν, ἐντεῦθεν, ἐντεῦθεν, ὧδε
Derivation: or פֹּא‎; (Job 38:11), or פֹּו‎; probably from a primitive inseparable particle 'p' (of demonstrative force) and הוּא‎;

KJV: here, hither, the one (other, this, that) side.

H6311. poh

or po or po מֹּה‎58, and (Ezekiel 40-41, 23 out of 35 t.) מּוֺ‎, also Job 38:11b מֹּא‎,

adverb of location here, hither (probably from the same demonstrative √ found in so, then (often in apodosis), אֵפוֺא אַף,‎: compare Köii. l, 247 f., 243, 331 f.): —

1 here:

a. Gen 19:12 מֹּה מִילְֿךָ עֹד‎ whom hast thou still here ? 22:5 מֹּה לָכֶם שְׁבוּ‎, 40:15; Num 22:8; 32:16; Deut 5:28; 12:8; Judg 4:20 אִישׁ מֹּה הֲיֵשׁ‎, 2Kin 3:11, etc.; asked in some surprise, Judg 18:3 פֹה מַהלְּֿךָ‎ = what is thy business here ? simil. 1Kin 19:9, 13; Isa 22:16; 52:5 מַהלִּֿיפֹֿה וְעַתָּה‎. compare the synonym כֹּה בָּזֶה,‎

2. b. עַדמֹּֿה‎ Job 38:11 a.

c. מִמֹּה‎ or מִמּוֺ‎, only Ezekiel 40-41, and always (except Ezek 40:12 a, where it must be supplied with ᵑ6 ᵐ5‎) repeated, on this side ... on that side (compare מִזֶּה ֗֗֗ מִזֶּה‎), 40:10 (twice in verse); 40:12b; 40:21 (twice in verse), etc.

d. with the interrogative אֵיפֹה אֵי,‎ where ?.

2 after a verb of motion, hither, 1Sam 16:11 פֹה עַדבּֿאֹוֺ‎, Ezra 4:2.
