בָּרִיחַ‎ (bârîyach｜baw-ree'-akh｜adjective｜fleeing)
[Heb] בָּרִיחַ‎, בָּרִחַ‎ ETCBC: בָּרִחַ‎ (adjv｜fleeing) OSHL: b.dc.ab TWOT: 284a GK: H1371
Derivation: or בָּרִחַ‎; (shortened) from בָּרַח‎;
Strong's: a fugitive, i.e. the serpent (as fleeing), and the constellation by that name
KJV: crooked, noble, piercing.

H1281. bariach

I. [בָּרִיחַ‎] adjective fleeing ( = ֗֗֗ בַּרִּיחַ‎) בִָּרחַ׃ נָחָשׁ‎ Job 26:13 of eclipse-dragon, בָרִחַ נָחָשׁ לִוְיָתָן‎ Isa 27:1 (probably figurative of Assyrians); as substantive בָּרִיחִים‎ 43:14 as fugitives (for other views compare Comm.); so probably also 15:5 בריחיה‎, see בְּרִיחַ‎.
