[Heb] נַעֲרָה‎ ETCBC: נַעֲרָה‎ (subs｜girl) OSHL: n.ei.ad TWOT: 1389c GK: H5855 Greek: γυνή, δούλη, δοῦλον, δοῦλος, θεράπων, κοράσιον, παιδίσκη, παῖς, παρθένος

H5291. naarah

II. נַעֲרָה‎ noun feminine girl, damsel; — absolute ׳נ‎ Judg 19:3 22t., also Qr Gen 24:14 20t. Genesis, Deuteronomy (נַעֲרָה‎ Kt in Pentateuch only Deut 22:19, see below); plural absolute נְעָרוֺת‎ 1Sam 9:11 +, construct נַעֲרוֺת‎ Est 4:4; suffix נַעֲרוֺתָיו‎ Ruth 2:22; 3:2, נַעֲרֹתֶיהָ‎ Gen 24:61; Exod 2:5, etc.; —

1 girl, damsel; ׳נ קְטַנָּה‎ little girl only 2Kin 5:2, compare 5:4, perhaps = young daughters Job 40:29; elsewhere = young woman 1Sam 9:11; especially as marriageable, Gen 24:14, 16, 28, 55, 57; 34:3, 12 (all J), Deut 22:15 (twice in verse); 22:16, 19, 20, 21, 24, 26 (twice in verse); 22:29; 1Kin 1:3-4, Est 2:4, 7, 8, 9, 12 (twice in verse); 2:13; specifically virgin ׳נ בְּתוּלָה‎ Judg 21:12; Deut 22:23, 28; 1Kin 1:2; Est 2:2-3,(compare Deut 22:15b; 22:20); ׳נ מְאֹרָשָׂה‎ betrothed girl 22:25, 27; young widow Ruth 2:6; 4:12; of a concubine Judg 19:3-4, 5, 6, 8, 9, a prostitute Amos 2:7.

2 of female attendants, maids (always plural), Gen 24:61 (J), Exod 2:5 (E), 1Sam 25:42; Prov 9:3; 27:27; 31:15; Est 2:9 (twice in verse); 4:4, 16; gleaners Ruth 2:5, 8, 22, 23; 3:2. —

Note. נער‎ Kt in Pentateuch as feminine (Qr נַעֲרָ֯‎) is probably not original; it is not found in Sam. Pentateuch, and feminine plural נַעֲרֹתֶיהָ‎ occurs Genesis, Exodus; compare Ges§§ 2n, 17c.
