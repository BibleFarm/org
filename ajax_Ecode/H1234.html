בָּקַע‎ (bâqaʻ｜baw-kah'｜verb｜cleave)
[Heb] בָּקַע‎ ETCBC: בקע‎ (verb｜split) OSHL: b.cr.aa TWOT: 271 GK: H1324 Greek: ἀνοίγω, ἀποστρέφω, διασπάω, εὑρίσκω, καταδυναστεύω, ῥήγνυμι, σχίζω, τήκω
Derivation: a primitive root;
Strong's: to cleave; generally, to rend, break, rip or open
KJV: make a breach, break forth (into, out, in pieces, through, up), be ready to burst, cleave (asunder), cut out, divide, hatch, rend (asunder), rip up, tear, win.

H1234. baqa

בָּקַע‎ verb cleave, break open or through (Late Hebrew id., MI15 השחרת מבקע‎ from break of dawn; Aramaic בְּקַע‎; compare Ethiopic profit, be useful, originally findere, aperire, Di) —

Qal Perfect בָּקַע‎ Psa 78:13, בָּֽקְעָה‎ Isa 34:15, בָּקַעְתָּ‎ Neh 9:11; Psa 74:15, וּבָֽקַעְתָּ֫‎ Ezek 29:7; Imperfect וַיִּבְקַע‎ Judg 15:19; Isa 48:21, וַיִּבְקְעוּ‎ 2Sam 23:16 = 1Chr 11:18, בְקָעוּהָ וַיִּ‎2Chr 21:17; Imperative וּבְקָעֵהוּ‎ Exod 14:16; Infinitive construct suffix בִּקְעָם‎ Amos 1:13; 2Chr 32:1; Participle active בּוֺקֵעַ‎ Isa 63:12; Eccl 10:9, בֹּקֵעַ‎ Psa 141:7; —

1 cleave, cleave open, followed by accusative, Judg 15:19 God cleft open the hollow (הַמֵּכְתֵּשׁ‎), and water came out, compare Isa 48:21 (object צוּר‎), also Psa 74:15 bring forth by cleaving, object יָנָחַ֑ל מַעְיָן‎, all three of divine operation; cleave or rip open pregnant women Amos 1:13; of a broken staff, tearing the shoulder Ezek 29:7 (but read כַּף‎ hand for ᵑ9 ᵐ5 כָּתֵף,‎ Sm (?)Co.); cleave wood Eccl 10:9 (|| אֲבָנִים מַסִּיעַ‎); of ploughing (furrowing) the earth Psa 141:7 (|| מֹּלֵחֵ‎; in simile); especially of dividing the sea, Exod 14:16 (P) Neh 9:11; Psa 78:13, compare Isa 63:12, object מַיִם‎; — in all these subject ׳י‎ except Exod 14:16 where he commands Moses; of hatching out (a brood, but no object expressed) Isa 34:15, subject קִמּוֺו‎ arrow-snake.

2 break through or into, followed by בְּ‎ 2Sam 23:16 = 1Chr 11:18; followed by accusative 2Chr 21:17 וֵיִבְקָעוּהָ בִיהוּדָה וַיַעֲלוּ‎; also 32:1, object suffix reference to cities, אֵלָיו לְבִקְעָם ויאמר‎ and he thought to break into them and so bring them unto himself.

Niph`al Perfect נִבְקַע‎ Job 26:8; Zech 14:4 (וְ‎ consecutive); נִבְקְעוּ‎ Gen 7:11; Isa 35:6, נִבְקָ֑עוּ‎2Chr 25:11; Prov 3:20; Imperfect יִבָּקַע‎ Isa 58:8, יִבֵָּק֑עַ‎ Job 32:19; 3feminine singular תִּבָּקַע‎ Isa 59:5, וַתִּבָּקַע‎ Num 16:31 3t.; Infinitive construct לְהִבָּקֵעַ‎ Ezek 30:16; —

1 be cleft, rent open, subject the ground, הָֽאֲדָמָה‎ Num 16:31 (J), הָאָרֶץ‎ 1Kin 1:40 (hyperb.); mountain Zech 14:4; burst open, of men hurled from rock 2Chr 25:12; of cloud beneath its weight of water Job 26:8; hyperb. of belly full of words seeking a vent, 32:19 like new wine-skins it will burst open; so of the water-receptacles (מעינות‎) of the great deep, at the flood Gen 7:11; of the water-masses themselves, תהומות‎ Prov 3:20, ונהלים מים‎ Isa 35:6; also of the Red Sea, המים וַיִבָּקְעוּ‎ Exod 14:21; of light breaking forth Isa 58:8 (figurative); of serpent's egg hatching out as a viper 59:5 אֶפְעֶה תִּבָּקַע הַזּוּרֶה‎.

2 be broken into, of city captured by breaches in walls 2Kin 25:4 = Jer 52:7; Ezek 30:16.

Pi`el Perfect בִּקֵּעַ‎ 2Kin 15:16; Job 28:10, וּבִקַּעְתִּ֫י‎ Ezek 13:13, בִּקֵּ֑עוּ‎ Isa 59:5; Imperfect יְבַקַּע‎ Psa 78:15, וַיְבַקַּע‎ Gen 22:3; 3feminine singular תְּבַקֵּעַ‎ Ezek 13:11, תְּבַקְּעֵם‎ Hosea 13:8; 2masculine singular תְּבַקַּעֿ‎ Hab 3:9, תְּבַקֵּ֑עַ‎ 2Kin 8:12; 2feminine plural וַתְּבַקַּעְנָה‎ 2:24; — cleave, cut to pieces, or rend open (often more complete or more violent than Qal), followed by accusative, of cleaving wood Gen 22:3 i.e. cut it up for burning, so 1Sam 6:14; of ripping open pregnant women 2Kin 8:12; 15:16; of tearing in pieces children 2:24; compare also Hosea 13:8 (figurative); of cleaving open rocks, to bring forth water Psa 78:15 (subject God); of cutting mining-shafts Job 28:10 בּ יְאֹרִים ׳בַּצּוּרוֺת‎; תְּבַקַּעאָֿ֑רֶץ נְהָרוֺת‎ Hab 3:9 into rivers thou cleavest (the) earth; break through or down (a wall, but no object expressed), Ezek 13:11 תְּבֵַקּ֑עַ סְעָרוֺת רוּחַ‎ (but Co תִּבָּקֵעַ‎), compare 13:13 בַחֲמָתִי סְעָרוֺת רוּחַ וּבִקַּעְתִּי‎; of hatching eggs Isa 59:5 צִפְעוֺנִיבִֵּקּ֑עוּ בֵּיצֵי‎ (figurative)

Pu`al Imperfect יְבָֻקּ֑עוּ‎ Hosea 14:1; Participle מְבֻקָּעָה‎ Ezek 26:10, מְבֻקָּעִים‎ Josh 9:4; — be ripped open, of women Hosea 14:1 (verb of masculine form); rent, of old wine-skins Josh 9:4; broken into, of a city in whose walls a breach has been made Ezek 26:10.

Hiph`il Imperfect1plural suffix וְנַבְקִעֶנָּה‎ Isa 7:6; Infinitive construct לְהַבְקִיעַ‎ 2Kin 3:26; — break into, followed by suffix reference to Judah, Isa 7:6 ׳ונב אֵלֵינוּ‎ let us break into it, lay it open, and so bring it unto ourselves (compare Qal 2Chr 32:1); break through (absolute) with sword, אֶלמֶֿלֶךְאֱדוֺם לְהַבְקִיעַ חֶרֶבּ שֹׁלֵף‎ 2Kin 3:26.

Hoph`al Perfect3feminine singular הָעִיר הָבְקְעָה‎ Jer 39:2 the city was broken into, entrance was made by a breach.

Hithpa`el Perfect הִתְכַָּקּ֑עוּ‎ Josh 9:13, Imperfect יִתְבּקָּ֑עוּ‎ Micah 1:4; — burst (themselves) open, of wine-skins Josh 9:13; cleave asunder, of valleys Micah 1:4.
