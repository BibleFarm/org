מְדִינָה‎ noun feminine province (an Aramaic word, compare Syriac , Arabic city) **compare also Old Aramaic מדינתא‎ city, Palmyrene מדיתא‎ city, suffix מדיתהון‎, plural מדינתא‎ SAC70 Lzb306 Cooke266. — Est 1:1 28t.; plural מְדִינוֺת‎ 1Kin 20:14 23t.; — a district of an empire,

1 districts of realm of Ahab with שׂרים‎ over them 1Kin 20:14-15, 17, 19.

2 of the Babylonian empire: Ezek 19:8; Dan 8:2 (of Elam); בַּמְּדִינוֺת שָׂרָתִי‎ princess among the provinces Lam 1:1 (of Judea).

3 of the Persian empire Est 1:1 + often; one of which was Palestine, Neh 1:3; the returning exiles, הַמְּדִינָה בְּנֵי‎ children of the province Ezra 2:1; Neh 7:6 having הַמְּדִינָה רָאשֵׁי‎ 11:3.

4 provinces in General Eccl 2:8; 5:7; מְדִינָה בְּמִשְׁמַנֵּי‎ in the fat places of the province Dan 11:24 (fertile regions, probably Egypt; other translations see in Bev.)
