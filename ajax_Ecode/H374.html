אֵיפָה‎35, אֵפָה‎

noun feminine ephah (etymology dubious, ᵐ5‎. οιφι etc., compare Coptic ôipi, Thes LagOr. ii. 2 & cit.) — ׳א‎ Num 5:15 +; אֵפָה‎ Exod 16:36 +; construct אֵיפַת‎ Lev 19:36 +; — ephah, a grain-measure.

1 a certain quantity of wheat, barley, etc. = ten omers (עֹמֶר‎) Exod 16:36 (compare in measure of offerings Lev 5:11; 6:13; Num 5:15; 28:5, all הָא ׳עֲשִׂירִית‎; = 1/10 chomer (חֹמֶר‎) Ezek 45:11 (= bath, בַּת‎, liquid measure q. v.) compare Isa 5:10; chiefly of offerings, see above & 1Sam 1:24; Ezek 45:13 (twice in verse); 45:24 (3 t. in verse); 46:5 (twice in verse); 46:7 (3 t. in verse); 46:11 (3 t. in verse); 46:14; compare Judg 6:19, but also of food 1Sam 17:17 compare Ruth 2:17 & Isa 5:10 above

2 receptacle or measure, holding an ephah, in prophetic vision Zech 5:6-7, 8, 9, 10; just measure אֵיפַתצֶֿדֶק‎ Lev 19:36 (|| צ ׳הִֶין ׳אַבְנֵיצֿ, ׳מאֹזְנֵיצֿ,‎) compare Ezek 45:10-11, וָצ ׳שׁלֵמָה אֵיפָה‎ Deut 25:15 (|| שׁ ׳אֶבֶן ׳וָצ‎); of unjust measure וְאֵיפָה אֵיפָה‎ 25:14; Prov 20:10; א ׳הַקְטִין‎ Amos 8:5; רָווֺן אֵיפַת‎ Micah 6:10. (On the actual size of ephah, compare בַּת‎).
