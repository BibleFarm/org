מַדּוּעַ‎ (maddûwaʻ｜mad-doo'-ah｜adverb｜wherefore?)
[Heb] מַדּוּעַ‎ ETCBC: מַדּוּעַ‎ (inrg｜why) OSHL: j.au.an TWOT: 848h GK: H4508 Greek: τίς
Derivation: or מַדֻּעַ‎; from מָה‎ and the passive participle of יָדַע‎;
Strong's: what (is) known?; i.e. (by implication) (adverbially) why?
KJV: how, wherefore, why.

H4069. maddua

מַדּוּעַ‎61 and :(Exod 18:19) מַדֻּעַ‎

adverb wherefore? (probably contracted from מַהיָּֿדוּעַ‎ what being known ? i.e. from what motive? so Ges Ew§325 c Ol§223 f, compare in Greek τί μαθών;) — wherefore? on what account ? Gen 26:27 אֵלָ֑י בָּאתֶם מַדּוּעַ‎, 40:7; Exod 1:18; 2:18; 3:8 (in an indirect question), 5:14; 18:14; Lev 10:17; Josh 17:14 (all in Hexateuch), Judg 5:28 (twice in verse); 2Sam 3:7; 11:10 etc., Isa 5:4; 50:2; 63:2 (all in Isaiah); in Jeremiah 16 t., oft, rhetorically, after a double question introduced by הֲאִ֗֗֗ם‎, expressing affected surprise: Jer 2:14 (see Gf) is Israel a slave (unable to defend himself)? wherefore, then, is he become a prey? (some other cause must therefore be found for Israel's misfortune), 2:31; 8:5, 19, 22; 14:19; 22:28; 49:1, compare 30:6; Job 3:12; 18:8 (never in Psalms).
