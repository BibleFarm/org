יֶקֶב‎ (yeqeb｜yeh'-keb｜noun｜masculine｜wine-vat)
[Heb] יֶקֶב‎ ETCBC: יֶקֶב‎ (subs｜pit) OSHL: j.cx.ab TWOT: 900a GK: H3676 Greek: ληνός, οἶνος, ὑπολήνιον
Derivation: from an unused root meaning to excavate;
Strong's: a trough (as dug out); specifically, a wine-vat (whether the lower one, into which the juice drains; or the upper, in which the grapes are crushed)
KJV: fats, presses, press-fat, wine(-press).

H3342. yeqeb

יֶ֫קֶב‎ noun masculineProv 3:10 wine-vat (a trough or hollow excavated (חצב‎ Isa 5:2) in the rock for receiving the juice trodden out in the גַּת‎: Benz212f.), sometimes also wine-press (the trough in which the grapes were trodden out); — absolute יֶ֫קֶב‎ 5:2 4t.; יָ֑קֶב‎ Num 18:30; construct י֫קֶב ֶ‎ Judg 7:25; suffix יִקְבֶ֑ךָ‎ Deut 15:14; 16:13; plural יְקָבִּים‎ Job 24:11 4t.; יִקְבֵּי‎ Zech 14:10; יְקָבֶיךָ‎ Prov 3:10; — wine- vat, Isa 5:2; Hag 2:16; Joel 4:13 (|| גַּת‎), Prov 3:10; often || גֹּרֶן‎, Num 18:27, 30; Deut 15:14; 16:13; 2Kin 6:27; Hosea 9:2; Joel 2:24; apparently of the wine-press Job 24:11 דָּֽרְכוּ יְקָבִים‎, Isa 16:10n (hence Jer 48:33). Designating particular localities, Judg 7:25 זְאֵב יֶקֶב‎, Zech 14:10 הַמֶּלֶךְ יִקְבֵי‎ (near Jerusalem).
