חָרוּל‎ (chârûwl｜khaw-rool'｜noun｜masculine｜chickpea)
[Heb] חָרוּל‎ ETCBC: חָרוּל‎ (subs｜chickling) OSHL: h.fu.ab TWOT: 743a GK: H3017 Greek: ἄγριος, φρύγανον
Derivation: or (shortened) חָרֻל‎; apparently, a passive participle of an unused root probably meaning to be prickly;
Strong's: properly, pointed, i.e. a bramble or other thorny weed
KJV: nettle.

H2738. charul

חָרוּל‎ noun [masculine] a kind of weed, perhaps chickpea (cicercula), see Löwp. 153 (Syriac fodder for horses; ᵑ7‎ Prov 24:31 gives חוּרְלָא‎) — absolute ׳ח‎ Job 30:6; Zeph 2:9; plural חֲרֻלִים‎ Prov 24:31; — as growing in devastated land, collective Zeph 2:9; in vineyard of slothful, plural ח פניו ׳כִּסּוּ‎ Prov 24:31 (|| קִמְּשׂגִים‎); as sole shelter of certain outcast peoples Job 30:7 (שִׂיחִים‎).
