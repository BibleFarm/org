ב‎

בּ ב,‎, Bêth, 2nd letter; post Biblical Hebrew = numeral 2 (and so in margin of printed MT); ב֟‎ = 2000; no evidence of this usage in OT times.

I. בְּ‎,

preposition in (Moabite בּ‎, Syriac , Arabic , Ethiopic ) before tone-syllables in certain cases (Ges§ 102. 2) בָּ‎, with suffix בִּי‎; בְּךָ‎ (Ex 7:29; 2Sam 22:30; Psa 141:8 בְּכָה‎), in pause and feminine בָּךְ‎; בּוֺ‎ (once, Jer 17:24 Kt בֹּה‎), בָּהּ‎; בָּנוּ‎; בָּכֶם‎, [בָּכֶן‎]; בָּם בָּהֶם,‎ [also בָּהֵ֫מָּה‎ Exod 30:4; 36:1; Hab 1:16], בָּהֶן‎ 1Sam 31:7; Isa 38:16; Ezek 42:14 [15 times בָּהֵן‎ (FrMM 235), and thrice, Leviticus 5:22; Num 13:19; Jer 5:17, בָּהֵ֫נָּה‎]. Preposition denoting properly in, Greek ἐν, but applied in many derived and figurative significations. The senses expressed by בְּ‎ are grouped by the Rabbis in three classes, הַכְּלִי בֵּית‎ Beth vasculi, וְהַנְּגִיעָה הַדִּיבִּוּק בֵּית‎ Beth coniunctionis et viciniae, הָעֵזֶר בֵּית‎ Beth auxilii; and the same arrangement may be followed here, though the limits between the three classes are not clearly defined, and they sometimes overlap one another.

I. In:

1 strictly, of position in a place (which often is expressed more precisely by בְּתוֺךְ בְּקֶרֶב,‎), as בַּבַּיִת‎ in the house, בָּעִיר‎ in the city, בַּדּוּד‎ in the pot, בָּאָרֶץ‎ in the land, etc. constantly. Hebrew idiom also says בָּהָר‎ in the mountain Exod 24:18 etc., even in cases where we could hardly avoid saying on, as 1Kin 11:7; 19:11: so בְּחֹרֵב‎ Deut 1:16 and elsewhere; הָהָר בְּראֹשׁ‎ Exod 24:17; Psa 72:16. Preceded by a verb of motion (especially עָבַר‎) בְּ‎ = through, as Gen 12:6 and Abram passed through בָּאָרֶץ‎ in the land = passed through it, 13:17; 2Sam 24:2 +; in (= through) a gate, Isa 62:10; Jer 17:19; Micah 2:13. Figurative to speak בְּאָזְנֵי‎ in the ears of ...; to be good (or evil, etc.) בְּעֵינֵי‎ in the eyes of ...

2 of presence in the midst of a multitude, among, Exod 14:28 there was not left בָּהֶם‎ among them even one, Lev 26:36; 2Sam 15:31 Ahitophel בַּקֹּשְׁרִים‎ is among the conspirators, 2Kin 18:5 יְהוּדָה מַלְכֵי בְּכֹל‎. So בְּךָ‎ in thee (of Israel, collective) Deut 7:14; 15:4, 7; 18:10; 23:11; 28:54 (different from עִמָּ֑ח‎ beside thee Lev 25:35). — Specifically

a. of an individual, implying eminency among: Josh 14:15; Jer 46:18 Tabor among the mountains, 49:15; Prov 30:30; Song 1:8 בַנָּשִׁים הַיָּפָה‎ the fair one (= the fairest) among women, Lam 1:1: compare Luke 1:42. On 1Sam 17:12 see Dr.

b. hence with some verbs, when the action refers to only a part of the object, as בְּ הִכָּהַ‎ to smite among... i.e. to smite some of... (different from הִכָּה‎ with accusative); בְּ הָרַג‎ Psa 78:31; בְּ נָשָׂא‎ to bear in, i.e. to share in bearing, Num 11:17 and elsewhere; בְּ בָּנָה‎ to build in or at Zech 6:15; Neh 4:4; בְּ עָשָׂה‎ Exod 5:9; בְּ עָמֵל‎ to labour on Jonah 4:10; בְּ שָׁתָה בְּ, אָכַל‎ to eat or drink of Judg 13:16; Prov 9:5; בְּ חָלַק‎ to give a share in Job 39:17.

c. specifying the parts of which a whole consists (especially in P) Gen 7:21; 8:17; 9:10, 16; 17:23; Exod 12:19; Num 31:11, 26; Hosea 4:3.

3 with reference to the limits enclosing a space, within: Exod 20:10 בִּשְׁעָרֶיךָ‎ within thy gates, Isa 56:5 בְּחוֺמוֺתַי‎ within my walls.

4 often pregn. with verbs of motion, when the movement to a place results in rest in it, into: after בָּא‎ Gen 19:8; Isa 19:23; נָתַן‎ Gen 27:17; שָׁלַח‎ to send Lev 16:22; in among Josh 23:7, 12; 1Kin 11:2. — Hosea 12:7 (an extreme case) בְּ שׁוּב‎ to return (and rest) in thy God, 1Sam 16:3 (unless לַזָּבַ֑ח‎ should be read, as 16:7). — בְּעַיִן עַיִן‎ (with) eye (looking) into eye Num 14:14; Isa 52:8; בְּפָנִים מָּנִים‎ Deut 5:4; פְ ׳עֵינֵי בְּ‎ the eyes of ... are upon, both in favourable (11:12; Psa 101:6) and hostile (Amos 9:8; Job 7:8) sense.

5 applied to time, as Gen 1:1 בְּרֵאשִׁית‎ in the beginning; 2:2 הַשְּׁבִיעִי בַּיּוֺם‎ on the seventh day; Judg 10:8 הַהִיא בַּשָּׁנָה‎ in that year; & constantly.

6 of a state or condition, whether material or mental, in which an action takes place: so often, בְּשָׁלוֺם‎ in peace Gen 15:15; בַּצָּרָה‎ in distress Psa 91:15; בְּתֻמּוֺ הָלַךְ‎ to walk in his integrity; Exod 5:19 בְּרָע‎ in evil case.

7 בְּ‎ introduces the predicate, denoting it as that in which the subject consists, or in which it shews itself (the Beth essentiae, — common in Arabic, especially with a participle or adjective and in a negative sentence: Qor 2:79 and God (appears) not as one remiss; 2:7 and they are not believers [compare French en — en honnête homme]; see WAG ii. § 56 a): namely

a. a primary predicate, Exod 18:4 the God of my fathers בְּעֶזְרִי‎ was my help, Psa 146:5; Hosea 13:9 (read בְעֶזְֶרךָ׃ מִי כִּי‎ with ᵑ6 ᵐ5‎ Che and others); Psa 68:5 שְׁמוֺ בְּיָהּ‎ his name consists in Yah, Job 23:13; 37:10. With the predicate in the plural (as plural of majesty) Psa 118:7 ׳י בְּעֹזְרָ֑י‎ J. is my great helper, 54:6 (see Che), Judg 11:35.

b. a secondary predicate, Exod 6:3 and I appeared unto them שַׁדָּ֑י בְּאֵל‎ as God Almighty, Num 26:53 בְּנַחֲלָה‎ as an inheritance, 34:2; Ezek 46:16; 47:14; Deut 10:22; 26:14 I have not put away therefrom בְּטָמֵא‎ as one unclean = while unclean, 28:62; Isa 40:10 יָבוֺא בְּחָזָק‎ he cometh as a strong one, Psa 35:2 (where see De) and rise up as my help, 35:16, 39:7; 55:19; Prov 3:26; Job 36:32 (De).

c. a predicate as accusative, Isa 48:10 I have refined thee בְכָ֑סֶף וְלֹא‎ but not as though silver, Ezek 20:41; Num 18:26; 36:2; Josh 13:6-7, 23:4; Ezek 45:1; 47:22 (twice in verse) (read יַמִּלוּ‎) 48:29 (see ᵐ5‎ Co) all בְּנַחֲלָה‎ (compare

b.), Psa 78:55 & allotted it נַחֲלָה בְּחֶבֶל‎ as a line of inheritance (i.e. as a measured inheritance). Isa 26:4 י עוֺלָמִים צוּר יהוה בְּיָהּ ׳כִּי‎ is different — for in Yah there is a rock of ages (compare Qor 4:7; 4:47 there is sufficiency in God as a patron).

d. in comparisons, Psa 37:20 consume away בֶּעָשָׁן‎ in the form of, as, smoke 78:33; 102:4; Job 34:36; 36:14.

II. Denoting proximity —

1 at, by (not very common): 1Sam 29:1 בָּעַיִן‎ by the spring; Ezek 10:15, 20 כְּבָר בִּנְהַר‎ by the river Chebar (1:3 עַל‎).

2 on: Gen 8:20; Num 23:2 בַּמִּזְבֵּחַ‎ on the altar; Judg 8:21 on the necks of the camels; 1Kin 2:5 and elsewhere בְּמָתְנָיו‎ on his loins; Isa 59:17 a helmet בְּראֹשׁוֺ‎ on his head.

3 often with verbs of touching, approaching, taking hold of, cleaving, etc., as תָּפַשׂ תָּמַךְ, מָּגַּע, קָרַב, נָגַשׁ, נָגַע, חָשַׁק, דָּבֵק, אָחַז,‎ (see these words).

4 with words expressing or implying an act of hostility —

a. against: Gen 16:12 בּוֺ כֹּל וְיַד בַכֹּל יָדוֺ‎ his hand against all, and the hand of all against him; מְּ יַד ׳הָֽיְתָה בּ‎ Deut 13:10; 1Sam 5:9; 18:17 +? verbs (q. v.) of fighting (רִיב נִלְחַם,‎), going up to invade (עָלָה‎), being angry (אַף חָרָה אָנַף, הִתְעַבֵּר,‎), sinning or acting treacherously (כִּחֵשׁ כִּזֵּב, שִׁקֵּר, מָּשַׁע, מָעַל, מָרָה, מָרַד, בָּגַד, חָטָא,‎), testifying (הֵעִיד עָנָה,‎), mocking (הֵתֵל הִלְעִיג,‎), feeling loathing (קוּץ‎), rebuking (גָּעַר‎: properly to protest loudly), speaking (דִּבֶּר‎: Num 12:8b), etc., & even (an extreme case) Hosea 7:14 בִי יָסוּרוּ‎ they turn aside (so as to be) against me. In a weaker sense בַּקִּיר מַשְׁתִּין‎ mingens ad parietem 1Sam 25:22 and elsewhere

b. down to, upon (super with accusative), especially in such phrases as בּוֺ דָּמָיו‎ Lev 20:9 and elsewhere his blood be upon him; בְראֹשׁוֺ דָּמוֺ‎ his blood be upon his head Josh 2:19; י (הֵשִׁיב בְּראֹשׁוֺ׳שׁוּב )‎ Judges 8:57 (רָעָה‎), 1Kin 2:33 (דָּם‎), 2:44 (רָעָה‎), Psa 7:17 (עֲמָלוֺ‎); מּ דֶּרֶךְ נָתַן ׳בְּראֹשׁוֺ‎ 1Kin 8:32 & often in Ezekiel, as Ezek 9:10; 11:21.

III. 1. With —

a. of accompaniment: Num 20:20 כָּבֵד בְּעַם‎ with much people, Josh 22:8; Judg 11:34; 1Kin 10:2; 2Kin 5:9; Jer 41:15; Exod 21:22 and he shall give בִּפְלִלִים‎ with arbitrators (arbitrators being employed), Isa 8:16 בְּלִמֻּדָֽי׃‎ with my disciples, i.e. having them present; Exod 8:1; 8:13; Jer 11:19 בְּלַחְמוֺ עֵץ‎ a tree with its sap; 1Kin 19:19 הֶעָשָׂר בִּשְׁנֵים וְהוּא‎ and he with the 12th.

b. often of what one takes or brings with one: Gen 32:11 בְּמַקְלִי‎ with my staff I passed over Jordan, Judg 11:34; 15:1; 1Sam 1:24; Isa 7:24; Micah 6:6 and elsewhere בְּ קִדֵּם‎ to go to meet with; בְּ בָּא‎ to come with Lev 16:3; Psa 66:13; 71:16. (In Arabic this usage is developed more fully than in Hebrew, and literally to come with, literally to go away with, are used idiomatically in the sense of to bring, and to take away respectively: WAG ii. § 56 b.) Hence בְּלֹא בִּבְלִי, בְּאֶפֶס, בְּאֵין,‎ = without.

c. of concomitant (or surrounding) conditions, as בְּחִמָּזוֺן‎ with (or in) haste; בְּצֶדֶק‎ with (or in) righteousness; בִּשְׁגָגָה‎ in error; שׁוֺפָר וּבְקוֺל בִּתְרוּעָה‎ 2Sam 6:15; often in such phrases as בְּצִדְקָֽתְךָ‎ Psa 31:2 in thy righteousness; בְּחַסְדְּךָ‎ in thy mercy Exod 15:13; — בְּאָשְׁרִי‎ with my happiness! = happy am I Gen 30:13; Psa 29:4 the voice of ׳י‎ is בַּכֹּחַ‎ with power = is powerful; Exod 32:12 בְּרָעָה‎ with evil purpose; Psa 73:8 בְרָע‎ in wickedness; 90:10 בִּגְבוּרֹת‎ with strength.

2 of the instrument or means: as

a. בַּחֶרֶב‎ with the sword Exod 5:3 etc.; בְּרַגְלַיִם‎ with the feet Isa 28:3; . . . בְּיַד‎ by the hand of ... (see below יָד‎); to stone בְּאֶבֶן‎ or בָּֽאֲבָנִים‎ with stones Lev 20:2; Num 14:10 etc.; בְּ שָׁתָה‎ to drink with a cup Gen 44:5; Amos 6:6 (compare in Aramaic Dan 5:2); to cry בְּגָרוֺן‎ with the throat Isa 58:1; to burn בָּאֵשׁ‎ in or with fire (often); to slay or to perish בָּרָעָב‎ through hunger or בַּדֶּבֶר‎ through pestilence Exod 16:3; Jer 21:9 + often (compare Job 27:15 יִקָּבֵ֑רוּ בַּמָּוֶת‎); to save with or by Judg 7:7; 1Sam 14:6.

b. idiomatic, with certain verbs, as בְּ שָׁחַק‎ to play with Job 40:29; בְּ עָבַד‎ to labour with a person (as with an instrument), i.e. to use him as a slave Exod 1:14; Jer 22:13; 27:7 and elsewhere; בְּ עָשָׂה‎ 18:23; Neh 9:24; Dan 11:7; Est 1:15; 2:11; 3:11; 6:6; & perhaps בְּ דִּבֶּר‎ (of God) to speak with one Num 12:2, 8a and elsewhere (see Ew§ 217 f. (3); DrSm ii. 23, 2). Further ביהוה הִתְנַבֵּא בַּבַּעַל,‎ to prophesy with or by ׳י‎ or Baal, ׳י‎ or Baal being the inspirer; דָּרַשׁ בְּ, שָׁאַל‎ to inquire or ask by means of a god (or oracle).

c. ביהוה‎ through ׳י‎ (= by His aid) in many connections, as Psa 18:30; 44:6; 56:5; 56:11; Psalm 60:14; Isa 26:13; 45:25; Hosea 1:7; Zech 10:12; with passive verbs Deut 33:29; Isa 45:17 (to be saved): and even of the immediate cause Num 36:2 to be commanded ביהוה‎ by ׳י‎, Gen 9:6 בָּאָדָם‎ by man shall his blood be shed (both unusual), Hosea 14:4.

d. allied is the use of בְּ‎ in such phrases as to bless, swear, speak, prophesy, etc., בְּשֵׁם‎ in the name of ... (i.e. the name being used or appealed to in the act) Deut 6:13; Jer 11:21, etc. (so to swear ביהוה‎ by ׳י‎ Josh 2:12 etc.,בִּימִינוֺ‎ Isa 62:8; to bless בְּךָ‎ with thee Gen 48:20, to swear with me Psa 102:9, i.e. using my name in oath, Jer 29:22); almost = in the authority and power of 1Sam 17:45; 25:5; 1Kin 21:8; Micah 4:5; Zech 10:12; Psa 20:6; 44:6; 89:25.

e. בַּמָּה‎ or בַּמֶּה‎ by means of what? how? Gen 15:8; Judg 6:15; 16:5-6, 10; 1Kin 22:21; Mal 2:17. בְּזֹאת‎ by means of this Gen 42:15, 33. Exod 7:17; Num 16:28; with this = on this condition Gen 34:15, 22; 1Sam 11:2; Isa 27:9.

3 of cost or price (the Beth pretii), the price, whether given or received, being treated as the instrumental means by which the act is accomplished, with, for, at the cost of: thus regularly

a. with שָׂכַר‎ hire Gen 30:16, מָּדָה‎ redeem Exod 34:20, אֵרֵשׂ‎ betroth 2Sam 3:14, קָנָה‎ buy 24:24; 1Kin 2:23 בְּנַפְשׁוֺ‎ at the cost of his life hath A. spoken this word, 2Sam 23:17 who went בְּנַפְשׂוֺתָם‎ at peril of their lives, Prov 7:23; Lam 5:9; Josh 6:26 יְיַסְּדֶנָּה בִּבְכֹרוֺ‎ at the price of his firstborn shall he lay its foundations, 1Chr 12:19 בְּרָאשֵׁינוּ‎ to the jeopardy of our heads he will fall away, etc.

b. with מָכַר‎ sell Deut 21:14; עָבַד‎ serve Gen 29:18, 20; Hosea 12:13; הֵמִיר‎ exchange Lev 27:10; Hosea 4:7 their glory I will exchange for ignominy, Psa 106:20; בְּנֶשֶׁךְ נָתַן‎ to give for interest Lev 25:37; Psa 15:5; in other connections Gen 23:9; 47:16f.; Isa 45:13; Lam 1:11; Cant 8:7; 8:11; Deut 19:21 בְּעַיִן עַיִן בְּנֶפֶשׁ נֶפֶשׁ‎ life for life, eye for eye; Isa 2:22 הוּא נֶחְשָׁב בַּמֶּה‎ at what is he to be accounted? 7:23 a thousand vines כָּ֑סֶף בְּאֶלֶף‎ at a thousand (shekels of) silver. — Hence (perhaps) the idiomatic usages בְשָׁנָה שָׁנָה‎ year for year, one year like another, annually Deut 15:20 and elsewhere; בְּיוֺם יוֺם‎ (late), בְּיוֺם כְּיוֺם‎ 1Sam 18:10; בְּפַעַם כְּפַעַם‎ (see מַּעַם יוֺם,‎); בְּחֹדֶשׁ חֹדֶשׁ‎ 1Chr 27:1.

4 rather peculiarly, in certain cases where the object of an action may be treated as the instrument by which it is accomplished: as בְּראֹשׁ הֵנַיעַ‎ to shake with the head Jer 18:6; Job 16:4 (as well as ראֹשׁ הֵנִיעַ‎ Psa 22:8); to open with the mouth Job 16:10, with the lips Psa 22:8; to gnash with the teeth Job 16:9 (to gnash the teeth Psa 35:16); to wink with the eye Prov 6:13 (to wink the eye, ib. 10:10); בְּקוֺל נָתַן‎ to utter with the voice Psa 46:7; 68:34; Jer 12:8 (but קוֺל נָתַן‎ is more common); to stretch out with the hand Lam 1:17; בַּמַּטֶּה הֵרִים‎ (unusual) Exod 7:20. WAG ii. § 56 b β DeJob 31:12; Prov 20:30.

6 of the material with which a work is wrought, both absolute בַזָּהָב עָשָׁה‎ to work with gold Exod 31:4; 1Kin 7:14; and to make a thing with (in our idiom, of) gold Exod 38:8; Ezek 7:20; 1Kin 15:22 (בָּנָה‎). Without a verb Lev 13:52; 2Chr 9:18.

IV. בְּ‎ is used also with certain classes of verbs, though the explanation of its use may be sometimes doubtful: namely

a. with verbs of taking refuge, trusting, relying, as נִשְׁעַן חָסָה, בָּטַח, הֶאֱמִין,‎.

b. with verbs of ruling, governing, restraining, as שָׁלַט רָדָה, עָצַר, נָגַשׂ, מָשַׁל, מָלַךְ,‎.

c. with verbs of rejoicing, feeling pleasure or satisfaction, etc., as שָׂבַע רָצָה, חָפֵץ, שָׂמֵחַ, שׂוּשׂ, עָלַץ, גִּיל,‎ (but with this verb מִן‎ is more common). [Probably a case of

III. 5.]

d. with verbs expressive of sensible perception, to denote the pleasurable or attentive exercise of the faculty concerned, as בְּ שָׁמַע‎ to listen to, חָזָה רָאָה, בְּ, הִבִּיט‎, to look upon, בְּ הֵרִיחַ‎ to smell at (see these words).

e. occasionally also with verbs of speaking, thinking, mentioning, knowing, to denote the object of the action, as בְּ דִּבֶּר‎ to speak about Deut 6:7; Psa 87:3 and elsewhere (see below דִּבֶּר‎); שִׂיחַ הָגָה, בְּ, הִלֵּל‎ 44:9; 63:7; 69:13; יָדַע‎ Jer 38:24; זָכַר‎ once 3:16, הִזְכִּיר‎ often; Psa 71:6 תְהִלָּתִי בְּךָ‎ of thee is my praise.

V. Followed by an infinitive with, בְּ‎ forms a periphrasis for the gerund, though in English it is commonly to be rendered by a verb and conjunction, namely: —

1 as a temporal conjunction, as Gen 2:4 בְּהִבָּֽרְאָם‎ in their being created = when they were created, 4:8 בִּהְיוֺתָם‎ in their being (= when they were) in the field; and constantly. Sometimes it has in appearance the force of after that, as 33:18; Exod 3:12; 13:17; but as a rule this is really due to the action denoted by the infinitive being treated as extending over a period within which the action of the principal verb takes place: so especially in the phrase מִמִּצְרַיִם יִשְׂרָאֵל בְּצֵאת‎, even of events at the close of the 40 years, Deut 4:45; 23:5; Josh 5:4, the whole period being treated as that in which Egypt was left (compare 2Kin 2:1, where the time included is future). Cases, however, occur in which this explanation will hardly apply, as Deut 27:4; 24:12.

2 as a causal conjunction (compare above

III. 5), as Gen 19:16 עָלָיו י ׳בְּחֶמְלַת‎ through J.'s having compassion upon him, Exod 16:7; 33:16; 34:29; Deut 1:27 י ׳בְּשִׂנְאַת אֹתָנוּ‎ through J.'s hating us, etc. (9:28 similarly מִן‎), 1Kin 18:18 (= in that ye have ...) Ezek 9:8; 43:8; 44:7; 2Chr 28:6.

3 as a concessive conjunction, when = though: Isa 1:15; Psa 46:3 אָ֑רֶץ בְּהָמִיר‎ though the earth do change.

Note. — Exod 10:12 בָּאַרְבֶּה‎ can only be rendered 'with the locusts,' the locusts being conceived as implicit in Moses' uplifted hand: but probably לָאַרְבֶּה‎ should be read. Thrice in late Hebrew בְּ‎ is used peculiarly: 1Chr 7:23 for with misfortune was it in his house (בְּרָעָה‎ chosen for the purpose of explaining בְּרִיעָה‎); 9:33 בַּמְּלָאכָה עֲלֵיהֶם‎ it devolved upon them with the work; Ezra 3:3 for with terror (was it) upon them from the peoples of the countries (the sentence without a verb as often in Chronicles: DrIntr. 504 f.): compare 8:34. compare Ew§ 295 f.
