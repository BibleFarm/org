H577. annah

or anna אָֽנָּ֫א‎ interjection (from אָהּ‎ and נָא‎, q. v.) a strong particle of entreaty, ah, now! I (or We) beseech thee! often followed by an imperative; Gen 50:17 (to Joseph) וג נָא שָׂא ׳אָנָּא‎ Ah, now ! forgive, we pray, etc., Exod 32:31 (to God); elsewhere always followed by ׳י‎ or אֲדֹנָי‎ Psa 118:25 (twice in verse); Neh 1:5, 11; Dan 9:4. Written אָֽנָּ֫ה‎ 2Kin 20:3 (= Isa 38:3) Jonah 1:14; 4:2; Psa 116:4; 116:16.

אנב‎ (perhaps spring, leap, so DlHA 65, Pr 114, inferred from Assyrian annabu, hare; against this NöZMG 1886, 734).
