[Heb] פּוּךְ‎ ETCBC: פּוּךְ‎ (subs｜mascara) OSHL: q.ao.ag TWOT: 1742 GK: H7037 Greek: πολυτελής

KJV: fair colours, glistering, paint(-ed) (-ing).

H6320. puk

מּוּךְ‎ noun [masculine] antimony, stibium (Late Hebrew = Biblical Hebrew; compare possibly Arabic open, separate; Syriac pulverize; but מּוּךְ‎ perhaps foreign word); — black mineral powder, for increasing brilliance of eyes by darkening edges of lids; עֵינֶוהָ בֵּמּ ׳וַתָּשֶׂם‎ 2Kin 9:30, וגו ׳תִּקְרְעִי‎ Jer 4:30; (on practice in Arabic see WeGGN, 1893, 443; in Egypt, LaneMod.Egypt.ed. 5, i. 45 f.); figurative Isa 54:11 of dark cement setting off precious stones (but WeDLZ, Aug. 2, 1890 CheHpt and others read נֹפֶךְ‎ q. v.); פ ׳אַבְנֵי‎ 1Chr 29:2 are perhaps stones of brilliant hue of antimony. — compare proper name הַמּוּךְ קֶרֶן‎, and כָּחַל‎.
