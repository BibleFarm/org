H4432. Molek

מֹ֫לֶךְ‎ proper name, of a divinity Molech (ᵐ5‎ Μολοχ, ᵑ9‎ Moloch) (= מֶלֶךְ‎ i.e. (divine) King, with vowels of בּשֶׁת‎ to denote abhorrence, HoffmZAW iii. 1883, 124 RSSemitic i. 353; 2nd ed. 372; compare HoffmGG Abh. xxxvi. 1890 a (May, 1889), 25; Phoenician proper name, of divinity מלך‎ Milk, in proper names compare Bloch, DrDt.222 f.); — with article ׳הַמּ‎: — the god to whom Israel sacrificed children with fire (in valley of Hinnom); מּ לַ בָּנֵשׁ ׳העביר‎ 2Kin 23:10; לַמּ ׳העביר‎ Jer 32:35 (|| הַבַּעַל‎), Lev 18:21 (H); לַמּ ׳נתן‎ 20:2-3, 4 (H); more Generally הַמּ אַחֲרֵי ׳לִזְנוֺת‎ 20:5 (H). In 1Kin 11:7 read probably מִלְכֹּם‎, q. v.
