בֹּהוּ‎ (bôhûw｜bo'-hoo｜noun｜masculine｜emptiness)
[Heb] בֹּהוּ‎ ETCBC: בֹּהוּ‎ (subs｜emptiness) OSHL: b.aj.ab TWOT: 205a GK: H983
Derivation: from an unused root (meaning to be empty);
Strong's: a vacuity, i.e. (superficially) an undistinguishable ruin
KJV: emptiness, void.

H922. bohu

בֹּ֫הוּ‎ noun [masculine] emptiness (on form see Ges§ 84a, 1 b Sta§ 95, 198 a, on usage compare LagOr. ii. 60 f.) always with תֹּהוּ‎ q. v.; — וָבֹהוּ תֹּהוּ‎ Gen 1:2 of primeval earth; Jer 4:23 of earth under judgment of ׳י‎; בֹהוּ וְאַבְנֵי קַותֿֿהֹוּ‎ Isa 34:11, the line of wasteness and the stones of emptiness, i.e. plummets, employed, not as usual for building, but for destroying walls; compare Di & see below אבן‎ 6
