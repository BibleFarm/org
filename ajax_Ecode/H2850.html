H2850. Chitti

חִתִּי‎ adjective and

name, of a people Hittite(s) (Egyptian –-tá, –etá W Max MüllAsien u. Europa, 319 ff.; Assyrian –atti, SchrKG 192 ff. COT on Gen 10:19 DlPa, 269 ff.; Tel el-Amarna –atti, –atta BezTel el Amarn. Brit. Mus. 150) — masculine always with article הַחִתִּי‎ 23:10; Exod 3:8 +; feminine חִתִּית‎ Ezek 16:3, 45; masculine plural הַחִתִּים‎ Josh 1:4 3t.; feminine plural חִתִּיֹּת‎ 1Kin 11:1; —

1. adjective , of seller of Machpelah to Abraham, הַחִתִּי עֶפְרוֺן‎ Gen 23:10; 49:29-30, 50:13; ׳הַח בֶּןצֹֿחַר עֶפְרֹן‎ 25:9 (all P); of fathers of Esau's wives ׳הַח בְּאֵרִי‎ 26:34; הַח ׳אֵילוֺן‎ 26:34; 36:2 (all P); also of warriors of David, הַח ׳אֲחִימֶלֶךְ‎ 1Sam 26:6, and especially הַח ׳אוּרִיָּה‎ 2Sam 11:3, 6, 17, 21, 24; 12:9-10, 23:39; 1Kin 15:5; 1Chr 11:41; feminine singular = substantive, only of (religious) ancestry of Jerusalem Ezek 16:3 thy father was the Amorite, and thy mother was a Hittite woman חִתִּית‎, similarly 16:45; feminine plural = substantive of Solomon's foreign wives, חִתִּיֹּת‎ 1Kin 11:1 Hittite women (+ צֵדְנִיֹּת אֲדֹמִיֹּת, עַמּוֺנִיּוֺת, מוֺאֲבִיּוֺת,‎).

2. n. usually

a. collective הַחִתִּי‎ the Hittites: in lists of Canaanitish peoples Gen 15:20 (JE), Exod 3:8, 11 (both J), 13:5; 23:23, 28; 33:2; 34:11; Num 13:29; Josh 3:10 (all J E), Deut 7:1; 20:17; Josh 9:1; 12:8; 24:11 (all D); read also for הַחִוִּי‎ Judg 3:3 (so We Mey BuUrg 350); in Josh 11:3 (D) strike out הַחִתִּי‎ We Mey Bul.c., and read then הַחִתִּי‎ for הַחִוִּי‎ vb (so ᵐ5‎, not ᵐ5‎L); further 1Kin 9:20 2Chr 8:7; Ezra 9:1; Neh 9:8.

b. plural הַחִתִּים אֶרֶץ כֹּל ׃הַחִתִּים‎ Josh 1:4 (D) nearly = land of Canaanites; הַח ׳ארץ‎ specifically of northern home of Hittites Judg 1:26; so also 2Sam 24:6 where read קֵ֫דְשָׁה הַחִתִּים אֶרֶץ‎ (for חָדְשִׁי תחתים ᵑ0‎, see קֵדֶשׁ חָדְשִׁי,‎); הַחִתִּים מַלְכֵי‎ 1Kin 10:29 2Chr 1:17 (both + אֲרָם מַלְכֵי‎), 2Kin 7:6 (+ מִצְרַיִם מַלְכֵי‎, both feared by אֲרָם‎). Hence it appears that (םׅ הַחִתִּי‎ had their proper seat in the north — where also they were encountered by Assyrians from time of Tiglath-Pileser I (see Schr Dl1. c.), and by Egyptians from time of Tutmes III (see W Max Müll.l.c.) — (compare also Judg 3:3; Josh 11:3 above), but that individual Hittites were known in Israel (compare, besides 1Sam 2:6; 2Sam 11:3 etc., 1Kin 9:20 above); that the Hittites were regarded (by J E D) as one of the peoples of Canaan, and that the name even came to be used in more General sense for Canaanites. Only in P do they appear as having a definite settlement in the south, and are designated by בְּנֵיחֵֿת‎ (see חֵת‎), as well as by the adjective הַחִתִּי‎.
