I. עָזַב‎213 verb leave, forsake, loose (Late Hebrew id. (rare); Arabic be remote, absent, depart, Assyrian ezêbu, leave, Shaph`el ušezib, rescue, compare Biblical Aramaic שֵׁיזֵב‎; — Ethiopic widowed Di973); —

1 leave, with accusative (on order of meanings compare NöZMG. xl (1886). 726):

a. = depart from, accusative of person Gen 44:22 (twice in verse) (J), 2Kin 2:2, 4, 6; 4:30; Num 10:31 (P), Ruth 1:16; Jer 9:1 (|| מֵאֵת הָלַךְ‎); accusative of location 1Kin 8:6; Jer 25:28 (׳י‎ as lion leaving lair); with מִן‎ local intransitive (strangely) 18:14 (of snow).

b. leave behind, accusative of person Exod 2:20 (J), 2Sam 15:16 (+ infinitive), Ezek 24:21; accusative of thing + בְּיַד‎ Gen 39:12-13, + אֶצְלִי‎ 39:15; 39:18 (all J); + ב‎ location 50:8 (J); accusative of person and thing Exod 9:21 (J).

c. leave in the presence of (לִפְנֵי‎) 1Chr 16:37 (ל‎ of accusative, compare ל‎ 3b above), 2Chr 28:14.

d. leave in safety, כְּבוֺדְכֶם חַעַזְבוּ וְאָ֫נָה‎ Isa 10:3.

e. leave in a given condition, situation, ׳ע בְמַחֲלֻיִים אֹתוֺ‎2Chr 24:25 they left him in great suffering; with adverb accusative Ezek 23:29; a city מְּתוּחָה‎ Josh 8:17.

f. leave undisturbed, let alone Ruth 2:16.

g. leave unexercised, Gen 24:27 pregnantly he hath not left his kindness and his faithfulness from (being) with (מֵעִם‎) my master; so ׳ע אֶתֿ חַסְדּוֺ‎ Ruth 2:20 (both of ׳י‎); ׳ע חֵמָה‎ Psa 37:8 (|| מֵאַף הֶרֶף‎).

h. leave in the hand of, entrust to, accusative of thing + בְּיַד‎ Gen 39:6 (J), + אֶלֿ‎ Job 39:11 (|| בְּ בָּטַח‎), + עַלֿ‎ Psa 10:14 (accusative omitted, his cause). — So also possibly (with לְ‎) Nehemiah 3:34 (reading לֵאלֹהִים‎ for לָהֶם ᵑ0‎), but text probably otherwise corrupt.

i. leave to (ל‎) one (unaided), לוֺ מֵעֲזֹב וְחָדַלְתָּ֫‎ Exod 23:5 a (E) thou shalt refrain from leaving it (that is, the affair) to him; compare בֵּיצֶיהָ לָאָרֶץ תַּעֲזֹב‎ Job 39:14 (of ostrich).

j. leave over, remaining, accusative of person Josh 2:21 (JE); accusative of thing + לְ‎ person Lev 19:10; 23:22 (H), Malachi 3:19.

k. leave = have nothing to do with Prov 9:6.

2 leave, abandon, forsake:

a. abandon, accusative of thing: (1) land, house, city, tent, etc., 1Sam 31:7 = 1Chr 10:7; 2Kin 7:7; Jer 9:18; 48:28; 51:9; 2Chr 11:14; Isa 17:9 (+ מִמְּנֵי‎ of enemy); passive participle of cities 17:2; Jer 4:29; Zeph 2:4, compare עֲזוּבָה‎ Isa 17:9 participle = substantive = deserted region. (2) oxen 1Kin 19:20, idol-images (+ שָׁם‎) 2Sam 5:21 = 1Chr 14:12, flock Zech 11:17; passive participle of eggs Isa 10:14.

b. forsake, (1) human subject and object: Gen 2:24 (J; a man his parents), 1Sam 30:13; Josh 22:3 (D), Jer 49:11; Psa 27:10 (parent a child), Prov 2:17 (wife her husband, אַלוּף‎), compare אִשָּׁה עֲזוּבָה‎ Isa 54:6, and ׳ע‎ of personified Zion 60:15; 62:4. (2) an animal its young Jer 14:5 (object om).

c. = neglect, Levite Deut 12:19; 14:27, poor Job 20:19.

d. especially (1) figurative of forsaking God (׳י‎), apostatizing, Judg 10:10; Deut 28:20; 31:16; Jer 1:16 38t. (often Jeremiah and later); + Jonah 2:9 (object חַסְדָּם‎, i.e. God, see חֶסֶד‎

II); with ל‎ + infinitive only ׳אֶתיֿ לִשְׁמֹר עָֽזְבוּ‎ Hosea 4:10 (Oort We Now doubt לִשְׁמֹר‎). (2) object ׳י‎s law, commands, covenant, etc., Deut 29:24; 1Kin 19:10, 14 14t.; house of ׳י‎2Chr 24:18; Nehemiah 10:40. (3) forsake, fail to follow, obey advice, instruction, wisdom, reproof: 1Kin 12:8, 13 2Chr 10:8, 13; Prov 2:13; 4:2, 6; 10:17; 15:10; 27:10. (4) but also of forsaking idols Ezek 20:8, sins of various kinds 23:8; Isa 55:7; Prov 28:13 (object omitted; + מוֺדֶה‎ confess); הַמַּשָּׁא‎ usury Neh 5:10.

e. of God's forsaking, abandoning men: Deut 31:17; Isa 42:16; 49:14; 54:7; 2Chr 12:5; Ezra 9:9; Psa 9:11; 22:2 8t. Psalms; + בְּיַד‎ Neh 9:28; Psa 37:33; 16:10 thou wilt not abandon my soul לִשְׁאוֺל‎; of temporary abandonment 2Chr 32:31 (+ infinitive of purpose); etc. (34 t. in all); + Jer 12:7 (׳י‎ abandoning his house).

f. of God's leaving, i.e. ceasing to regard, the earth, thus giving impunity to crime, Ezek 8:12; 9:9.

g. of strength (כֹּחַ‎) forsaking one Psa 38:11, courage (לֵב‎) 40:13, kindness and faithfulness (וֶאֱמֶת חֶסֶד‎) Prov 3:3.

3 let loose, set free, let go תַּעֲזֹב עָזֹב עִמּוֺ‎ Exod 23:5b (E) thou shalt by all means free it (that is, the beast) with him, (aid him to set it free; on sense, compare Deut 22:4; DHM below II.עזב‎); וְעָזוּב עָצוּר‎ shut up and freed, proverb. phrase, = all classes of people 32:36; 1Kin 14:10; 21:21; 2Kin 9:8; 14:26 (exact meaning dubious; probably either = bond and free, or [see RSSem i. 437, 2nd ed. 456] under taboo and free from it); let go Job 20:13 (c, accusative of wickedness as morsel in mouth); let loose my complaint 10:1 (עָלַי‎ apud me, compare עַל‎

II 1 d); פָנַי אֶעֶזְבָה‎ Job 9:27 I will loosen, relax, my face, (compare Thes De Me Stu Di BuhlLex Bu; > abandon my [gloomy] countenance AV RV SS BaeKau Du).
