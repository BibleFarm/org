חֹמֶר‎ (chômer｜kho'mer｜noun｜masculine｜cement｜heap｜homer)
[Heb] חֹמֶר‎ ETCBC: חֹמֶר‎ (subs｜clay), חֹמֶר‎ (subs｜homer) OSHL: h.dp.ab, h.dp.ac, h.do.ad TWOT: 683c GK: H2816, H2817, H2818 Greek: ἄχυρον, κονιορτός, κόρος, πηλός
Derivation: from חָמַר‎;
Strong's: properly, a bubbling up, i.e. of water, a wave; of earth, mire or clay (cement); also a heap; hence, a chomer or dry measure
KJV: clay, heap, homer, mire, motion.

H2563. chomer

I. חֹ֫מֶר‎ noun masculine cement, mortar, clay; — ׳ח‎ absolute Gen 11:3 13t.; construct Isa 10:6 2t.; —

1 mortar, cement for holding building-stones (brick) together Gen 11:3 (J), Exod 1:14 (P; both || לְבֵנִים‎); Nahum 3:14 (|| טיט‎).

2 clay:

a. as material of vessels Jer 18:4, 6, ׳ח היצר‎ Isa 29:16, in simile of God's fashioning man 45:9; 64:7; Job 10:9; as material of human bodies ח ׳בָּתֵּי‎ 4:19; compare 33:6; as material of bulwarks (disparagingly) 13:12; ׳ח חותם‎ = seal-clay i.e. clay upon which seal is pressed.

b. = mire ׳ח חוצות‎ Isa 10:6; לַח ׳הֹרָנִי‎ Job 30:19 he hath cast me into the mire, i.e. deeply humiliated me (עָפָר‎ and אֵפֶר‎ in || clause); as simile of commonness, abundance 27:16 (|| עָפָר‎).

II. חֹ֫מֶר‎ noun [masculine] heap (compare 1. חֲמוֺר‎) — ׳ח‎ construct ׳ח רַבִּים מַיִם‎ Hab 3:15 a heap of great waters (|| יָם‎) — but text dubious, see I. חמר‎; plural חֳמָרִים הֳמָרִים‎ Exod 8:10 (J), of dead frogs gathered in heaps.

R Exod 36:19 a. b Strm4760, Imêru, a measure ZimBP 6 n) — ׳ח‎ absolute Isa 5:10 5t. Ezekiel; construct Hosea 3:2-3t.; plural חֳמָרִים‎Num 11:32; — ׳ח‎ of barley Hosea 3:2; Ezek 45:13, compare ח ׳זֶרַע שְׂעֹרִים‎ Lev 27:16 i.e. land on which a ׳ח‎ of barley seed was sown (taxed at 50 shekels); ׳ח‎ of wheat Ezek 45:13; in General ח ׳זֶרַע‎ Isa 5:10; = 10 ephahs (or baths, see בַּת‎ below בתת‎) Ezek 45:11 (3 t. in verse); 45:14 (twice in verse) (in 45:14 strike out Co); on actual size of ׳ח‎, = 393.9 litres, see HultschMetrol. 2nd ed. 448, 452f.
