מָלַק‎ (mâlaq｜maw-lak'｜verb｜nip)
[Heb] מָלַק‎ ETCBC: מלק‎ (verb｜nip off) OSHL: m.ck.aa TWOT: 1207 GK: H4916
Derivation: a primitive root;
Strong's: to crack a joint; by implication, to wring the neck of afowl (without separating it)
KJV: wring off.

H4454. malaq

מָלַק‎ verb nip, nip off (head of bird; without necessarily severing it from body, compare Lev 5:8) (Late Hebrew id., Aramaic מְלַק‎,); —

Qal Perfect consecutive אֶתרֿאֹשׁוֺ וּמָלַק‎ Lev 1:15, i.e. head of dove; compare יַבְדִּיל וְלֹא עָרְמּוֺ מִמּוּל אֶתרֿאֹשׁוֺ וּמָלַק‎ 5:8. compare Di-Ry. ᵐ5‎ ἀποκνίζω.
