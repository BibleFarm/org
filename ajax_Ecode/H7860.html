H7860. shoter

שֹׁטֵר‎ noun masculine official, officer (properly scribe, secretary? or from original meaning as arranger, organizer?); — absolute ׳שׁ‎ Prov 6:7, ׳שׁו‎2Chr 26:11; plural שֹׁטְרִים‎ Deut 1:15 +, etc.; — apparently subordinate officer, judicial, civil or military (compare DrDeuteronomy 1. 15 GrayNum 11:16); organizing people for marching Josh 1:10; 3:2 (both E), compare Deut 20:5, 8, 9; appointed over Israel by Egyptian overseers Exod 5:14, compare 5:6; 5:10; 5:15; 5:19 (all J); elsewhere + זְקֵנִים‎ Num 11:16 (E), Deut 29:9; 31:28; + שֹׁפְטִים‎ 16:18; 1Chr 23:4; 26:29, + ׳ז‎ and ׳שֹׁפ‎ Josh 8:33 (RD), and ( + רָאשִׁים‎) 23:2 (RD), 24:1 (E, or RD); ׳שֹׁט‎ as minor judges also Deut 1:15; ׳שֹׁט אֶתהַֿמֶּלֶךְ הַמְשָֽׁרְתִים‎ 1Chr 27:1, chosen from Levites 2Chr 19:11; 34:13 (+ שׁוֺעֲרִים סוֺפְרִים,‎); as title of individual 26:11 (|| הַסּוֺפֵר‎); of ant, וּמשֵׁל שֹׁטֵר קָצִין אֵיןלָֿהּ‎ Prov 6:7.
