H2903. totaphoth

טוֺטָפוֺת‎ noun feminine plural bands (Late Hebrew טוֺטֶפֶת‎ Aramaic טוֺטֶפְתָּא‎ and especially plural טוטפין‎ id.) — always bands, frontlet-bands, between the eyes (|| יד על אוֺת‎; compare עֵינֶיךָ בֵּין זִכָּרוֺן‎ Exod 13:9 || id.); figurative of dedication of firstborn עיניך בין לְטוֺטָפֹת ֗֗֗ וְהָיָה‎ 13:16 (JE); of commandments of לְטו ׳וְהָיוּ ׳י,‎ Deut 11:18 and לְטֹטָפת וְהָיוּ‎ 6:8. — This injunction, originally figurative for perpetual remembrance (otherwise Kn, and Kleinl.c. 666 ff., see above, BenzArchaeology 111 NowArchaeology i. 134), was taken literally by later Jews, and hence the custom of wearing phylacteries; see Di (on Exod 13:16) WinerRE RiHWB (article Denkzettel) SmithDict. Bib. (article Frontlets) StaZAW 1894, 312 f., 317.
