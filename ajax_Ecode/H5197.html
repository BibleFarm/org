H5197. nataph

[נָטַף‎] verb drop, drip, figurative (especially of prophet) discourse (Late Hebrew id.; נְטַף ᵑ7‎ drop, drip; Syriac drip, drop (NöSyr. Gr. § 105) etc.; Arabic id.; Ethiopic id., colare (stillando), expercolare); —

Qal Perfect3masculine plural נָָֽטְפוּ‎ Judg 5:4 2t.; נָטָּ֑פוּ‎ 5:4; Imperfect3feminine singular תִּטֹּף‎ Job 29:22; 3masculine plural יִטּפוּ‎ Joel 4:18; 3feminine plural תִּטֹּפְנָה‎ Prov 5:3; Song 4:11; Participle נֹטְפוֺת‎ 5:13; — drop, drip, usually transitive, with accusative, of clouds dropping water (מַיִ‎), in storm Judg 5:4, || heavens 5:4 (object omitted), Psa 68:9 (id.; all theoph.); of hands dripping myrrh (מוֺר‎) Song 5:5; in predict. עָסִיס יִטְּפוּ‎ Joel 4:18 the mountains shall drip must; figurative of (seductive) speech תִּטּ ׳נֹפֶת‎ Prov 5:3 the lips of the strange woman drop honey, so perhaps Song 4:11 (Bu of kisses); נ ׳שִׂפְתוֺתָיו מוֺר‎ 5:13 his lips drip myrrh (perhaps of sweet breath). Intransitive only מִלָּתִי תִּטֹּף עָלֵימוֺ‎ Job 29:22 upon them my speech used to drop.
