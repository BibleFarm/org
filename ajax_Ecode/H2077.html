H2077. zebach

I. זֶ֫בַח‎162 noun masculineJudg 16:23 sacrifice **Phoenician זבח‎ of an offering of bread and oil CISi. 165, 12; 167. 8, compare RSSemitic 205 (2nd ed. 222). ׳ז‎ absolute Gen 31:54 +; construct Exod 34:25 +; זָ֑בַח‎ Lev 17:8 16t.; suffix זִבְחוֺ‎ 7:16; Ezek 34:15, זִבְחֲכֶם‎ Lev 19:6; plural זְבָחִים‎ Gen 46:1 +; construct זִבְחֵי‎ Lev 17:5 +; suffix 3 masculine plural זִבְחֵיהֶם‎ 17:5, זְבָחֵימוֺ‎ Deut 32:38, זִבְחֹתָם‎ Hosea 4:19 etc.; — the common and most ancient sacrifice, whose essential rite was eating the flesh of the victim at a feast in which the god of the clan shared by receiving the blood and fat pieces. In the older literature it is distinguished from מִנְחָה‎ and עֹלָה‎, in the later literature from חַטָּאת‎ and אָשָׁם‎.

I. general name for all sacrifices eaten at feasts: —

1 of the God of Israel Gen 46:1; Exod 10:25; 18:12 (E) Hosea 3:4; 6:6; 9:4; Amos 5:25; Isa 1:11; 19:21; Deut 12:27; 18:3; 1Sam 2:13, 29; 3:14; 6:15; 9:12-13, 15:22 (twice in verse); 16:3, 5 (twice in verse); 2Sam 15:12; 1Kin 8:62; 12:27; 2Kin 5:17; 16:15; Psa 40:7; 50:8; 51:18; 51:19; Prov 15:8; 21:3, 27; Isa 43:23-24, 56:7; Jer 6:20; 7:21-22, 17:26; 33:18; Ezek 40:42; 44:11; 1Chr 29:21 (twice in verse); 2Chr 7:1, 4, 5, 12; Neh 12:43; Dan 9:27; Ecclesiastes 4:17. They should be צֶדֶק זִבְחֵי‎ sacrifices of righteousness (offered in righteousness by the righteous) Deut 33:19; Psa 4:6; Psalm 51:21; הַבְהָבַי זִבְחֵי‎ sacrifices of gifts to me (׳י‎) Hosea 8:13; the temple is זָבַ֑ח בֵּית‎2Chr 7:12; they should not be ׳ז רִיב‎ sacrifices of strife, where strife prevails Prov 17:1.

2 of other deities Exod 34:15; Num 25:2; Deut 32:38 (JE) Judg 16:23; 2Kin 10:19, 24; Hosea 4:19; Isa 57:7; Ezek 20:28; these are מֵתִים זִבְחֵי‎ sacrifices to dead things (lifeless gods, opposed to ׳י‎ as living), Psa 106:28.

II. there are several kinds of זְבָחִים‎ which are gradually distinguished:

1 the covenant sacrifice, between Jacob and Laban Gen 31:54 (E), with God Psa 50:5 (compare Exod 24:5; 1Sam 11:15).

2 the passover הַמֶּסַח חַג זֶבַח‎ Exod 34:25 (JE), פסח זבח‎ 12:27 (J); and probably also זבחי‎ 23:18 (JE) = 34:25 (id.)

3 annual sacrifice הַיָּמִים זֶבַח‎ 1Sam 1:21; 2:19; 20:6, compare ׳ז מִשְׁמָּחָה‎ sacrifice of the clan 20:29.

4 thank offering ׳ז (הַ)תּוֺדָה‎ Lev 7:12; 22:29; Psa 107:22; 116:17, which is given as הַזָּבַח זִבְחוֺ, ׳ז, קָרְבָּנוֺ‎ Lev 7:16-17, and as a variety of שְׁלָמִים‎ 7:13, 15; it is implied in תְרוּעָה זִבְחֵי‎ Psa 27:6; yet זֶבַח‎ is Generic with תּוֺדָה‎2Chr 29:31 (twice in verse).

5 in ritual of H & P זבחים‎ are defined by שׁלמים‎ (q. v.) Lev 3:1 39t. Leviticus, Numbers, 1Sam 10:8; 1Kin 8:63; 2Chr 30:22; 33:16; Prov 7:14, but sometimes (rarely) ׳ז‎ alone is used in this sense, with or without suffix, where the meaning is plain from the context: Lev 17:5, 7, 8; 19:6; 23:37 (H) Num 15:3, 5, 8; Josh 22:26, 29 (all P). These ritual offerings are of three varieties נֶדֶר תּוֺדָה,‎ and נְדָבָה‎ Lev 7:12, 16 (P); in the several lists זבחים‎ are distinguished from תודות‎ Amos 4:4f.; 2Chr 29:31, from נדרים‎ Num 15:8 (P) Deut 12:6, 11; Jonah 1:16, from נדבות‎ Amos 4:4f.; Deut 12:6 and from the more comprehensive שׁלמים‎ Num 15:8; Josh 22:27 (P). So also the sacrifice at the institution of the covenant at Horeb Exod 24:5 (J E) and the sacrifice at the installation of Saul 1Sam 11:5 are defined as שׁלמים זבחים‎. Thus ׳ז‎ seems not only to be used for all these special forms but also to include other festal sacrifices not defined in the codes of law. The ritual was the same for the entire class. They were all sacrifices for feasts in which the flesh of the victim was eaten by the offerers, except so far as the officiating priests had certain choice pieces and the blood and fat pieces went to the altar for God. The sacrifice at the institution of the covenant at Horeb, the Passover victim, and the ram of consecration, were special, in that there was an application of a portion of the blood to the persons and things which were to be consecrated, Exod 12:22; 24:8; Lev 8:23.

6 the slaughter of hostile nations is also a זֶבַח‎ offered by God Himself, in which the vultures devour the flesh of the victims Isa 34:6; Jer 46:10; Ezek 39:17 (twice in verse); 39:19; Zeph 1:7-8,.

7 the verb used with זֶבַח‎ is ordinarily זָבַח‎ but others also are found e.g. עָשָׂה‎ Num 6:17; 15:3, 5, 8; Josh 22:23 (P) 1Kin 12:27; 2Kin 5:17; 10:24; Jer 33:18 (compare Sabean דֿבח עסי‎ Hal148 DHMZMG 1888, 374); הֵבִיא‎ Amos 4:4; Deut 12:6, 11; Jer 17:26, הִגִּישׁ‎ Amos 5:25, הִקְרִיב‎ Lev 7:11, 16; 22:21 (all P), עָבַד‎ Isa 19:21, נָתַן‎ Ecclesiastes 4:17, זֶבַח אוֺ עֹלָה יַעֲלָה‎ Lev 17:8 (H), ז ׳בִּשֵּׁל‎ Ezek 46:24, וְהַזָּבַ֑ח אֶתהָֿעוֺלָה שָׁחַט‎ 40:42; compare 44:11.
