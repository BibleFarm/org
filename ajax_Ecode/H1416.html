[Heb] גְּדוּד‎ ETCBC: גְּדוּד‎ (subs｜band) OSHL: c.ao.ab TWOT: 313a GK: H1522 Greek: γεννάω, ἰσχυρός, λῃστής
Derivation: from גָּדַד‎;
Strong's: a crowd (especially of soldiers)
KJV: army, band (of men), company, troop (of robbers).

H1416. gedud

I. גְּדוּד‎ noun masculineGen 49:19 band, troop (as making inroads; others, as a division, detachment (as severed), but this usually later in Hebrew) — ׳ג‎ absolute 1Sam 30:8 19t.; construct 2Chr 25:9; plural גְּדוּדִים‎ 2Sam 4:2 2t.; construct גְּדוּדֵי‎ 2Kin 6:23 6t.; suffix גְּדוּדָיו‎ Job 19:12; 25:3; —

1 marauding band (making incursions, inroads, compare גדד‎) 1Sam 30:8, 15 (twice in verse); 30:23 compare 1Chr 12:22, also 2Chr 22:1; 1Kin 11:24; 2Kin 5:2; 6:23; 13:20-21, 24:2 (4 t. in verse) compare 2Sam 22:30 = Psa 18:30, & Gen 49:19 (see גַּד‎ 30:11) Hosea 6:9; 7:1; Jer 18:22; גִּדוּדִים שָׂרֵי‎ 2Sam 4:2, compare הַגְּדוּד בְּרָאשֵׁי וַיִּתְּנֵם‎ 1Chr 12:19; figurative of God's attacking forces, his chastisements Job 19:12 (compare 25:3 below)

2 troop, of divisions of army of Israel (late) מִלְחָמָה צְבָא גְּדוּדֵי‎ 1Chr 7:4, compare 2Chr 26:11; band of Israel, i.e. troop of mercenaries hired from Israel by Amaziah 25:9-10, הַגְּדוּד בְּנֵי‎ i.e. soldiers of the band25:13; of army in General בַּגְּדוּד כְּמֶלֶךְ‎ Job 29:25, in simile; also Micah 4:14 בַּתגְּֿדוּד‎ of Zion.

3 foray, raid 2Sam 3:22.
