[טָבַח‎] verb slaughter, butcher, slay (Late Hebrew id. (rare) and derivatives; Phoenician טבח‎ participle; Assyrian ‰abâ—u AsrbAnnals iii. 56; Aramaic , טְבַח‎ (rare) and derivatives; Arabic cook meat, also bake bread; Ethiopic slay) —

Qal Perfect3masculine singular suffix וּטְבָחוֺ‎ consecutive Exodus 21:37; 3feminine singular טָֽבְחָה‎ Prov 9:2; 2masculine singular טָבַחְתָּ‎ Lam 2:21; 1singular טָבַחְתִּי‎ 1Sam 25:11; Imperative וּטְבֹחַ‎ Gen 43:16; Infinitive construct טְבֹחַ‎ Ezek 21:15; לִטְבֿוֺחַ‎ Psa 37:14 3t. (so Baer; van d. H לִטְבּוֺחַ‎, except לִטְב֑וֺחַ‎ Jer 51:40); Passive participle טָבוּחַ‎ Deut 28:31; —

1 slaughter, butcher animals for food Exodus 21:37 (E), Prov 9:2 (figurative of Wisdom's furnishing her table); so with accusative of congnate meaning with verb ׳ט טֶבַח‎ Gen 43:16 (J), ׳ט טִבְחָה‎ 1Sam 25:11; compare also Deut 28:31, where punishment lies in fact that the owner does not eat of the slain ox; לִטְבֿ֑וֺחַ יוּבַל אַלּוּף כְּכֶשֶׂב‎ Jer 11:19 like a mild lamb that is led to slaughter, simile of the prophet exposed to his enemies, 51:40 simile of ׳י‎s vengeance upon Babylon (see also טִבְחָה טֶבַח,‎), 25:34 of kings and rulers, under figure of shepherds & choice sheep.

2 in poetry & figurative, slay, kill ruthlessly, with accusative of person Psa 37:14; absolute Lam 2:21(|| הָרַג‎; opposed to חמל‎); with accusative of congnate meaning with verb ׳ט טֶבַח‎ Ezek 21:15 of a sword sharpened for the slaughter of Israel in judgment.
