[Heb] עָקֹב‎ ETCBC: עָקֹב‎ (subs｜something knobby) OSHL: p.eu.af, p.eu.ae TWOT: 1676c GK: H6814, H6815 Greek: βαθύς, λευκός, σκολιός
Derivation: from עָקַב‎; in the original sense, a knoll (as swelling up); in the denominative sense
Strong's: (transitive) fraudulent or (intransitive) tracked
KJV: crooked, deceitful, polluted.

H6121. aqob

I. עָקֹב‎ adjective

1 insidious, deceitful, Jer 17:9 מִכֹּל הַלֵּב עָקֹב‎.

2 foot-tracked (denominative from I. עָקֵב‎) Hosea 6:8 מִדָּם עֲֹקֻבָּה אָוֶן מֹּעֲלֵי קִרְיַת גִּלְעָד‎.

II. עָקֹב‎ adjective steep, hilly (see √; compare difficult mountain path, Qor 90:11 hill); — Isa 40:4 לְמִישׁוֺר הֶעָקֹב והיה‎ let the steep ground (Chronicles) become a plain (|| הָֽרְכָסִים‎). compare Ecclus 6:20.
