בְּהֵמֹות‎ (bᵉhêmôwth｜be-hay-mohth'｜noun｜masculine｜behemoth)
[Heb] בְּהֵמֹות‎ ETCBC: בְּהֵמֹות‎ (subs｜hippopotamus) OSHL: b.al.ac TWOT: 208b GK: H990
Derivation: in form a plural or בְּהֵמָה‎, but really a singular of Egyptian derivation;
Strong's: a water-ox, i.e. the hippopotamus or Nile-horse
KJV: Behemoth.

H930. Behemoth

בְּהֵמוֺת‎ noun masculine behemoth, i.e. hippopotamus (apparently plural intensive of foregoing; according to Di Job 40:15 compare De Isa 30:6 from an (assumed) Egyptain p-ehemau, ox of the water) Job 40:15 (on identity, compare further BoHieroz. iii. 705); probably also Psa 73:22 עִמָּ֑ךְ הָיִיתִי בְּהֵמוֺת‎ a behemoth was I with (toward) thee (so Hi De; Che beasts); according to De Or also in בַּהֲמוֺת‎ Isa 30:6 the burden of the behemoth of the south (supposed to be a designation of Egypt; but this unlikely, compare Che Di, read therefore) rather beasts of the south, namely of Judah.

בהן‎ (compare Arabic IV. shut, cover (see foregoing) whence also = בֹּהֶן‎; as closing and covering the hand, compare Lane).
