1 without article: בעל במות‎ Num 22:41 (poetry Balaam); פעור בעל‎ 25:3, 5 (E) Deut 4:3 (see below). This divine name is not used elsewhere in Hexateuch It probably originated from the sense of divine ownership, rather than sovereignty (RSSem 92). It seems to have been used in Northern Israel = אדון‎ in the South. It was the special name of the God of the Canaanites, Philistines, etc., = Babylonian בֵּל‎, compare SchrSK 1874, 335 ff. In later times scribes substituted בּשֶׁת‎, in proper name (ירבשׁת‎ = אשׁבשׁת ירבעל,‎ = אשׁבעל‎, see בּשֶׁת‎, GeiZMG 1862, 728 ff.), & also in the text for בעל‎ Hosea 9:10; Jer 11:13 (hence ἡ βάαλ 2:23; 7:9; 11:13, 17; 19:5; Hosea 2:10; 13:1 +, Rom 11:4, see DiBaal mit d. weib. Art1 Kel, MBA 1881, June 16 Dr 2Sam 4:4).

2 with article: הַבַּעַל‎ Judg 2:13; 6:25, 28, 30, 31, 32; 1Kin 16:31-32, (twice in verse); 18:19, 21, 22, 25, 26 (twice in verse); 18:40; 19:18; 1 Kings 22:54; 2Kin 3:2; 10:18-19, (twice in verse); 10:20-21, (3 t. in verse); 10:22-23, (3 t. in verse); 10:25-26, 27 (twice in verse); 10:28; 11:18 (twice in verse); 17:16; 21:3; 23:4-5, 2Chr 23:17 (twice in verse); Jer 2:8; 7:9; 11:13, 17; 12:16; 19:5 (twice in verse); 28:13; Jeremiah 28:27; 32:29; 32:35; Hosea 2:10; 13:1; Zeph 1:4.

3 הַבְּעָלִים‎ emphatic plural (compare האדונים האלהים,‎) the great lord, the sovereign owner Judg 2:11; 3:7; 8:33; 10:6, 10; 1Sam 7:4; 12:10; 1Kin 18:18; 2Chr 17:3; 24:7; 28:2; 33:3; 34:4; Jer 2:23; 9:13; Hosea 2:15; 2:19; 11:2 (or local special Ba`als, see DrSm. p. 50; pillars of Baal MV).

4 with attributive: בְּרִית בַּעַל‎ Lord of convenant Judg 8:33; 9:4 (compare ברית אל‎ 9:46; NöZMG 1888, 478); ׳ב זְבוּב‎ Lord of flies 2Kin 1:2-3, 6, 16, Philistine god, ᵐ5‎ Βααλ μυῖαν (Beelzebub, Matt 12:24) compare BaeRel 25.

II. בַּ֫עַל‎ 1. proper name, of a location city in the tribe of Simeon 1Chr 4:33 = בְּאֵר בַּעֲלַת‎.

2. proper name, masculine a. a Reubenite 1Chr 5:5;

b. a Gibeonite 1Chr 8:30; 9:36.
