H7799. shushan

or shoshan or shoshannah I. שׁוֺשָׁ֑ן שׁוּשַׁן,‎ noun masculine שׁוֺשַׁנָּה‎

noun feminine usually lily, probably any lily-like flower (TristrNHB 462 ff. PostHast. DB LILY LöwNo. 323; Late Hebrew שׁוֺשַׁנָּה‎, Arabic (Vulgar ), especially iris, Aramaic שׁוּשַׁנְתָּא‎, (PS4344), Greek σοῦσον LewyFremdw. 48; original loan-word from Egyptian sššn, šôš§n, ErmanZMG xlvi (1892), 117); — lily: in simile, כַּשּׁוֺשַׁנָּה יִפְרַח‎ Hosea 14:7 (of Israel), ׳כְּשׁוֺ הַחוֺחִים בֵּין‎ Song 2:2 (simile of bride), so construct הָעֲמָקִים שׁוֺשַׁנַּת‎ 2:1 (compare חֲבַצֶּלֶת‎); plural שׁוֺשַׁנִּים‎ 2:16; 4:5; 6:2-3, 7:3 and (figurative of lover's lips) 5:13; of flower-shaped capitals of pillars, שׁוּשַׁן מַעֲשֵׂה‎ 1Kin 7:19, ׳מ שׁוֺשָׁ֑ן‎ 7:22; compare שׁוֺשָׁ֑ן מֶּרַח‎ of brim of molten sea 7:26, || ׳פ שׁוֺשַׁנָה‎2Chr 4:5; elsewhere only Psalm-titles: עַשֹֿׁשַׁנִּים‎ Psa 45:1; 69:1, עֵדוּת עַלשֿׁוּשַׁן‎ 60:1, עֵדוּת אֶלשֿׁשַׁגִּם‎ 80:1; meaning not clear, see Bae and Commentaries al.; yet compare עֵדוּת‎, below עוד‎.
