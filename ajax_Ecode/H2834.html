חָשַׂף‎ verb strip off, strip, make bare —

Qal Perfect חָשַׂף‎ Isa 52:10, חֲשָׂפָהּ‎ Joel 1:7, חָשַׂפְתִּי‎ Jer 13:26; 49:10; Imperfect וַיֶּחֱשׂף‎ Psa 29:9; Imperative feminine singular חֶשְׂמִּיֿ‎ Isa 47:2; Infinitive absolute חָשׂף‎ Joel 1:7; construct לַחְשׂף‎ Isa 30:14; Hag 2:16; Passive participle חֲשׂוּפָה‎ Ezek 4:7, חֲשׂוּפַי‎ Isa 20:4 (plural construct ? compare below). —

1 strip off, expose oneself by removing (object שֹׁבֶל‎, flowing skirt, train) Isa 47:2 of Babylon, personified as queen (|| שׁוֺק גַּלִּי‎), ׳ח עַלמָּֿנַיִךְ שׁוּלַיִךְ‎ Jer 13:26.

2 strip, lay bare, figurative of ׳י‎ Isa 52:10 object קדשׁו זְרוֺעַ‎; of prophet, Ezek 4:7 (but Co strike out see as interpolation); object person Jer 49:10 (|| גִּלֵּיתִי‎); חשׂפהּ חָשׂף‎ Joel 1:7 of locusts stripping fig-tree; שֵׁת חֲשׂוּפַי‎ Isa 20:4 (participle either singular collective, with formative ending יַ֯‎, De LagSe i. 19 (compare 69), BN 192; or plural construct, see Ges§ 87, 1 c.); ׳ח יְעָרוֺת‎ Psa 29:9 of voice of ׳י‎ (i.e. a storm) stripping forests.

3 draw (water) Isa 30:14; (wine) Hag 2:16; properly take from the surface, skim.
