H3847. labash

or labesh לָבַשׁ לָבֵשׁ‎ verb put on (a garment), wear, clothe, be clothed (Late Hebrew id.; Aramaic לָבֵישׁ‎, Assyrian labâšu; Arabic ; Ethiopic ); —

Qal Perfect 3 masculine singularלָבֵשׁ‎ Psa 93:1 (twice in verse), לָבַשׁ‎ Job 7:5 2t., וְלָבַשׁ‎ (consecutive) Lev 6:3 3t.; suffix וּלְבָשֵׁם‎ (consecutive) 16:4; 3feminine singular לָֽבְשָׁה‎ Judg 6:34 2t., etc. (Ezek 42:14 read probablyוְלָֽבְשׁוּ‎ Qr, for ילבשׁו‎ Kt, but clause perhaps interpolated, see Co); Imperfect3masculine singular יִלְבַּשׁ‎ Deut 22:5 +; suffix וַיִּלְבָּשֵׁנִי‎ Job 29:14 יִלְבָּשָׁם‎ Exod 29:30; 1singular suffix אֶלְבָּשֶׁ֑נָּה‎ Song 5:3; 3feminine pluralתִּלְבַּשְׁןָ‎ 2Sam 13:18, etc.; Imperative masculine singularלְבַשׁ‎ 1Kin 22:30, etc.;Infinitive absolute לָבוֺשׁ‎ Hag 1:6; construct לִלְבּשׁ‎ Gen 28:20; Lev 21:10; Participle active plural הַלֹּבְשִׁים‎ Zeph 1:8; passive absolute לָבוּשׁ‎ 1Sam 17:5; Dan 10:5; לָבֻשׁ‎ Ezek 9:2-3t.; construct לְבוּשׁ‎ Dan 12:6-7, לְבֻשׁ‎ Ezek 9:11 3t.; plural construct לְבֻשֵׁי‎ 23:6 2t.; —

1. a. literally put on (one's own) garment (accusative) Gen 38:19; 1Sam 28:8; 2Sam 14:2; 1Kin 22:30 2Chr 18:29; Exod 29:30; Lev 6:3; 6:4; 16:4 (twice in verse); 16:23-24, 32; 21:10; Deut 22:5; Ezek 44:17, 19, and so 42:14 Qr (see above), Jonah 3:5; Song 5:3; Est 4:1; 5:1; with accusative garment + עַלבְּֿשָׂרוֺ‎ Lev 16:3; of putting on armour Jer 46:4; = wear (more or less habitually), with accusative of garment, Isa 4:1; Deut 22:11; Zeph 1:8; Zech 13:4; of Jerusalem under figure of woman Jer 4:30, of rulers under figure of shepherds Ezek 34:3, compare תִּלְבַּשְׁןָ כֵּן‎ 2Sam 13:18 (no accusative)

b. very often figurative, put on, be clothed with, with accusative of garment; — רִמָּה בְּשָׂרִי לָבַשׁ‎ Job 7:5 my flesh is clothed (i.e. covered) with worms; of Jerusalem, be clothed with inhabitants Isa 49:18; of pasture, with flocks Psalm 65:14; more often the garment is some abstract quality, e.g. righteousness, majesty, beauty, strength, etc.; — כַּשִּׁרְיָן צְדָקָה וַיִּלְבַּשׁ‎ 59:17 he (׳י‎) hath put on righteousness as a breastplate, compare 59:17 (vengeance), Psa 93:1 (majesty), 93:1 (strength), 104:1 (honour and majesty), Isa 51:9 (strength); also Job 40:10; said of men, 2Chr 6:41 (salvation), Psa 132:9 (righteousness), Job 29:14 (id.); of Zion Isa 52:1 (twice in verse); also in bad sense, clothed with terror, trembling, shame, etc., Ezek 7:27; 26:16; Job 8:22; Psa 35:26; 109:29, compare 35:18 (cursing).

c. literally put on, object omitted, לִלְבּשׁ נָתַןבֶּ֗֗֗גֶד‎ Gen 28:20; Job 27:17; Infinitive absolute לָבוֺשׁ‎ Hag 1:6 (there is) a clothing, but he has nothing for warmth.

d. once with בּוֺ לָבַשׁ לְבוּשׁאֲ֗֗֗שֶׁר בְּ‎ Est 6:8.

e. Passive participle, clothed with, chiefly Ezek.; — with accusative of garment, Ezek 9:2-3, Zech 3:3; Prov 31:21; Dan 10:5; of wearing armour 1Sam 17:5; construct before garment, הַבַּדִּים לְבֻשׁ‎ Ezek 9:11; 10:2, 6, 7, compare 23:6, 12; 38:4; Dan 12:6-7,.

f. figurative: — י ׳וְרוּחַ אֶתגִּֿדְע֑וֺן לָֽבְשָׁה‎ Judg 6:34 and the spirit of ׳י‎ clothed itself with Gideon, i.e. (GFM) took possession of him (compare possessed); so 1Chr 12:18; 2Chr 24:20; וַיִּלְבָּשֵׁנִי לָבַשְׁתִּי צֶדֶק‎ Job 29:14 and it clothed itself in me, as it were, became incarnate in me.

Pu`al only Participle plural מְלֻבָּשִׁים‎ arrayed, with accusative of garment 1Kin 22:10 2Chr 18:9; 5:12; absolute in full apparel Ezra 3:10.
