H2552. chamam

[חָמַם‎] verb be or become warm (Late Hebrew id., Pi`el, make warm, Aramaic , חֲמַם‎ be warm; Arabic become hot, of water; also heat, kindle fire in) —

Qal Perfect חַם‎ Psa 39:4; Exod 16:21; וְחַם‎ consecutive 1Kin 1:2; Eccl 4:11; חַמּוֺתִ֫י‎ Isa 44:16; Imperfect יָחֹם‎ 44:16; וַיָּ֫חָם‎ 2Kin 4:34; Isa 44:15; also יִחַם‎ 1Kin 1:1; יֵחַם‎ Deut 19:6; יֵחָ֑ם‎ Eccl 4:11; 3feminine singular תֵּחַם‎ Ezek 24:11; יֵחַמּוּ‎ Hosea 7:7; וַיֶּחֱמוּ‎ Gen 30:39; וַיֵּחַמְנָה‎ 30:38 (last seven forms could be also from יחם‎ q. v.; but no decisive evidence that this v is used in Qal; they are placed under חמם‎ by Thes Ew§ 193 a Ol§ 243 b; see also Köi. 365, 417 ff., who on account of יחם‎ Pi`el Infinitive 30:41; 31:10, derives last two, needlessly, from יחם‎); Infinitive לַחְמָם‎ Isa 47:14, compare Job 30:4 (where, however, ׳ל‎ is from לֶחֶם‎ Mich Di unless (Luzz Che) we read לְחַמֵּם‎ Pi`el Infinitive in both); חֹם‎ 1Sam 11:9; Hag 1:6; Neh 7:3; suffix חֻמּוֺ‎ Job 6:17; חֻמָּם‎ Jer 51:39; —

1 literally, be or grow warm Exod 16:21 (P; subject שֶׁמֶשׁ‎), compare 1Sam 11:9; Neh 7:3 and Job 6:17 (suffix of impersonal subject); from fire Isa 44:15-16, (twice in verse); 47:14 (compare above); natural heat 1Kin 1:1-2,(by personal contact), so 2Kin 4:34; Eccl 4:11 (twice in verse), from clothing Hag 1:6; of Jerusalem under figure of pot or caldron Ezek 24:11.

2 figurative, subject לִבִּי‎ Psa 39:4 (|| תִבְעַראֵֿשׁ בַהֲגִיגִי‎), compare Deut 19:6; of conspirators Hosea 7:7; Chaldeans Jer 51:39.

3 of heat in conception (animals) Gen 30:38-39, (compare יחם‎).

Niph`al Participle באלים נֵחָמִים‎ Isa 57:5 (Köi. 371), inflame oneself with, of idolatry.

Pi`el Imperfect3feminine singular תְּחַמֵּם‎ Job 39:14 keep eggs warm (of ostrich).

Hithpa`el Imperfect יִתְחַמָּם‎ warm oneself Job 31:20 כְּבָשִׂים מִגֵּז‎ by means of fleece of sheep.
