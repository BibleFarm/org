אַךְ‎159 adverb (perhaps from the same demonstr, root found also in כֵּן כֹּה, כִּי,‎).

1 surely.

2 with a restrictive force, emphasizing what follows:

a. in contrast to what precedes, howbeit;

b. in contrast with other ideas Generally, only

1 asseverative, often introducing with emphasis the expression of a truth (or supposed truth) newly perceived, especially in colloquial language, surely, no doubt (doch wohl); Gen 26:9 הִוא אִשְׁתְּךָ הִנֵּה אַךְ‎ of a surety, lo, she is thy wife ! 29:14; 44:28; Judg 3:24; 20:39; 1Sam 16:6 surely the anointed of ׳י‎ is before him ! 25:21; Jer 5:4; Psalm 58:12; Psa 73:1; 73:13; but also in other cases, though rarely, Isa 34:14-15, Zeph 3:7; Psa 23:6; 139:11; Psalm 140:14; Job 16:7; 18:21; & rather singularly Exod 12:15; 31:13; Lev 23:27, 39 (all P).

2 restrictive:

a. in contrast to what precedes, howbeit, yet, but : Gen 9:4 howbeit, flesh with the life thereof . . . ye shall not eat, 20:12; Exod 21:21; Lev 21:23; 27:26; Num 18:15, 17; 2Sam 3:13; Jer 10:24 correct me, בְּמִשְׁמָּט אַךְ‎ but with judgment, Job 2:6; 13:15; sometimes with an adversative force, as Isa 14:15; 43:24; before an imperative (minimizing the request), Gen 23:13 only, if thou wilt, I pray thee, hear me ! 27:13; Judg 10:15; 1Sam 18:17; 1Kin 17:13 and elsewhere So 1Sam 8:9 כִּי אַךְ‎ (see כִּי‎; and compare πλὴν ὅτι).

b. in contrast to other ideas Generally : — (α) Gen 7:23; 18:32 הַמַּעַם אַךְ‎ only this once (so Exod 10:17 and elsewhere) 34:15; 12:16 (note accents), 1Sam 18:8 תַמְּלוּכָה אַךְ לוֺ וְעוֺד‎ and there yet remains for him only the kingdom, 21:5; Isa 45:14 אֵל בְּךָ אַךָ‎ only in thee is God! Psa 62:2 etc. Job 14:22; Psa 37:8 fret not thyself לְהָרֵעַ אַךְ‎ (which leadeth) only to do evil, Prov 11:24 he that withholdeth more than is meet לְמַחְסוֺר אַךְ‎ (tendeth) only to penury, 14:23; 21:5; 22:16; (β) attaching itself closely to the following word (usually an adjective, rarely a verb), only, i.e. exclusively, altogether, utterly Deut 16:15 and thou shalt be שָׂמֵחַ אַךְ‎ altogether rejoicing, 28:29 (compare 28:33 רַק‎) Isa 16:7 נְכָאִים אַךְ‎ utterly stricken, 19:11; Jer 16:19 nought but lies, 32:30; Hosea 12:12; Job 19:13 זָרוּ אַךְ‎ are wholly estranged (with play on אַכְזָר‎ cruel).

c. as an adverb of time (with infinitive absolute), twice: Gen 27:30 בָּא יַעֲקֹבוְ֗֗֗עֵשָׂו יָצָא יָצאֹ אַךְ‎ only just (or scarcely) had Jacob gone out, . . . and (= when) Esau came in, Judg 7:19. — וְאַח‎ thrice : Gen 9:5 and only (second limitation of 9:3); Num 22:20 but only; Josh 22:19 but howbeit.

Note. — In some passages the affirmative and restrictive senses agree equally with the context; and authorities read the Hebrew differently. Thus only = nought but, altogether, is adopted by Ges Ew Hi De in Psa 23:6; 62:10; 73:1; 73:13; by Ew Hi De in 39:6; 39:7 (Che surely); by Ges Ew De in 39:12 (but Hi Che surely); by Ew Hi in 73:18 (De Che surely); by Ges Hi De in 139:11 (Ew doch). Isa 45:14 Ges Ew Hi Di only; but De Che of a truth.
