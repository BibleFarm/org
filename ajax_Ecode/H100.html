[Heb] אַגְמֹון‎ ETCBC: אַגְמֹון‎ (subs｜rush) OSHL: a.av.ad TWOT: 19 GK: H109 Greek: ἄνθραξ
Derivation: from the same as אֲגַם‎; a marshy pool (others from a different root, a kettle); by implication
Strong's: a bulrush (as growing there); collectively a rope of bulrushes
KJV: bulrush, caldron, hook, rush.

H100. agmon

אַגְמוֺן אַגְמֹן,‎ noun [masculine] rush, bulrush.

1 used as cord or line Job 40:26 (of twisted rushes, or spun of rush-fibre, compare Di on the passage); as fuel Job 41:12; simile of bending head Isa 58:5.

2 metaphor of the lowly, insignif. (|| כִּמָּה‎) Isa 9:13; 19:15.

אגן‎ (probably circular, round, compare Arabic ball of cheek & see Talmud אוֺגֶן‎ curved rim of a vessel).

אַגְמֹון‎
原文音譯：!Amg>a; ag-mone’
