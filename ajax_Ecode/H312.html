H312. acher

I. אַחֵר‎ adjective another (properly one coming behind), feminine אַחֶ֫רֶת‎ (with daghesh forte implicit); plural אֲחֵרִים‎ (as if from singular אָחֵר‎), once Job 31:10 אֲחֵרוֺת אֲחֵרִין,‎ (= Arabic , Sabean אאחֿר‎, Assyrian a—ru future feminine plural as substantive a—rat ûmê future of days) Gen 4:25 אַחֵר זֶרַע‎ another seed 8:10 אֲחֵרִים יָמִים שִׁבְעַת‎ seven other days Exod 22:4 אַחֵר בִּשְׂדֵה‎ in the field of another + often; אַחֵר אִישׁ‎ another man (husband) Gen 29:19; Deut 24:2; Jer 3:1; הָאַחֶרֶת חָצֵר‎ 1Kin 7:8 the other court, see חָצֵר‎; א ׳הַחוֺמָה‎2Chr 32:5 the other wall, see חוֺמָה‎. Appended to a proper name for distinction Ezra 2:31= Neh 7:34 (see 7:7 = Nehemiah 7:12) 7:33 (probably here text error, see Be Ry18: not in Ezra 2:29). Often with the collateral sense of different, as א ׳בְּגָדִים‎ other garments Lev 6:4; 1Sam 28:8; Ezek 42:14; 44:19; אֲבָנִים עָפָר,‎ Lev 14:42; רוּחַ‎ Num 14:24; אַ לְאִישׁ ׳וְנֶהְמַּכְתָּ֫‎ 1Sam 10:6; לֵב‎ 10:9; Ezek 11:19 (ᵐ5‎ Hi Sm); שֵׁם‎ Isa 65:15 (compare 62:2 חָדָשׁ‎); with that of strange, alien, as אַ ׳אִישׁ‎ Deut 20:5-6, 7; 28:30 ( so אֲחֵרִים אַחֵר,‎ alone Psa 109:8; Job 31:8, 10; Isa 65:22; Jer 6:12; 8:10 and elsewhere); עַם‎ Deut 28:32; אֶרֶץ‎ 29:27; Jer 22:26; אִשָּׁה‎ Judg 11:2; לָשׁוֺן‎ Isa 28:11 (|| שָׂפָה בְּלַעֲגֵי‎); especially in the phrase אֲחֵרִים אֱלהִים‎ other gods (63 t.) Exod 20:3 (= Deut 5:7) 23:13 (both J E) Josh 24:2, 16 (E) 1Sam 26:19; Hosea 3:1, & particularly in Deuteronomy (Hosea 6:14; Deut 8:19 15t.) & Deuteronomic writers, as Josh 23:16; Judg 2:12, 17, 19 Jeremiah (18 t.) & compiler of Kings; אַחֵר אֵל‎ once Exod 34:14 (JE). So אַחֵר‎ alone Isa 42:8; Psa 16:4. Of time, following, next (rare) הָאַ ׳בַּשָּׁנָה‎ Gen 17:21 (P) in the next year; 2Kin 6:29 הָאַ ׳בַּיּוֺם‎; אַ ׳דּוֺר‎ Joel 1:3; Psa 109:13 in poetry the next Generation (Judg 2:10 in prose = another Generation).
