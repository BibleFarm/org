the‎, He, fifth letter; = numeral 5 in post-Biblical Hebrew; no evidence of this usage in OT times.

הֶ הָ, הַ, , הַּ‎ (on the use of these different forms, see the Grammars: e.g. Ges§ 35),

definite article, the (so Moabite, Phoenician (Schröd§ 62-4), Liµyan (Northwest Arabia) ha (DHMEpigr. Denkm. 4. 13. 58 ff.); not Assyrian Aramaic or Ethiopic: Arabic , of which, before dentals, sibilants, and liquids, the l is written but not pronounced, thus pronounced 'ash-shamsu = Hebrew הַשֶּׁמֶשׁ‎) — in General the use of the article in Hebrew is analogous to its use in Greek or German: but naturally there are applications peculiar to Hebrew (compare with what follows Ges§ 126 Ew§ 277): —

1 joined with substantives:

a. to mark a definite concrete object, as Gen 1:1 the heavens and the earth, הַמֶּלֶךְ‎ the king, etc. Never, however (as in Greek e.g. ὁ Πλάτων), before true proper names, though it is used with certain terms, chiefly geographical, of which the original appellative sense has not been lost, as הַבַּעַל‎ 'Baal,' literally 'the lord,' in plural הַבְּעָלִים‎ i.e. the various local Baals, הַשָּׂטָן‎ the Adversary Job 1:6ff. (as a proper name שָׂטָן‎, 'Satan,' only 1Chr 21:1); הַלְּבָנוֺן‎ (but not הַחֶרְמוֺן‎), הַיַּרְדֵּן‎ (but not הָאַרְנֹן‎), הַשָּׁרוֺן הַכַּרְמֶל,‎ the Sharon, הַשְּׁפֵלָה‎ the (Judaean) lowland, הַמִּישׁוֺר הַכִּכָּר,‎ the (Moabite) table-land, הָעַי הָעֲרָבָה,‎ '`Ai,' הַגִּבְעָה‎ 'Gibeah,' Judg 20:5ff., הַגָּלִיל‎ 'Galilee,' הַגִּלְעָד הַבָּשָׁן, הַגִּלְבֹּעַ, הַגִּלְגָּל,‎ (often), הַמִּסְגָּה הָעֹפֶל, הַיְשִׁימוֺן, הָרָמָה, הַמִּצְמָּה, הַשִּׁטִּים,‎.

b. with an adjective to denote one who exhibits a quality κατ’ ἐξοχήν, i.e. to express the comparative or superlative degree: so often, as Gen 1:16 הגדל המאור‎ the greater light, הקטן המאור‎ the lesser light, 27:1 הגדול בנו‎ = his elder son, 48:14 הצעיר‎ the younger, 42:13 הַקָּטֹן‎ the little one, i.e. the youngest, (of Joseph's brethren), Lev 21:10 מֵאֶחָיו הַגָּדוֺל‎ the chiefest of his brethren, Num 35:25 + הגדול הכהן‎ the chief priest, ב ׳הצעיר‎ the least among Judg 6:15, מן הצעירה‎ the least of 1Sam 9:21, ב ׳הַיָּפָה‎ the fairest among Song 1:8; 2Kin 10:3; Prov 30:30, compare Job 14:15.

c. with nouns which are not definite in themselves, but acquire their definition from the context, or from the manner in which they are introduced: thus (α) in the standing phrases הַיּוֺם‎ to-day, Gen 4:14; 21:26 + often; הַלַּיְלָה‎ to-night, 19:5; 30:15 +, once 1Sam 15:16 last night; so הַשָּׁנָה‎ this year, 2Kin 19:29; Jer 28:16; הַמַּעַם‎ this time, Gen 18:32 +. (β) הַנָּהָר‎ the river (κατ’ ἐξοχήν), i.e. the Euphrates; Exod 2:15 the well, the well namely of the district, Josh 8:11 the valley, 1Sam 17:3; 19:10 the wall, 19:13 the bed, 20:21 הַנַּעַר‎ the lad (whom Jonathan would naturally take with him), 20:34 the table. Hence occas. where a suffix would define the noun more precisely, as הַחֲמוֺר‎ 2Sam 19:27 + = my ass, Judg 3:20; 1Sam 1:9 הַכִּסֵּא‎ = his seat, Judg 4:15; 1Kin 22:35; 2Kin 10:15 + הַמֶּרְכָּבָה‎, 1Sam 18:10; 20:33 הַחֲנִית‎.

d. it is a peculiarity of Hebrew thought to conceive an object as defined by its being taken for a particular purpose, and thus by a kind of prolepsis to prefix the article to the noun denoting it: 1Sam 10:1 and Samuel took הַשֶּׁמֶן אֶתמַּֿךְ‎ literally the cruse of oil, not, however, a cruse which had been defined previously, but one rendered definiteby being now taken; in English idiom 'a cruse of oil,' 10:25 בַּסֵּפֶר‎ literally in the scroll or book, the one, namely taken for the purpose, i.e. in a scroll (so Exod 17:14; Num 5:23; Job 19:23), 21:10 בַּשִּׂמְלָה‎, Judg 4:18 בַּשְּׂמִיכָה וַתְּכַסֵּהוּ‎, 4:19; 7:13 הָאֹהֶל‎ a tent, 8:25; 9:48 אֶתהַֿקַּרְדֻּמֹּת‎ hatchets, 20:16 every one able to sling אֶלהַֿשַּׂעֲרָה בָּאֶבֶן‎ with a stone at a hair, 1Sam 6:8 בָּאַרְגָּז‎ (unless indeed the אַרְגָּז‎ was an understood appendage in every cart), Num 11:27 הַנַּעַר‎, a young man, 13:23 בַּמּוֺט‎ on a pole, Josh 2:15 בַּחֶבֶל‎ with a cord, 2Sam 17:17 השׁפחה‎ a girl (compare Dr1Sam 1:4; 19:13). Sometimes it is uncertain whether an article is to be referred to c or

d: e.g. 1Sam 2:13 his prong or a prong, 2Sam 18:9 his mule or a mule, etc.

e. with nouns that denote objects or classes of objects that are known to all, as הַמַּיִם הַזָּהָב, הַצּאֹן,‎; Gen 13:2 Abram was very rich וּבַזָּהָב בַּכֶּסֶף בַּמִּקְנֶה‎, Exod 31:4 וּבַכֶּסֶף בַּזָּהָב לַעֲשׂוֺת‎, Deut 14:26 and thou shalt lay out the money וּבַשֵּׁכָר וּבַיַּיִן וּבַצּאֹן בַּבָּקָר‎, 2Kin 9:30 עֵינֶיהָ בַּמּוּךְ וַתָּשֶׂם‎, in French 'elle mit du fard à ses yeux.' It is, however, remarkable that this usage depends mostly on the punctuation, הַלֶּחֶם הַיַּיִן, הַכֶּסֶף,‎ etc. (except as applied to denote definite quantities of gold, wine, etc., as Josh 6:24) being far less common than יַיִן כֶּסֶף,‎ etc., but לַכֶּסֶף בַּכֶּסֶף,‎ etc. being much more frequently than לְכֶסֶף בְּכֶסֶף,‎ etc.: for instances in which the article forms part of the consonantal text, see Gen 6:20; 7:8 + העוף‎ and הבהמה‎, Deut 8:3 הַלָּחֶם‎, 1Kin 5:8; 5:13; Isa 28:7 הַיַּיִן‎ and הַשֵּׁכָר‎, 60:17; Ezek 15:4, 7 הָאֵשׁ‎, Hab 2:5; Prov 20:1; Psalm 65:14 הַצּאֹן‎, Song 1:11; Eccl 7:12. compare below,

f. in comparisons, the object compared being, as a rule, not an individual as such, but one exhibiting the characteristics of a class: Isa 1:18 כַּתּוֺלָע כַּשָּׁנִים,‎ like scarlet, like crimson (both meant Generally), 5:24 יִהְיֶה כַּמַּק‎, 5:28 כַּצַּר‎ and כַּסּוּפָה‎, 10:14 כַּקֵּן‎, 13:8 כַּיּוֺלֵדָה‎ (as always with this word, e.g. 42:14; Psa 48:7), 11:7 כַּבָּקָר‎; + often (The usage is not, however, quite uniform, at least according to the punctuation: there occurs e.g. כְּקַשׁ‎ Job 41:21; Isa 47:14; כְּאַרְיֵה‎ Psa 7:3 and elsewhere: and we find both כַּלָּבִיא‎) Isa 5:29 and כְּלָבִיא‎ Hosea 13:8; כָּאֲרִי‎ Isa 38:13 and כַּאֲרִי‎ Num 24:9; כַּגִּבּוֺר‎ Isa 42:13 and כְּגבור‎ Job 16:14; etc.) Similarly Gen 19:28 הַכִּבְשָׁן כְּקִיטֹר‎ as the smoke of a furnace, Num 11:8, 12 אֶתהַֿיֹּנֵק הָאֹמֵן יִשָּׂא כַּאֲשֶׁר‎, Deut 28:29 (הָעִוֵּר‎), Judg 8:18b הַמֶּלֶךְ בְּנֵי כְּתֹאַר‎, 14:6 הַגְּדִי כְּשַׁסַּע‎ like (a lion's) rending a kid, 16:9; 1Kin 14:15; Zech 12:10 הַיָּחִיד עַל מִסְמֵּד כְּ‎ as mourning over an only child, 13:9 +. Where, however, the standard of comparison is not the class in General, but only a particular part of it, defined by a special epithet (whether adjective or verb), the article is naturally omitted: thus כַּמֹּץ‎ like chaff (in General) Isa 41:15, but עֹבֵר כְּמֹץ‎ like chaff passing away 29:5; כַּצֵּל‎ Job 14:2, but נָטוּי כְּצֵל‎ Psa 102:12; כַּקַּשׁ‎ Isa 40:24, but נִדָּף כְּקַשׁ‎ 41:2; כָּאַיָּל‎ 35:6, but, וג על תַּעֲרֹג ׳כְּאַיָּל‎ like a hind (that) longeth for streams of water Psa 42:2; כָּאֵשׁ‎ Isa 9:17, but יער תבער כְּאֵשׁ‎ like fire (that) kindleth a wood Psa 83:15; כֶעָשָׁן‎ Isa 51:6, but מֵאֲרֻבָּה כְּעָשָׁן‎ like smoke from a chimney Hosea 13:3: so Isa 62:1b Job 9:26; 11:16 עָֽבְרוּ כְּמַיִם‎ like waters (that) have passed by, etc.; Deut 32:2 a כַּטַּל כַּמָּטָר,‎, but 32:2 b עֲלֵיעֵֿשֶׂב כִּרְבִיבִים עֲלֵידֶֿשֶׁא, כִּשְׂעִירִים‎. Where the article is found, although a rel. clause follows (as Psa 1:4; 49:13; Isa 61:10), this is probably to be regarded not as limiting the class of object compared, but as describing it.

g. prefixed to generic nouns (in the singular) it designates the class, i.e. it imparts to the noun a collective force, as Exod 1:22 כָּלהַֿבֵּן‎ all (literally the whole of) the sons, כָּלהַֿבַּת‎ all the daughters, Lev 17:8, 10 מִןהַֿגֵּר‎ of the strangers, who sojourn in their midst, Num 21:7 הַגנָּחָשׁ‎ the serpents; Gen 14:13; Ezek 24:26; 33:21 הַמָּלִיט‎ those who escaped; Josh 6:7 + הֶחָלוּץ‎, v 9:13 הַמְאַסֵּף‎; 8:19 הָאוֺרֵב‎; 1Sam 13:17 הַמַּשְׁחִית‎; Micah 2:13; Isa 6:4 הַקּוֺרֵא‎ the choir of criers; 1Sam 24:14 הַקַּדְמֹנִי‎ the ancients; Eccl 7:26 אֶתהַֿאִשָּׁה‎ woman, and often with Gentile names, as הַיְבוּסִי‎ the Jebusites, וְהַמְּלֵתִי הַכְּרֵתִי‎ 2Sam 8:18 and elsewhere, הַדָּנִי‎ Judg 18:1 etc., הַלֵּוִי‎ the Levites, Num 3:20, 18:23; Mal 2:8; Psa 135:20 +.

h. with nouns denoting abstract ideas, especially the names of moral qualities (compare Greek ἡ δίκη, French la justice), chiefly in two cases — (α) where the article is recognizable in the consonantal text, exceptionally, when some emphasis or definiteness is intended, as Deut 7:9 והחסד הברית שׁומר‎; הזה החסד‎ 2Sam 2:5; 1Kin 3:6; והרחמים החסד את‎ Jer 16:5 (contr. Zech 7:9); הצדק‎ Isa 1:26; 61:3; Eccl 3:16; הַצְּדָקָה‎ Isa 32:17 (twice in verse) (contrast 32:16) Dan 9:7 (emphatic); הַדַּעַת‎ Hosea 4:6; הָאֱמֶת‎ Gen 32:11 (followed by אֲשֶׁר‎), Isa 59:15 (contrast 59:14) Zech 8:3, 19; הָאֱמוּנָה‎ Isa 11:5b (contr. va) Jer 7:28; הַחָכְמָה‎ Job 28:12, 20; הַחַיִּים‎ Gen 2:9 +; הָאַהֲבָה‎ Song 2:7; 3:5; 8:4, 7; הַשֶּׁקֶר‎ Jer 7:4, 8; 23:26; הַמָּוֶת‎ Isa 25:8; Ruth 1:17 +; הָרֶשַׁע‎ Psa 125:3; Eccl 3:16; הָרִשְׁעָה‎ Zech 5:8; הַחשֶׁךְ‎ Isa 60:2; Eccl 2:13; see also Deut 30:15, 19 (Jer 21:8), 1Kin 7:14; Jer 32:19; Mal 2:5 והשׁלום החיים‎, Psa 123:4; 130:4; Dan 9:9; 1Chr 29:11; 2Chr 1:12; Prov 31:30; Eccl 2:18, 17; 7:12, 19; 10:6; 11:10: but in all such cases אֱמֶת צֶדֶק, חֶסֶד,‎, etc. are far more common. (β) where the article depends on the punctuation, after prepositions, especially בְּ‎, but with much irregularity, as בַּחֶסֶד‎ Isa 16:5; Prov 20:28 (but ׳בְּח‎ Hosea 2:21; Prov 16:6), בַּצֶּדֶק‎ 25:5 (elsewhere always ׃בְּצ ׳בִּצְדָקָה‎ also always); בֶּאֱמֶת‎ and בֶּאֱמוּנָה‎ always; בַּשֶּׁקֶר‎ Isa 28:15 (but || כָּזָב‎, not הַכָּזָב‎) Jer 5:31; 13:25; 20:6; 23:14 (בְּשֶׁקֶר‎ 3:10; 29:9); בַּשָּׁלוֺם‎ Psa 29:11; Job 15:21 (elsewhere בְּשָׁלוֺם‎); Zech 12:4 to smite וּבַעִוָּרוֺן בַּשִּׁגָּעוֺן‎ (but Deut 28:28 וּבְעורון בְּשׁגעון‎), compare Gen 19:11; Deut 28:22; to enter with one בַּמִּשְׁמָּט‎ Job 9:32; 22:4 (but בְּמשׁפט‎ Psa 143:2), contrast also Prov 18:5; with 24:23; לַמִּשְׁמָּט‎ Isa 59:11; Psa 9:8 (but ׳לְמ‎ Isa 5:7; Psa 122:5); Prov 2:2; 7:4 לַחָכְמָה‎, (but never הַחכמה‎ in Proverbs 1-9, or indeed in the whole book); 2:3; 7:4 לַבִּינָה‎, Job 39:17 בַּבִּינָה‎ (but never הַבִּינָה‎); Prov 2:2-3,לַתְּבוּנָה‎ (but הַתְּבוּנָה‎ only 1Kin 7:14 emphatic); to perish בַּצָּמָא‎ Judg 15:18 +; Isa 29:21 בַּתֹּהוּ‎, 32:19 הָעִיר תִּשְׁמַּל בַּשִּׁמְּלָה‎ German in die Niedrigkeit sinkt die Stadt, 45:16 together they go בַכְּלִמָּה‎ (in die Schmach), 46:2 בַּשְּׁבִי‎ into captivity, 47:5 בַחשֶׁךְ בֹּאִי‎ (so always: never בְחשׁך‎). The living language may have used the article more readily after a preposition, where it did not lengthen the word by an entire syll.; still the disparity of usage between α and β makes it not improbable that the article in β is in many cases not original but due to the punctuators.

i. to mark the vocative: 1Sam 17:55 הַמֶּלֶךְ חֵינֿפשׁך‎ as thy soul liveth, O king, I do not know, 17:58 הַנַּעַר אתה מי בן‎ Whose son art thou, lad ? 2Sam 14:4 Help, O king ! 1Kin 18:26 עֲנֵנוּ הַבַּעַל‎, 2Kin 9:5 הַשָּׂר אֵלֶיךָ‎, Isa 42:18 שְׁמָ֑עוּ הֵַחרְשִׁים‎, Jer 2:31 וג רְאוּ אַתֶּם ׳הַדּֿוֺר‎, Ezek 37:4 הַיְּבֵשׁוֺת הָעֲצָמוֺת‎ O dry bones, Mal 3:9 כֻּלּוֺ הַגּוֺי‎ (Dr§ 198 Obs. 2).

b. (rare) when the substantive qualified by the adjective or participle was felt to be sufficiently definite for its own article to be dispensed with, as sometimes with the word day Gen 1:31 השׁשׁי יום‎, 2:3 השׁביעי יום‎, Exod 12:15, 18; 20:10 (= Deut 5:14) Lev 19:6; 22:27: so with מָנוֺא‎ entrance Jer 38:14, שַׁעַר‎ Zech 14:10; חָצֵר‎ court 1Kin 7:8, 12; Ezek 40:28 and elsewhere; also in certain phrases (peculiar to P) where the substantive is defined by כל‎, as Gen 1:21 הַחַיָּה כָּלנֶֿפֶשׁ‎ all living souls, 1:28 הָרֹמֶשֶׂת כָּלחַֿיָּה‎ all living things that creep, etc., 7:21; 9:10; Lev 11:10, 46; further in isolated cases, hardly reducible to rule, 24:10; 1Sam 12:23 הַטּוֺבָה דֶּרֶךְ‎, 16:23 הָרָעָה רוּחַ‎, 2Sam 12:4 העשׁיר לְאישׁ‎ Jer 6:20; 17:2; Zech 4:7; Psa 104:18; Ezra 10:9 הַתְּשִׁיעִי חֹדֶשׁ‎ (quite exceptional in OT). (With pronouns this use is so rare that, where it occurs, it is dubious if the text be sound: 1Sam 17:12, 17; Jer 40:3 Kt Micah 7:11). And with the participle: 1Sam 25:10 הַמִּתְמָּֽרְצִים עֲבָדִים רַבִּים‎ many are the slaves who break away etc., Isa 7:20; Jer 27:3; 46:16 = 50:16 הַיּוֺנָה חֶרֶב‎ the oppressing sword, Ezek 2:3 (but ᵐ5‎ Co omit הגוים‎), 14:22; 32:22, 24; Psa 62:4; Prov 26:18; Judg 21:19 (very anomalous, read probably לַמְּסִלָּה‎). This usage is somewhat more frequently in the later parts of OT; and in Post-Biblical Hebrew it is very General (e.g. הָרַע יֵצֶד‎ the evil inclination): see further Dr§ 209.

c. with the participle, where the participle with the article forms really the subject: Gen 2:11 הַסּוֺבֵב הוּא‎ not 'it was encompassing,' but 'it is that which encompassed,' 45:12 הַמְדַבֵּר מִּי‎ my mouth is that which speaketh, Deut 3:21 הָראֹוֺת עֵינֶיךָ‎ thine eyes were those which saw, 4:3; 8:18 ὅτι οὗτος ἐστιν ὁ διδοὺς σοί, Isa 14:27; 66:9 (see Dr§ 135. 7).

3 The article is prefixed exceptionally — mostly in the latest Hebrew — with the force of a relative to the verb: Josh 10:24 אִתּוֺ הֶהָֽלְכוּא‎ that went with him, Ezek 26:17, הַהֻ֫לָּלָה הָעִיר‎, 1Chr 26:28 שְׁמוּאֵל הַהִקְדִּישׁ וְכֹל‎ and all that Samuel had dedicated, 29:8, 17; 2Chr 1:4 ַבּהֵכִין‎ in (the place) that he had prepared, 29:36; Ezra 8:25; 10:14, 17. According to the punctuation, it occurs similarly elsewhere, as Gen 18:21 הַשָבּאָה‎ (so 46:27; Job 2:11), 21:3 הַנּוֺלַדלֿוֺ‎, 1Kin 11:9 & Dan 8:1 הַנִּרְאָה‎, Isa 51:10 הַשָּׂ֨מָה֨‎, 56:3 הַנִּלְוָה‎, Ruth 1:22; 2:6; 4:3 (all הַשָּׁ֫בָה‎): but in all these passages, the change of a point, or even sometimes of an accent, would restore the normal participial construction (as לוֺ הַנּוֺלָד הַבָּשָאה,‎ compare Gen 48:5, הַנִּרְאֶה‎), which is, no doubt, what was intended by the original writers, and is recognised elsewhere by the Masora, e.g. 12:7; 35:1 הַנִּרְאֶה‎, 46:26 & Ruth 4:11 הַבָּאָ֣ה‎ (compare Ew§ 331 b Ges138, 3 b). Once, still more anomalously, before a preposition 1Sam 9:24 וְהֶעָלֶיהָ‎ (as though καὶ τὸ ἐπ’ αὐτῆς): but read probably וְהָאַלְיָה‎ and the fat tail, see Dr. (In Arabic also occurs, though very rarely, as a relative: WAG i. § CG 117). — On the anomalous use of the article with a word in the stative construct, see Grammars, as Ew§ 290 d, Ges§ 127 R. 4, also Dr§ 190-1.
