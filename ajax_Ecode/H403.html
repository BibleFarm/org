H403. aken

אָכֵן‎ (perhaps from אַח‎; compare Aramaic דֵּח‎ & דִּכֵּן‎)

adverb with strong asseverative force:

a. surely, truly, especially at beginning of a speech (stronger & more decided than אַח‎) Gen 28:16; Exod 2:14; 1Sam 15:32; Isa 40:7; 45:15; Jer 3:23 (twice in verse); 4:10; 8:8. In 1Kin 11:2 אָבֵן‎ stands unusually; and מֶּןֿ‎ (compare ᵑ7 ᵑ6 ᵐ5‎) should probably be read (so Klo).

b. emphasizing a contrast, but indeed, but in fact, especially after אָמַרְתִּי‎ I said or thought, expressing the reality, in opposed to what had been wrongly imagined, Isa 49:4b (opposed to va) 53:4 (opposed to 53:3 end) Jer 3:20 (opposed to the expectation 3:19 b) Zeph 3:7b Psa 31:23b (opposed to 31:23a) 66:19; 82:7 (opposed to 82:6) Job 32:8 (opposed to 32:7).
