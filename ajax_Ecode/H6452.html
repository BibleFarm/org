פָּסַח‎ (pâçach｜paw-sakh'｜verb｜limp｜pass over)
[Heb] פָּסַח‎ ETCBC: פסח‎ (verb｜be lame) OSHL: q.by.aa, q.bz.aa TWOT: 1786 1787 GK: H7173, H7174 Greek: παρέρχομαι, περιποιέομαι
Derivation: a primitive root;
Strong's: to hop, i.e. (figuratively) skip over (or spare); by implication, to hesitate; also (literally) to limp, to dance
KJV: halt, become lame, leap, pass over.

H6452. pacach

I. מָּסַח‎ verb pass or spring over; —

Qal Perfect3masculine singular ׳פ‎ Exod 12:27, ׳וּפ‎ consecutive V:23 (J); 1 singular וּפָסַחְתִּ֫י‎ 12:13 (P), all with עַל‎, of ׳י‎ passing over (sparing) houses of Israel; Infinitive absolute מָּסוֺחַ‎ Isa 31:5 a passing over (SC. עַליְֿרוּשָׁלַםִ‎).

II. [מָּסַח‎] verb limp (Thes below I. ׳פ‎ but dubious; Arabic is dislocate; according to Gerber73 II. ׳פ‎ is denominative from מִּסֵּחַ‎ [and this below I. ׳פ‎?]); —

Qal Participle plural הַסְּעַמִּים עַלשְֿׁתֵּי מֹּסְחִים‎ 1Kin 18:21 figurative (see [ סְעִמָּה‎]).

Niph`al Imperfect3masculine singular וַיִּמָּסֵחַ וַיִּמֹּל‎ 2Sam 4:4 and he fell and was made limping (lame).

Pi`el Imperfect3masculine plural עַלהַֿמִּזְבֵּחַ וַיְפַסְּחוּ‎ 1Kin 18:26 they went limping (along by the altar), i.e. probably in their dance, see Kit PietschmPhön. 220 (Gr ויפסעו‎ [ = וַיִּפְשְׂעוּ‎] stepped).
