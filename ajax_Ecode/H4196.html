1 J E narrate that altars were built by Noah after leaving the ark Gen 8:20; by Abraham at Shechem 12:7, Bethel 12:8, Hebron 13:18, mountain in land Moriah 22:9; by Issac at Beersheba 26:25; by Jacob at Shechem 33:20 (yet this perhaps mistake for מַצֵּבָה‎, being object of וַיַּצֶּבשָֿׁם‎, not elsewhere with מזבח‎, compare also Di), at Bethel 35:7, by Moses at Rephidim Exod 17:15, Horeb 24:4, by Balak at Bamoth Baal, Pisgah, & Peor Num 23:1, 14, 29; by Joshua on Mt. Ebal Josh 8:30: the prophetic histories narrate that altars were built by Gideon at Ophra Judg 6:24; by a man of God at Bethel 21:4; by Samuel at Ramah 1Sam 7:17; by Saul after Michmash 14:35; by David on the threshing floor of Ornan 2Sam 24:25 = 1Chr 21:18; 22:1; that Solomon sacrificed on the altar at Gibeon 1Kin 3:4 and built altars in the temple at Jerusalem 6:20; 8:64; that Jeroboam built an altar at Bethel 12:32 (which was destroyed by Josiah 2Kin 23:15); and that Elijah repaired an ancient altar on Carmel 1Kin 18:30. An altar in Egypt is predicted Isa 19:19. All this accords with the law of the Covenant code Exod 20:24-26 which recognises a plurality of altars and prescribes that they shall be built of אֲדָמָה‎, or of unhewn stones, and without steps. So אֲבָנִים‎ Deut 27:6 (JE), שְׁלֵמוֺת אֲבָנִים‎ Josh 8:31, and twelve stones 1Kin 18:30, 32; compare Isa 27:9. The altar was also a place of refuge Exod 21:24 (JE) 1Kin 1:50-51, 2:28.

2 D prescribes one central altar Deut 12:5f. 27; but no attempt to enforce this principle appears before Hezekiah(2Kin 18:4, 22), and Josiah, whose reform is more effectual, 23:8-20.

3 P limits sacrifices to the altars of the tabernacle. A great altar was built east of the Jordan, but it was according to P only as an עֵד‎ after the pattern of the altar before the Tabernacle Josh 22:10-34. P describes two altars:

a. the הָעֹלָה מִזְבַּח‎ Exod 30:28; 31:9; 35:16; 38:1; 40:6, 10, 29; Lev 4:7, 10, 25 (twice in verse); 4:30, 34 = ׳מ הַנְּחשֶׁת‎ Exod 38:30; 39:39, made of acacia wood plated with brass, 5*5*3 cubits in size, with four horns and a network of brass, on which all sacrifices by fire were made 27:1; Exodus 8; 38:1-7;

b. ׳מ קְטֹרֶת מִקְטַר‎ made of acacia wood plated with gold, 1*1*2 cubits in size, with four horns and a crown of gold Exod 30:1-6 = ׳מ הקטרת‎ 30:27; 31:8; 35:15; 37:25 = ׳מ הזהב‎ 39:38; 40:5, 26; Num 4:11 = ׳מ הַסַּמִּים קטרת‎ Lev 4:7; these altars are named elsewhere only in Chronicles: 1Chr 6:34; 16:40; 21:29; 2Chr 1:5-6,.

4 Solomon made two altars for the temple at Jerusalem:

a. ׳מ הַנְּחשֶׁת‎ for the court 1Kin 8:64 (which was laid aside by Ahaz 2Kin 16:14-15, who made an altar after the model of one he saw in Damascus 16:11); in 16:14 הַנּ ׳הַמִּזְבַּח‎ (construct form with article) is erroneous, read probably מִזְבַּח‎ compare Ges§ 127. 4 a near the end; this altar, according to Chronicles, measured 20*20*10 cubits 2Chr 4:1; this (ה)נחשׁת מזבח‎ also 7:7 & Ezek 9:2; = ׳מ העולה‎2Chr 29:18, repaired by Asa 15:8;

b. an altar of cedar, plated with gold, in the temple before the דְּבִיר‎ 1Kin 6:20, 22; 7:48; = ׳מ הזהב‎2Chr 4:19; = ׳מ הַקְּטֹרֶת‎ 1Chr 28:18; 2Chr 26:16.

5 Ezekiel plans two altars for the new temple:

a. a table of wood 3*2 cubits Ezek 41:22;

b. an altar for the court, probably same dimensions as 4a, with stairs Ezek 43:13-17.

6 after the return Jeshua built an altar on the ancient site in the court of the temple Ezra 3:2.

7 besides these altars used in the worship of Yahweh, Ahab built an altar to Baal in Samaria 1Kin 16:32; Ahaz made an altar on the roof of the upper chamber, and other kings of Judah made altars in the two courts, all of which were destroyed by Josiah 2Kin 23:12.

9 the dedication of the altar was המזבח חֲנֻכַּת‎ Num 7:10-11, 84, 88; 2Chr 7:9; it was anointed with oil מָשַׁח‎ Exod 40:10; Lev 8:11; Num 7:10 (all P).

12 the horns of the altar were especially for the application of the blood of the sin-offering in the ritual; sin is represented as graven מִזְבְּחוֺתֵיכֶם לְקַרְנוֺת‎ Jer 17:1; therefore of blood for cleansing it was said המ קרנות על ׳נָתַן‎ Exod 29:12; Lev 4:7, 18, 25, 30, 34; 8:15; 9:9; 16:18 (all P). An ancient custom is referred to, Psa 118:27 המזבח עַדקֿרנות בַּעֲבֹתִ֑ים אִסְרוחַֿג‎ bind the festal victim with cords, unto the horns of the altar: Ainsworth paraphrases: 'that is, all the court over, untill you come even to the hornes of the altar, intending hereby many sacrifices,' so De; but Che 'bind the procession with branches, (step on) to the altar-horns,' that is in sacred procession round the altar.

13 the יְסוֺד‎ (q. v.) is also referred to in the ritual of the sin-offerings in the phrases יסוד אל יָצַק‎ Lev 8:15; 9:9 & יסוד אל שָׁפַךְ‎ Exod 29:12; Lev 4:7, 18, 25, 30, 34 (all P) יסוד אל יִמָּצֵה‎ 5:9.

14 the altar was קָדָשִׁים קֹדֶשׁ‎ Exod 29:37; 40:10 (P); but repeated sin-offerings were necessary to keep it pure and cleanse it from the pollution of the people in whose midst it was situated.
