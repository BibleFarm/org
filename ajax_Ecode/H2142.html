a. remember past experiences (accusative) Gen 42:9 (E), 2Kin 9:25 (accusative of person agent); followed by clause with מִי‎ Job 4:7; things formerly known (accusative) Jer 17:2; followed by clause with כִּי‎ Judg 9:2; with implied regret, longing Num 11:5 (JE), Psa 42:5; 137:1; Lam 1:7 compare Psa 77:7, negative 137:6 (|| שׁכח‎ 137:5); so (followed by בְּ‎) Jer 3:16 (|| לב על עלה‎ and פקד‎).

b. recall past distress, etc.; object not expressed, Lam 3:20 (זכר זכור‎), Job 11:16 (שׁכח‎ in || clause); negative, followed by accusative Isa 54:4 (|| שׁכח‎), Prov 31:7 (|| id.).

c. remember sins, (1) to repent of them Deut 9:7 (+ אלתֿשׁכח‎, followed by clause with אתאֿשׁר‎), elsewhere only Ezek., usually with accusative Ezek 16:61 (+ וְנִכְלַמְתְּ‎), 16:63 (object not expressed), 20:43; 36:21; (2) to renew and repeat them 23:19, negative 23:27.

a. to their advantage: — followed by accusative Gen 40:14 (E), 40:23 (E; negative, || שׁכח‎), 1Sam 25:31.

b. to make use of them (accusative) Nahum 2:6.

c. their acts (accusative) to their advantage 2Chr 24:22, to their disadvantage 2Sam 19:20 (negative); to make vengeance Deut 25:17.

d. remember human obligations, accusative of thing: אַחִים בְּרִית‎ Amos 1:9; compare perhaps Est 2:1 (accusative of person and also of deed); negative Eccl 9:15 (accusative of person), Psa 109:16 (followed by עשׂות חסד‎).
