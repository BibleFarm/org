Derivation: (Aramaic) from the same as נָדָן‎;
Strong's: a sheath; figuratively, the body (as the receptacle of the soul)
KJV: body.

H5085. nidneh

[גין‎] with בְּגִין בְּ,‎

preposition on account of (so Galilean Aramaic, D§ 47, 3), read perhaps in Dan 7:15: see נִדְגֵה‎.

נִדְנֵ֑ה‎ (Masora Baer) noun [masculine] sheath (לְדָן נְדָן, ᵑ7‎; see Biblical Hebrew (late) נָדָן‎, Persian loan-word); — ׳בְּ֗֗֗נ‎ Dan 7:15 my spirit in (its) sheath, i.e. my body; < נִדְנָה‎ emphatic, or suffix נִדְנַהּ‎ (K§ 54, 3. β)); or (NöGGA. 1884, 1022 Bev) נְדָנַהּ‎; but expression at best strange; read probably דְּנָה בְּגִין‎ on account of this (בְּגִין‎ as ᵑ7‎J; D§ 47. 3 (2nd ed. 47. 10)), M72* compare Buhl Dr.
