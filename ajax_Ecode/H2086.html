זֵד‎ adjective insolent, presumptuous: — ׳ז‎ Prov 21:24; plural זֵדִים‎ Jer 43:2 11t.; — always masculine, and always of opposition to ׳י‎, wickedness; with qualified noun expressed, only הַזֵּדִים הָאֲנָשִׁים‎ 43:2, of opponents of Jeremiah; elsewhere used substantively, as technical term for godless, rebellious men; once singular שְׁמוֺ לֵץ יָהִיר זֵד‎ Prov 21:24 a haughty insolent one, scorner is his name (|| זָדוֺן בְּעֶבְרַת עוֺשֶׂה‎); usually plural Isa 13:11(ז ׳גְּאוֺן‎; || עָרִיצִים גַּאֲוַת‎); Mal 3:13 (|| רִשְׁעָה עשֵֹׁי‎), Malachi 4:19 (|| עשֵֹׁה רִשְׁעָה‎); and in Psa 86:14 (|| עָרִיצִים‎), 119:21(|| הַשֹּׁגִים מִמִּצְוֺתֶיךָ‎), 119:51; 119:69; 119:78; 119:85; 119:122; so probably also 19:14 עַבְדֶּ֑ךָ חֲשׂךְ מִזֵּדִים גַּם‎ also from presumptuous men hold back thy servant (Ew Ol Hup Che RVm); > presumptuous sins Deal.
