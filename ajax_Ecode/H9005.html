ו‎, Waw, sixth letter; in modern Hebrew = numeral 6; no evidence of this usage in OT times.

וָ וּ, וְ,‎

demonstrative adverb and

conjunction so, then, and (Phoenician, Moabite, Aramaic ו‎; Arabic ; Ethiopic Assyrian u DlW (but between verbs the usually connective is the enclitic ma KAT2. 508, Dl§§ 82, 150). The use in Hebrew shews that original וְ‎ was not a merely copulative conjunction, but that it possessed a demonstrative force (compare Dr§ 122), which is often perceptible as such, though in other cases, from its being applied to denote mere juxtaposition or connection [compare Greek τε, and Latin tum .... tum = 'both .... and,' both properly demonstratives: Assyrian ma has also a demonstrative force, Dl§ 79], it is less palpable) — וְ‎ before shwa' mobile or a labial is vocalized וּ‎; often before a tone-syll., when the word itself has a disj. accent, וָ‎ (as וַָמתִּי׃‎ Gen 19:19; וָמֵ֖תוּ‎ 33:13; וָמֵ֑ת‎ 44:9; וָחָֽיָה׃‎ Exod 1:16; וָיָ֑מָּה‎ Josh 15:46; וָ֔לֹ֕א‎ 2Sam 13:26; וָאָ֖רֶץ‎ Isa 26:19; וָקָ֔מוּ‎ 49:7; וָחָֽתָּה׃‎ Jer 48:1; וָרָֹע‎ Psa 10:15); especially when it forms with the preceding word a pair, whether of parallel or opposed ideas (as וָכֹה כֹּה‎ Exod 2:12, וָמִ֖י מִי‎ 10:8, וָאָ֑בֶן אֶבֶן‎ Deut 25:13; וָדֹ֑ר דֹּר‎ 32:7 +; וָבֹ֔הוּ תֹּהוּ‎ Gen 1:2, וָרֶמֶ֛שׂ בְּהֵמָה‎ 1:24, וָרָֽע׃ טוֺב‎ 2:19, וָנָ֖ד נָע‎ 4:12, וָלָ֖יְלָה וְיוֺם וָחֹ֛רֶף וְקַיִץ‎ 8:22, וֵָלךְ׃ קַח‎ 12:19, וָאָֽרֶץ׃ שָׁמַיִם‎ 14:19, וָאָ֑תָּה אֲנִי‎ 31:44, וָה֑וּא אֲנִי‎ 41:11, וָשׁוּבוּ עִבְרוּ‎ Exod 32:27, וָהֵ֛ם אַתָּה‎ Num 16:16, וָמֶ֑לָךְ אֱלֹהִים‎ 1Kin 21:13, וָנֶ֖כֶד נִין‎ Isa 14:22, וָשֶׁ֖בֶר שֹׁד‎ 59:7; and often: see more fully Bö§ 600); occasionally also with a conjunctive accent (as לְעֹלָֽם׃ וָשַחי‎ Gen 3:22 compare Lev 18:5, שָׁם וָמַ֣תְנוּ‎ 2Kin 7:4, חֲדָשָׁ֑ה וָאָ֣רֶץ‎ Isa 65:17; Prov 25:3, גָּדוֺל׃ וָשֶשׁבֶר שֹׁ֖ד‎ Jer 48:3, compare 32:29). — וְ‎ is used very freely and widely in Hebrew, but also with much delicacy, to express relations and shades of meaning which Western languages would usually indicate by distinct particles. But in Hebrew particles such as לָכֵן לְמַעַן, בַּעֲבוּר, אוּלָם, אָכֵן, אַךְ, אָז, אוֺ,‎, etc., were reserved for cases in which special emphasis or distinctness was desired: their frequent use was felt instinctively to be inconsistent with the lightness and grace of movement which the Hebrew ear loved; and thus in AV, RV, words like or, then, but, notwithstanding, howbeit, so, thus, therefore, that, constantly appear, where the Hebrew has simply וְ‎.

Josh 7:24 (10 t.) 2Sam 17:28f.: often however it is prefixed only to the last, as Gen 5:32; 10:1; Deut 18:10 etc.; occas. even it connects only the first two, 1Kin 8:47; Isa 1:13; Psa 45:9; Job 42:9. Remarkably, however, וְ‎ as a mere conjunction is, as a rule, not in classical Hebrew attached directly to verbs (especially in the perfect), the construction with וְ‎ consecutive (see

2) being (as in Moabite) preferred: thus וַיִּבֶן יָצָא‎ is said, not וּבָנָה יָצָא‎. Exceptions in classical Hebrew are (a) sometimes where synonyms are coupled, as Num 23:19 וְדִבֶּר ֗֗֗ אָמַר‎, Deut 2:30; Judg 5:26; 1Sam 12:2; Isa 1:2 ורוממתי גדּלתי‎ 2:11; 5:14; 8:8; 19:6; 29:20; (b) isolated cases, difficult to reduce to rule (perhaps sometimes due to textual error), as Gen 28:6; 38:5; Judg 3:23; 7:13; 16:18; 1Sam 1:12; 3:13; 4:19; 5:7; 10:9; 17:38, 48; 25:20 and elsewhere (in other passages, apparently similar, the perfect and וְ‎ has a frequent. force (see 2), as Gen 37:3; Exod 36:29f.; Num 10:17f. 21:15, 20; 1Sam 2:22; 16:14; 17:34f. (see Dr) 27:9; 2Sam 16:13; 17:17, perhaps also Gen 15:6; 21:25; 34:5). In later parts of OT, probably through Aramaic influence, the perfect with simple וְ‎ occurs more frequently: so especially in Ecclesiastes, where it is all but universal (e.g. 2:11-12, 13, 15 etc.). With the imperfect, the simple וְ‎ is not so unidiomatic, even in classical Hebrew (compare 3); see 1:9, 26; 9:27; 17:2; 22:17; 27:29; Exod 23:8 (frequently) 24:7; Num 14:12; Deut 17:13; 30:12-13, Josh 3:13; 7:3; Judg 7:3; 13:8; Isa 5:29; 14:10; often also in Jeremiah, Isa2, Job, Psalms, e.g. 40:30; 41:20 (Dr§ 116: contrast 28:13). See more fully Dr§ 130-4 Ges§ 112. 6. Special senses: —

a. it sometimes = and specially, Gen 3:16; 1Kin 11:1 many strange women, and specially the daughter of Pharaoh, Isa 1:1 Judah and (= and particularly) Jerusalem, 2:1; 9:7; Psa 18:1.

b. and in particular ('und zwar'), and that (explicative), Gen 4:4; Judg 7:22; 1Sam 17:40 וּבַיַּלְקוּט‎, 28:3 in Ramah, and that in his city (unusual: text suspicious), 2Sam 13:20 וְשֹׁמֵמָה‎ and that desolate, Isa 57:11 I have been silent וּמֵעוֺלָם‎ and that from of old, 32:7; Jer 15:13; Amos 3:11 (si vera lectio), 4:10; Zech 9:9b Mal 1:11; Psa 68:10; Prov 3:12; Lam 3:26; Eccl 8:2; Dan 1:3; 8:10; 9:25, 27; Neh 8:13; 1Chr 9:27; 2Chr 8:13; 29:27 (but' even' for ו‎, before ל‎ and infinitive, e.g. in Isa 44:28; Jer 17:10; 19:12, is wrong; see Ew§ 351 c, Dr§ 206).

c. sometimes it introduces an idea which so exceeds or adds to what has preceded, that it is nearly equivalent to also, 1Sam 25:43; 1Kin 2:22 ask also the kingdom for him; unusually Hosea 8:6 והוא מישׂראל כי‎ for of Israel is it also, Eccl 5:6 והבלים‎ (see De) 2Chr 27:5 (but see ᵐ5‎). Or it may be rendered yea: so especially in the ascending numerations 3-4 Amos 1:3, 6, 9, 11, 13; 2:1, 4, 6; Prov 30:18, 21, 29, 6-7 6:16; Job 5:19, 7-8 Micah 5:4 — the first number being aggravated, or augmented, by a higher. In one idiom וּגְבוּל‎, occurring in geographic descriptions, it is used peculiarly, seemingly = at the same time: Num 34:6 and as for the western border, וּגְבוּל הַגָּדוֺל הַיָּם לָכֶם וְהָיָה‎ the great sea shall be to you also (German 'zugleich') a border, Deut 3:16 וּגְבוּל הַנַּחַל תּוֺךְ‎ the middle of the stream being at the same time the border, 3:17; Josh 13:23, 27; 15:12, 47 (but these, except Num 34:6, might be cases of 5c γ).

d. it connects alternative cases, so that it = or: Exod 20:10, 17; 21:16 he that stealeth a man בְיָדוֺ וְנִמְצָא וּמְכָרוֺ‎ and selleth him, and (= or if) he be found in his hand, 21:17; Lev 21:14; 22:23-24, Prov 29:9 (Fl Ew RV: whether . . . or) Job 31:13, 16, 26, etc.

e. it connects contrasted ideas, where in our idiom the contrast would be expressed explicitly by but; in such cases prominence is usually given to the contrasted idea by its being placed immediately after the conjunction: Gen 2:17 but of the tree of the knowledge . . . thou shalt not eat, 4:2, 5; 6:8; 17:21; 31:10 etc., 1Kin 2:26; 10:7; 11:32, 34; 15:14; Prov 10:1-2, 3, 4 + often; even after לֹא‎ (where כִּי‎ or אִם כִּי‎ might be expected), as Gen 42:10; Exod 21:18; Lev 26:45; Deut 11:11; Judg 19:12; 1Kin 3:11.

f. it introduces a contrasted idea in such a way as to suggest a question, especially before a pronoun, Judg 14:16 I told it not to my father or my mother, אַגִּיד וְלָךְ‎ and shall I told it unto thee? 2Sam 11:11; Jer 25:29 תִנָּקוּ הִנָּקֵה וְאַתֶּם‎ and shall ye be guiltless? 45:5; Ezek 20:31; 33:25b Jonah 4:11. So the וְ‎ consecutive and perfect (see 2a), Exod 5:5 וְהִשְׁבַּתֶּם‎ and will ye make them rest from their burdens? Num 16:10 וּבִקַּשְׁתֶּם‎ and seek ye the priesthood also? 1Sam 25:11; Isa 66:9; Psa 50:21 and shall I keep silence? Job 32:16 (compare Dr§ 119 γ).

g. attaching a fresh subj. (or object) to a clause already grammatically complete, it = and also, Gen 2:9b Num 16:2, 18 and they stood at the entrance of the tent of meeting, ואהרן ומשׁה‎ and Moses and Aaron (stood also), 16:27; Exod 35:22; Judg 6:5; Gen 1:16; b 12:17; 44:2; 46:15; Exod 29:3; Jer 32:29 (compare DrSm. i. 6, 11, & p. 293): when the idea thus attached is subordinate, or not logically embraced in the principal predicate, it approximates to the Arabic or 'waw of association' (followed by an accusative: WAG ii. § 37), Est 4:16 אָצוּם וְנַעֲרֹתַי אֲנִי‎ I will fast (singular) and (= with) my maidens, Exod 21:4; 1Sam 25:42; 29:10b (but insert here אַתָּה‎ with ᵐ5‎) 2Sam 12:30 (but read וּבָהּ‎, as 1Chr 20:2) 1Sam 20:10; Neh 6:12; Gen 4:20; Isa 42:5; Jer 19:1 (but read מזקני וְלָקַחְתָּ֫‎ with ᵐ5‎) 2Chr 2:3; 13:11; compare Jer 22:7 (וְכֵלָיו אִישׁ‎), Job 41:12. Whether Isa 48:16 וְרוּחוֺ‎ belongs here, is dubious

k. in circumstantial clauses וְ‎ introduces a statement of the concomitant conditions under which the action denoted by the principal verb takes place: in such cases, the relation expressed by וְ‎ must often in English be stated explicitly by a conjunction, as when, since, seeing, though, etc., as occasion may require. So very often, as Gen 11:4 let us build a tower בַשָּׁמַיִם וְראֹשׁוֺ‎ and its top in the heavens (= with its top in etc.), 18:12 shall I have pleasure, זָקֵן וַאדֹנִי‎ and my lord is old (= my lord being old)? (The analogous use of the is very common in Arabic: Wii. § 183.) 2 The ו‎ consecutive (formerly called the Waw 'conversive'); —

a. with the

imperfect (וַּ‎ with following daghesh; before וָ א,‎), as וַיּאֹמֶר‎ properly = and he proceeded to say, chiefly in contin. of a preceding perfect tense (so Moabite);

b. with the

perfect (וְ‎, — in 1 & 2 singular, the tone, with certain exceptions [see Dr§ 110], being thrown forward to the ultima), as וְיָָֽשַׁבְתָּ֫‎ properly so [namely, as limited by a verb, or other term, preceding] hadst thou sat, chiefly in continuation of a preceding imperfect tense, in its various senses of future, jussive, or frequentative. Thefurther analysis of these idioms belongs to the grammar; see on a DrCh. vi., Ges§ 111, and on

b DrCh. viii. Ges§ 112. Here it must suffice to note — (a) ו‎ consecutive (especially with the imperfect) frequently couples two verbs in such a manner that the first, indicating the general character of the action, receives its closer definition in the second: in such cases, the first may often be represented in English by an adverb, as Gen 26:18 וַיַּחְמֹּר וַיָּשָׁב‎ and he turned and dug = and he dug again; so often; and similarly with הוֺסִיף‎ 25:1, מִהַר‎ 24:18, הוֺאִיל‎ Josh 7:7, etc.; more exceptionally, Gen 30:27; Est 8:6, and (not consecutive) Gen 47:6; Job 23:3; Song 2:3; Lam 3:26 (see Ges§ 120, 2 a; compare in Syriac Nö§ 335-6. In Arabic the stronger form of the conjugation here corresponds: Wii. § 140). (b) וַּ ‎ with the imperfect sometimes expresses a contrast = and yet, Gen 32:31 I have seen God face to face וַתִּנָּצֵל‎ and yet my soul is delivered, Deut 4:33; 2Sam 3:8; 19:29; Isa 51:12; Psa 73:14; 144:3; Job 10:8 + (compare Dr§ 74 β). (c) there is a tendency in the later books of the OT to use the perfect with simple וְ‎, where the classical language would employ the imperfect with וַּ ‎ (compare above

1); so especially in Ecclesiastes, where וַּ ‎ occurs thrice only, Job 1:17; 4:1, 7. (d) a double perfect with ו‎ consecutive is sometimes used, informally but neatly, with a hypothetical force; thus (a) in past or present time Exod 16:21 וְנָמָֽס׃ הַשֶּׁמֶשׁ וְחַם‎ and the sun used to be warm, and it used to melt = and if (or when) the sun was warm, it melted, 33:10; 1Kin 18:10; Jer 18:4, 8 וְנִחַמְתִּ֫י וְֹשָׁב‎ = and if it turns, I repent, 20:9 (see RV); (β) in future time, Gen 44:22 ומת אביו ועזב‎ and he will leave his father, and he will die = and if he leaves his father, he will die, 33:13; 42:38; Exod 4:14; 12:13; 1Sam 16:2; 19:3; Ezek 33:3; 39:15 etc. (Dr§ 147-9, Ges§ 159. 2 e).

3 With a

a. וְ‎ consecutive and the Perfect — in answer to אִם‎ or כִּי‎ constantly, as Exod 19:5; 23:22 if thou hearkenest to my voice, וְאָֽיַבְתִּ֫י‎ then am I (= I will be) enemy to thy enemies (see Dr§ 136-8); after the casus pendens, 12:44 every servant that is bought for money, וּמַלְתָּ֫ה‎ thou shalt circumcise him, etc. Num 14:31; 24:24; 1Sam 25:27; 2Sam 14:10 he that spake unto thee, אֵלַי וַהֲבֵאתוֺ‎ so hast thou brought him (= thou shalt bring him) unto me, Isa 9:4; 56:6f. +; Exod 12:15; 31:14; Deut 17:12 +; after various time-determinations, as Gen 3:5 in the day of your eating from it וְנִפְקְחוּ‎ then shall your eyes be opened, Exod 16:6 וִידַעְתֶּם עֶרֶב‎ at even, then shall ye know, 16:7; 32:34; 2Sam 15:10; 1Kin 13:31 אֹתִי וּקְבַרְתֶּם בְּמוֺתִי‎ when I die, so ye shall bury me, etc., + often; after conjs., as כִּי‎ Gen 29:15, יַעַן‎ 1Kin 20:28; Isa 3:16f., תַּחַת‎ 60:15, etc., (see Dr§ 123).

b. וַּ ‎ consecutive and the imperfect (but much less frequently than the perfect), as Gen 30:30 the little that thou hadst, וַיִּפְרֹץ‎ it hath increased, Exod 9:21; 2Sam 4:10; 1Kin 9:20f. 15:13 +; after time-determinations, Gen 22:4 on the third day וַיִּשָּׂא‎ then Abraham lifted up his eyes, 19:15; 27:34; 37:18; 1Sam 6:6; 12:8; Isa 6:1; Hosea 11:1 when Israel was a child, וָאֹהֲבֵהוּ‎ then I loved him, +; occasionally after conjunctions, as כַּאֲשֶׁר‎ Exod 16:34, יַעַן‎ 1Sam 15:23, לְמַעַן‎ Isa 45:4, אִם‎ Psa 59:16 (Dr§ 127).

c. in other cases (not 'consecutive') — all more or less uncommon: namely a. וְ‎ closely joined to the imperfect Gen 13:9 if to the left, וְאֵימִינָה‎ then I will take the right (compare 2Sam 12:8) Exod 12:3 on the 10th of the month וְיִקְחוּ‎ then let them take, Num 16:5; Isa 43:4; Hosea 4:6; 10:10; Psa 69:33; Job 15:17; 2Sam 22:41; Prov 23:24 Kt (Dr§ 125); β. separated from the verb, Exod 8:22; Lev 7:16 יֵאָכֵל וְהַנּוֺתָר וּמִמָּחֳרָת‎ and on the morrow, then the remainder shall be eaten, Josh 3:3; 1Kin 8:32; Isa 8:7; 48:7; Job 14:7; 20:18; b 23:12; 25:5; 32:14 +; γ. without a verb, Gen 40:9 וְהִנֵּה בַּחֲלֹמִי‎ in my dream, behold, a vine was before me, 40:16; 2Sam 23:3f.; Prov 10:25, and the extreme cases 2Sam 15:34 thy father's slave, מֵאָז וַאֲנִי‎ I was that of old, — but now עַבְדְּךָ וַאֲנִי‎ well, I will be thine, Isa 34:12 (si vera lectio), Job 4:6b דְּרָכֶיךָ וְתֹם תִּקְוָָֽתְךָ‎ thy confidence — it is the uprightness of thy ways, 36:26 חֵקֶר וְלֹא שָׁנָיו מִסְמַּר‎ Ges. seine Jahre, da ist kein Zählen (compare Dr§ 124-5, 128-9). — With 4, 5 compare the Arabic , Wi. § 366 b, 367 c; ii. § 187; Dr§ 185 end.
