נֶ֫פֶשׁ‎756 noun feminineGen 49:6 (so even 2:19; Num 31:28; 1Kin 19:2 see AlbrZAW xvi (1896), 42 SS) soul, living being, life, self, person, desire, appetite, emotion, and passion (Ecclus 3:18; 4:1 (twice in verse); Eccl 4:2(twice in verse); Ecclesiasticus 13:12; Ecclesiasticus 14:11); — ׳נ‎ Gen 1:20 +; נָ֑פֶשׁ‎ 37:21 +; suffix נַפְשִׁי‎ 12:13 +; plural נְפָשׁוֺת‎ Ezek 13:18 13t.; נְפָשֹׁת‎ Exod 12:4; Lev 27:2; construct נַפְשׁוֺת‎ Gen 36:6 4t.; נַפְשֹׁת‎ Lev 21:11; נְפָשִׁים‎ Ezek 13:20 (but read חָפְשִׁים‎, see Co Berthol Toy); suffix נַפְשׁוֺתָם‎ 2Sam 23:17 +; נַפְשֹׁתָם‎ Num 17:3 +: —

1 = that which breathes, the breathing substance or being = ψυχή, anima, the soul, the inner being of man:

a. distinguished from בָּשָׂר וְעַד מִנֶּפֶשׁ ׃בָּשָׂר‎ Isa 10:18; הַבָּשָׂר עִם הַנֶּפֶשׁ‎ Deut 12:23; from שְׁאֵר‎ Prov 11:17; from בֶּטֶן‎ body Psa 31:10.

b. both the inner נפשׁ‎ and the outer בשׂר‎ are conceived as resting on a common substratum: תֶּאֱבָֽל׃ עָלָיו וְנַפְשׁוֺ יִכְאָ֑ב עָלָיו בְּשָׂרוֺ אַךְ‎ Job 14:22 only his flesh upon him is in pain, and his soul upon him mourneth; compare Psa 42:5; 42:7; 131:2; Job 30:16; Lam 3:20 [see עַל‎

1d], all poetical (compare 6c).

c. ׳נ‎ departs at death and returns with life: מֵתָה כִּי נַפְשָׁהּ בְּצֵאת וַיְתִי‎ Gen 35:18 (E) and it came to pass when her soul was going forth (for she died); נַפְשָׁהּ נָָֽפְחָה‎ Jer 15:9 she breathed out her soul, compare 1Kin 17:21-22, Job 11:20; 31:39.

d. often desired that the נפשׁ‎ may be delivered: from Sh®°ôl Psa 16:10; 30:4; 49:16; 86:13; 89:49; Prov 23:14; from שַׁחַת‎, the pit of Sh®°ôl, Isa 38:17; Job 33:18, 22, 28, 30.

2 The נפשׁ‎ becomes a living being: by God's breathing חיים נשׁמת‎ into the nostrils of its בשׂר‎; of man Gen 2:7 (J); by implication of animals also 2:19 (J); so Psa 104:29; 104:30; compare 66:9; man is חַיָּה נֶפֶשׁ‎, a living, breathing being Gen 2:7 (J); elsewhere חיּה נפשׁ‎ always of animals 1:20, 24, 30; 9:12, 15, 16 (all P), Ezek 47:9; so החיּה נפשׁ‎ Gen 1:21; 9:10 (both P), Lev 11:10, 46 (H); השׁרצת נפשׁ‎ 11:46 (H); חי כל נפשׁ‎ Job 12:10. נפשׁ‎ is frequently used with the verb ׃חיה‎ נפשׁ וחיתה‎ Gen 12:13; 19:20 (both J), 1Kin 20:32 (E), Psa 119:175; Jer 38:17, 20; נַפְשְׁךָ חֵי‎ 1Sam 1:26; 17:55; 20:3; 25:26; 2Sam 11:11; 14:9; 2Kin 2:2, 4, 6; 4:30 (all J E); compare נ ׳יְחַיֶּה‎ 1Kin 20:31 (E), Ezek 18:27; Psa 22:30; also Gen 19:19; Isa 55:3; Prov 3:22.

3 The נפשׁ‎ (without חיה‎ noun or verb) is specified:

a. a living being whose life resides in the blood [so in Arabic WeSkizzen iii. 217 G. JacobArab. Dicht. iv. 9 f.] (hence sacrificial use of blood, and its prohib. in other uses; first in D), Deut 12:23-24, only be sure that thou eat not the blood, for the blood is the living being (הַנֶּפֶשׁ הוּא הַדָּם‎); and thou shalt not eat the living being with the flesh (הַבָּשָׂר עִם הַנֶּפֶשׁ‎); thou shalt pour it upon the earth as water; this is enlarged in H, Lev 17:10-11, 12, 14 and in P Gen 9:4-5, compare Jer 2:34.

b. a serious attack upon the life is an attack upon this inner living being 2Sam 1:9; Jer 4:10; Jonah 2:6; Psa 69:2; 124:4; 124:5; Job 27:3.

c. נפשׁ‎ is used for life itself 171 t., of animals Prov 12:10, and of man Gen 44:30 (J); נפשׁ תחת נפשׁ‎ life for life Exod 21:23 (E), Lev 24:18 (H), 1Kin 20:39, 42; 2Kin 10:24; תחתיכם נפשׁנו‎ Josh 2:14 (J); בנפשׁ נפשׁ‎ Deut 19:21; בנפשׁ‎ for the life of 2Sam 14:7; Jonah 1:14; בכף נפשׁ שׂם‎ put life in one's own hand Judg 12:3; 1Sam 19:5; 28:21; Job 13:14; למות נפשׁו חרף‎ Judg 5:18 risked his life to die; בנפשׁ‎ at the risk of life Num 17:3 (P), 2Sam 23:17 = 1Chr 11:19 (twice in verse), 1Kin 2:23; Prov 7:23; Lam 5:9; נפשׁ בקּשׁ‎ Exod 4:19 (J), 1Sam 20:21; 22:23 (twice in verse); 23:15; 25:29; 2Sam 4:8; 16:11; 1Kin 19:10, 14; Jer 4:30; 11:21; 19:7, 9; 21:7; 22:25; 34:20-21, 38:16; 44:30 (twice in verse); 46:26; 49:37; Psa 35:4; 38:13; 40:15; 54:5; 63:10; 70:3; 86:14; Prov 29:10; נ ׳שׁאל‎ 1Kin 3:11 2Chr 1:11; 1Kin 19:4 = Jonah 4:8; נפשׁ הִכָּה‎ smite mortally Gen 37:21 (J), Deut 19:6, 11; Jer 40:14-15, נ ׳לקח‎ 1Kin 19:4; Jonah 4:3; Psa 31:14; Prov 1:19; מִמָּוֶת נֶפֶשׁ הִצִּיל‎ deliver life from death Josh 2:13; Psa 33:19; Psalm 56:14; נפשׁ מלּט‎1Sam 19:11; 2Sam 19:6 (4 t. in verse); 1Kin 1:12 (twice in verse); Jer 48:6; 51:6, 45; Ezek 33:5; Amos 2:14-15, Psa 89:49; 116:4; ׳נ פדה‎ 2Sam 4:9; 1Kin 1:29; Psalm 34:23; Psa 49:16; 55:19; 71:23; נ ׳שׁמר‎ 25:20; 97:10; Job 2:6; Prov 13:3; 16:17; 19:16; 22:5.

4 The נפשׁ‎ as the essential of man stands for the man himself:

a. paraphrase for personal pronoun especially in poetry and ornate discourse, 70 t.; (1) נַפְשִׁי‎ = me: נפשׁי תבא אל‎ Gen 49:6 let me not enter (poem in J); נ ׳תָּמֹת ישׁרים מות‎ Num 23:10 let me die, etc. (poem); נַפְשְׁי תָּמוֺת‎ Judg 16:30 (J); נפשׁי אמרה‎ Lam 3:24 I say. (2) נַפְשְׁךָ‎ = thee: נַפְשֶׁ֑ךָ תַּחַת לְאֻמִּים‎ Isa 43:4 peoples instead of thee; לְנַפְשֵׁךְ אָֽמְרוּ‎ 51:23. (3) נַפְשׁוֺ‎ = he: תלין בטוב נפשׁו‎ Psalms he will not dwell in good circumstances. (4) נַפְשֵׁנוּ‎ = we: יוֺקְשִׁים מִמַּח נִמְלְטָה כְּצִמּוֺר נַפְשֵׁנוּ‎ Psa 124:7. (5) נַפְשָׁם‎ = they, them: הָלָ֑כָה בַּשְּׁבִי נַפְשָׁם‎ Isa 46:2 they are gone into captivity; לנפשׁם אוי‎ 3:9.

b. = reflexive, self, 53 t.: נפשׁ על אסר‎ bind oneself Num 30:3; 30:5 (twice in verse); 30:6; 30:7; 30:8; 30:9; 30:10; 30:11; 30:12; נ ׳לְעַנֹּת‎ 30:14 to afflict oneself . (1) נַפְשִׁי‎ = myself: נפשׁי אדע לא‎ Job 9:21 I know not myself. (2) נַפְשְׁךָ‎ = thyself: נ ׳שׁמר‎ Deut 4:9 keep thyself. (3) נַפְשׁוֺ‎ = himself: כנ ׳אהב‎ 1Sam 18:1, 3; 20:17 loved as himself. (4) נַפְשָׁהּ‎ = herself: נ ׳צדּקה‎ Jer 3:11 justified herself. (5) נַפְשָׁם‎ = themselves: נ ׳הציל‎ deliver themselves Isa 47:14; Ezek 14:14, 20. (6) נַפְשֹׁתֵיכֶם נַפְשְׁכֶם,‎ = yourselves: נ ׳אַלתַּֿשִּׁאוּ‎ Jer 37:9 deceive not yourselves, also 42:20; 44:7; נ ׳ענּה‎ Lev 16:29, 31; 23:27, 32; Num 29:7 (P); לנ ׳נשׁמרתם‎ Deut 4:15; Josh 23:11 (D). (7) נַפְשׁוֺתֵינוּ‎ = ourselves: נ ׳על‎ Jer 26:19 against ourselves.

c. = person of man, individual, 144 t., first in D2; especially in H, P, and kindred writers: (1) with אָדָם‎: ׳נ אדם‎ Lev 24:17 opposed to ׳נ בהמה‎ 24:18 (both H), and so ׳נ אדם‎ human persons Num 31:35, 40, 46 (P) 1Chr 5:21; Ezek 27:13. Elsewhere without אדם‎: ׳נ ברכה‎ Prov 11:25 one who blesses; ׳נ רְמִיָּה‎ 19:15 idle person; ׳נ נ ׳תחת‎ person in place of person, Job 16:4; נ ׳על כמּר‎ Exod 30:15-16, Num 15:28; 31:50 (all P) Lev 17:11 (H). (2) נפשׁ‎ = person, any one: Deut 24:7; 27:25; Prov 28:17; Ezek 18:4 (3 t. in verse); 33:6; elsewhere only H P: Lev 2:1; 4:2, 27; 5:1-2, 4, 15, 17; Leviticus 5:21; 7:18, 20, 21, 25, 27; 23:29-30, (twice in verse); Num 5:6; 15:27, 30; 19:22; 31:19, 28; 35:11, 15, 30 (twice in verse); Josh 20:3, 9 (all P), Lev 17:10, 12, 15; 20:6 (twice in verse); 22:6, 11 (all H); מן הַהִוא הנ ׳נכרתה‎ that person shall be cut off from: only in Gen 17:14; Exod 12:15, 19; 31:14; Lev 7:20-21, 27; Num 9:13; 15:30-31, 19:13, 20 (all P), Lev 19:8; 22:3 (both H). (3) נפשׁ‎ collective for persons, in enumerations: Deut 10:22; Josh 10:28, 30, 32, 35, 37 (twice in verse); 10:39; 11:11 (all D2) Jer 43:6; 52:29-30, (twice in verse); Ezek 22:25; elsewhere only Gen 12:5; 46:15, 18, 22, 25, 26 (twice in verse); 46:27 (twice in verse); Exod 1:5 (twice in verse); 12:16; Num 31:35, 40 (all P). (4) נְפָשׁוֺת‎ persons Gen 36:6; Exod 12:4; 16:16; Num 19:18 (all P), Lev 18:29; 20:25; 27:2 (all H), 2Kin 12:5; Prov 11:30; 14:25; Ezek 13:18 (3 t. in verse); 13:19 (twice in verse); 13:20 (twice in verse); 17:17; 18:4; 22:27 (נפשׁים‎ 13:20 see above). (5) נפשׁ‎ = deceased person, sometimes with מֵת נֶפֶשׁ מֵת,‎ Num 6:6 (P), מֵת נַפְשֹׁת‎ (נֶפֶשׁ ᵑ6 ᵐ5‎) Lev 21:11 (H); usually without (ה)אדם נפשׁ מֵת,‎ Num 9:6-7, 19:11, 13 (P); or simply נפשׁ‎, Lev 19:28; 21:1; 22:4 (all H) Num 5:2; 6:11; 9:10 (all P); elsewhere only Hag 2:13.

5 נפשׁ‎ = seat of the appetites, in all periods (46 t.) —

a. hunger: ׳נ רְעֵבָה‎ hungry soul Psa 107:9; Prov 27:7; with noun or verb of שׂבע‎ satisfy Isa 56:11; 58:10; Jer 50:19; Ezek 7:19; Psa 63:6; 107:9; Prov 13:25; 27:7; לנ ׳מתוק‎ 16:24 sweet to the taste.

b. thirst: ׳נ עֲיֵפָה‎ weary soul Prov 25:25; Jer 31:25; ׳נ עיפה כארץ‎ Psa 143:6; נ ׳צמאה‎ 42:3; 63:2.

c. appetite in General: בנ ׳אֹיְבַי עָלָיו יַקִּיפוּ‎ Psa 17:9 my enemies compass me about with greed; פִיהָ וּפָֽעֲרָה נַפְשָׁהּ שְׁאוֺל הִרְחִיבָה‎ Isa 5:14 Sh®°ôl enlarged her appetite, etc., compare Hab 2:5; Prov 23:2 נֶפֶשׁ בַּעַל‎; Ecclesiastes uses נפשׁ‎ only in the sense of a, b, c; the נפשׁ‎ craves, lacks, and is filled with good things: Eccl 2:24; 4:8; 6:2-3, 7, 9; 7:28.

6 = ׳נ‎ seat of emotions and passions (151 t.) —

a. desire: נ ׳אִוְּתָה‎ soul desires Deut 12:20; 14:26; 1Sam 2:16; 2Sam 3:21; 1Kin 11:37; Job 23:13; Prov 13:4; 21:10; Micah 7:1; נ ׳תַּאֲוַת‎ Psa 10:3; Isa 26:8; נ ׳אַוַּת‎ Deut 12:15, 20, 21; 18:6; 1Sam 23:20; Jer 2:24; so also לנפשׁ‎ according to one's desire Deut 21:14; Psa 78:18; Jer 34:16; כנפשׁ‎ Deut 23:25; נפשׁנו האח‎ ah, our desire Psa 35:25; בנפשׁ‎ at one's desire 105:22; Ezek 16:27; בְדַבְּרוֺ ׳נ יָָֽצְאָה‎ Song 5:6; נ ׳נשׂא‎ lift up the soul, desire Deut 24:15; 2Sam 14:14 (read אליהם‎, not אלהים‎), Psa 24:4; 25:1; 86:4; 143:8; Prov 19:8; Jer 22:27; 44:14; Hosea 4:8.

b. abhorrence, loathing: נ ׳געלה‎ soul abhorreth Lev 26:11, 15, 30, 43 (H) Jer 14:19; נ ׳וגם בי בחלה‎ Zech 11:8 and their soul also fell a loathing against me.

c. sorrow and distress: נ ׳מרי‎ bitter, gloomy, discontented of soul Judg 18:25 (E) 2Sam 17:8; Job 3:20; Prov 31:6; נ ׳מר‎ 1Sam 22:2 compare Job 7:11; 10:1; Isa 38:15; Ezek 27:1; נ ׳עָֽגְמָה‎ Job 30:35 grieved; נ ׳אגמי‎ sad of soul Isa 19:10; נ ׳תבכה‎ my soul shall weep Jer 13:17; ׳נ ירעה‎ his soul trembleth Isa 15:4; נ ׳צרת‎ distress of soul Gen 42:21 (E).

d. joy: ׳נ תגיל‎ my soul rejoiceth Psa 35:9; Isa 61:10; נ ׳שׂמּח‎ rejoice the soul Psa 86:4; also 94:19; 138:3; Prov 29:17.

e. love: נ ׳אהבה‎ my soul loveth Song 1:7; 3:1-2, 3, 4; נ ׳יְדִדוּת‎ darling of my soul Jer 12:7; נ ׳דבקה ׳ב‎ his soul clave unto Gen 34:3 (J), with אחרי‎ Psa 63:9; ׳נ ׳ב חשׁקה‎ soul is attached to Gen 34:8 (P).

f. alienation, hatred, revenge: נ ׳תֵּקַע מן‎ the soul is alienated from Jer 6:8; Ezek 23:17-18, נ ׳נקעה מן‎ 23:18, 22, 28; נ ׳שְׂנֻאי‎ hated of soul 2Sam 5:8 (Qr); נ ׳שׂנאה‎ Psa 11:5; Isa 1:14.

g. other emotions and feelings: נ ׳השׁיב‎ bring back soul Lam 1:11, 19 (i.e. revive, compare with שׁוּב‎ 1Kin 17:21f.), hence figurative refresh, cheer, 17:16; Psa 19:8; Prov 25:14; Ruth 4:15; נ ׳שׁובב‎ Psa 23:3; נ ׳מַחְמַל‎ your souls' compassion Ezek 24:21; נ ׳קצרה‎ soul was impatient Num 21:4 (E), Judg 10:16; 16:16; Zech 11:8; נ ׳אאריך כי‎ that I should prolong my patience Job 6:11 אתנֿ ׳ידעתם הגר‎ ye know the feeling of the stranger Exod 23:9 (RD).

7 נפשׁ‎ is used occasionally for mental acts + לבב‎ (see

10); possibly also alone, owing to unconscious assimilation by late writers; but most, if not all, examples may be otherwise explained: מאד ידעת נפשׁי‎ Psa 139:14 my soul knoweth well (or I know well; compare 4a); נ ׳דעת טוב לא בלא‎ Prov 19:2 that the soul be without knowledge is not good (but RVm desire without knowledge, compare 6a); לנ חכמה ׳דעה‎ 24:14 know wisdom for thy soul (or according to thy desire, compare6a); בנ תדמי ׳אל‎ Est 4:13 think not in thy soul (or in thyself, compare 4b); ׳בג שׁער כמו‎ Prov 23:7 as he reckoneth in his soul (but RV in himself, compare 4b); נ תאמר ׳מה‎ 1Sam 20:4 (but AV RV following ᵐ5‎ ἐπιθυμεῖ = תְּאַוֶּה‎; see 6a).

8 נפשׁ‎ for acts of the will is dubious: נפשׁכם ישׁ אם‎ (את‎) if it is your purpose Gen 23:8 (P) 2Kin 9:15 (or if it is your desire, 6a); נ ׳בחרה‎ my soul chooseth Job 7:15; נ ׳מאנה‎ my soul, refuseth 6:7; Psa 77:3; ׳נ חפצה‎ their soul delighteth in Isa 66:3; ׳נ רצתה‎ my soul delighteth in 42:1; (all perhaps emotional, 6b, d, g).

9 נפשׁ‎ = character is still more dubious: נ ׳לאֹיָָֽֿשְׁרָה בּוֺ‎ Hab 2:4 his soul is not right in him (but ᵐ5‎ οὐκ εὐδοκεῖ ἡ ψυχή μου ἐν αὐτῷ [εὐδοκεῖ = רצתה‎], see

6); מְטֻמָּאָה לאֹ נַפְשִׁי‎ Ezek 4:14 my soul hath not (or I have not,

10. b. תְּלַהֵט גֶּחָלִים נַפְשׁוֺ‎ Job 41:13 his breath kindleth coals (of the crocodile).The piece is one of the latest in the book; primitive meaning hardly in such a passage; context favours reference to passion or fury; perhaps below

字義溯源：（受造之物的）呼吸，意即：生活力，（有極廣範的應用）：魂，生命，性命，精神，任何，嗜好，獸性，身體，氣息，人，心願，爭論，鬼，貪婪，人口，他，口，心，命，慾，於我，思念，致命地，一個人，屬自己的，有人，人位，歡樂，自己，他們，你們，我們，殺害，屍首，片，物，事物，意願，心願，願得到。這字出自（H5314（נָפַשׁ‎）*=舒暢）神用地上的塵土造人，將生氣吹在他鼻孔裏，他就成了活的魂（創2:7原文直譯），這句話，說出魂的來由。然而也照樣稱在人之外的一切動物為活的魂，不過一般都譯為：有生命的活物（創1:20,21,24,30）神自己也說，我的魂也不厭惡你們（利26:11，原文），又說，看哪，我的僕人，我所扶持，所揀選，魂裏所喜悅的（賽42:1，原文）神對他僕人的心意，和對神子民的心願，都由他的魂充分表達出來。這個編號，除了譯為魂之外，和合本有時也譯為命（創12:13），人口（創36:6），意（申21:14），願（詩27:12），人（箴11:30），心（詩42:1），靈（箴2:10），等等。魂乃是人的生命，人的自己，人的性格，有其感覺和嗜好。受造者的肉身生命（原文：魂）乃是在血中（利17:11），所以擊殺魂就是流血而奪去生命。當流便要保護約瑟就對他兄弟們說，我們不可害他的生命（原文：魂；創37:21），又說，不可流他的血（創37:22），也是把血和魂連在一起。因此，本書有時也將此編號譯為：魂生命。大衛說，他使我的靈魂（原文：魂）甦醒（詩23:3），就是說，主恢復，更新，舒暢他的生命，他的活力，他的全人
