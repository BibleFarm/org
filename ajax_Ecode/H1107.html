[Heb] בִּלְעֲדֵי‎, בַּלְעֲדֵי‎ ETCBC: בִּלְעֲדֵי‎ (prep｜without) OSHL: b.bt.ai TWOT: 246h GK: H1187
Derivation: or בַּלְעֲדֵי‎; constructive plural from בַּל‎ and עַד‎, not till, i.e. (as preposition or adverb)

H1107. bilade

בִּלְעֲדֵי‎ (perhaps from בַּל‎ and עֲדֵי עַד,‎ unto; Syriac = ἄνευ, χωρίς; Nabataean בלעד‎ except, EutNab 3, 9) — suffix בִּלְעָדַי‎ (3 t.), בַּלְעָדַי‎ (4 t.), בִּלְעָדֶיךָ‎ (once) — properly not unto, hence apart from, except, without:

a. Job 34:32 אֶחֱזֶה בִּלְעֲדֵי‎ except, apart from (what) I see myself, do thou instruct me. With suffix Gen 41:44 בִּלְעָדֶיךָ‎ apart from thee, without thee, no one shall lift up the head, Isa 45:6 for there is none בִּלְעָדָ֑י‎ except me. Also with suffix, as a particle of deprecation, Gen 14:24 בִּלְעָדַי‎ not to me ! i.e. I claim nothing, (in our idiom) not at all ! 41:16.

b. with מִבַּלְעֲדֵי מִן,‎ (so without). (α) apart from, especially with the collateral idea of without the knowledge and consent, Num 5:20; 2Kin 18:25 ( = Isa 36:10) am I now come up apart from, without ׳י‎ against this place to destroy it? Jer 44:19 (compare בִלְעָדֶיךָ‎ Gen 41:44). (β) apart from, besides, except, Josh 22:19; 2Sam 22:32a ( = Psa 18:32a) for who is God except ׳י‎? 18:32b; similarly Isa 43:11 מוֺשִׁיעַ מִבַּלְעָדַי וְאֵין‎, 44:6, 8; 45:21.
