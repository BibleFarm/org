H1974. hillul

[הִלּוּל‎] noun [masculine] (Late Hebrew id.) only plural הִלּוּלִים‎ rejoicing, praise:

1 הִלּוּלִים וִיַּעֲשׂוּ‎ Judg 9:27 i.e. a vintage-rejoicing, merry-making, connected with thanksgiving אֱלֹהֵיהֶם בֵּית וַיָּבאֹוּ‎ etc. (i.e. god baal-Berith, see 9:4).

2 of praise to ה ׳פקדֶשׁ ׳י,‎ Lev 19:24 (H) holiness of praise, i.e. a consecrated thing in token of thanksgiving for fruit, offered in 4th year (compare Late Hebrew).
