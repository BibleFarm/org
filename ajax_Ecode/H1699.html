דֹּבֶר‎ (dôber｜do'-ber｜noun｜masculine｜pasture｜speaking)
[Heb] דֹּבֶר‎, דִּבֵּר‎ ETCBC: דֹּבֶר‎ (subs｜pasture) OSHL: d.ai.al, d.ai.ad TWOT: 399c GK: H1824, H1825
Derivation: from דָבַר‎ (in its original sense);
Strong's: a pasture (from its arrangement of the flock)
KJV: fold, manner.

H1699. dober

[דֹּ֫בֶר‎] noun [masculine] pasture (compare Aramaic דַּבְרָא‎, Syriac ) — suffix הַדָּֽבְרוֺ בְּתוֺךְ כְּעֵדֶר‎ as a flock in the midst of its pasture Micah 2:12 (article with suffix, compare Ges§ 127, 4 b, who attaches ו‎ as conjunction to following word); כְּדֳבְרָם כְבָשִׂים וְרָעזּ‎ and lambs shall feed as (in) their pasture Isa 5:17.

דִּבֵּר‎ noun [masculine] speaking (so Ew§ 156) Jer 5:13 בָּהֶם אֵין וְהַדִּבֵּר‎; according to Hi Gf Ki (less probably) verb with article for relation (see [ דָּבַר‎]

Pi`el): but read rather הַדָּבָר‎, and the word is not in them.
