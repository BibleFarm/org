H5637. sarar

סָרַר‎ verb be stubborn, rebellious (usually towards ׳י‎) (Late Hebrew id., (rare); Assyrian sarâru); —

Qal Perfect3masculine singular ׳ס יִשְׂרָאֵל‎ Hosea 4:16 Israel is stubborn; Participle active וּמוֺרֶה סוֺרֵר בֵּן‎ Deut 21:18, compare 21:20; סוֺרְרִים בָּנִים‎ Isa 30:1, סוֺרֵר עַם‎ 65:2, ס ׳דּוֺר וּמֹרֶה‎ Psa 78:8 (all of Israel); מ ׳לֵב וּמוֺרֶה‎ Jer 5:23; סֹרֵרָ֔ה מָּרָה‎ Hosea 4:16 (simile of Israel, see above); as predicate סוֺרְרִים שָׂרֵיהֶם‎ 9:15 compare Isa 1:23; סוֺרְרִים סָרֵי‎ Jer 6:28 revolters among the rebellious (? compare סור‎

Qal near the end, and סַר‎ below); of loose woman וְסוֺרָ֑רֶת הִיא הֹמִיָּסה‎ Prov 7:11; as substantive = the stubborn, Psa 66:7; 68:7; 68:19; feminine singular סֹרֶ֫רֶת כָתֵף וַיִּתְּנוּ‎ Neh 9:29 and they presented a stubborn shoulder (of Israel) = Zech 7:11 (סֹרָ֑רֶת‎).
