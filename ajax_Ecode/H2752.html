II. חוֺרִי חֹרִי,‎ adjective, usually

proper name, of a people or

person (probably = cave-dweller, so Thes Rob Ges MV VB; see also W Max MüllAsien u. Europa 136, 155, 156) —

1. adjective, of a people הַח ׳שֵׂעִיר‎ Gen 36:20 Seir the Horite (P); read ׳הַחִ‎ also 36:2 for הַחִוִּי ᵑ0‎

2. proper name, of a people always with article; usually singular collective הַחֹרִיִ‎, ancient inhabitants of land of Edom Gen 14:6 (שֵׂעִיר בְּהַרֲרָם‎); in P called אֱדוֺם בְּאֶרֶץ שֵׂעִיר בְּנֵי‎ 36:21 (see 36:20); compare 36:29; 36:30; according to Deut 2:12 (where alone הַחֹרִים‎), 2:22 they were driven out by sons of Esau (yet see Gen 36:1 c. and Di); ᵐ5‎ ὁ Ξορραιος, οἱ Ξορραιοι, (36:29-30, Ξορρ(ε)ι).

3. proper name, masculine person

a. חֹרִי‎ an Edomite Gen 36:22 = 1Chr 1:39; ᵐ5‎ Ξορρ(ε)ι.

b. חִוֺרי‎ a Simeonite Num 13:5, but ᵐ5‎ Σουρ(ε)ι, ᵐ5‎L Σουδρι. — I. חֹרִי‎ see below I. חור‎.
