H2771. Charan

I. חָרָן‎ proper name, of a location city in northern Mesopotamia (Assyrian —arrânu = road, path COTGloss; compare DlPa 185; JenKosmologie 28 'junction of trading-routes' = cross-roads) — mentioned as city of Abram's sojourn Gen 11:31, where his father Terah died 11:32, and whence he departed for Canaan 12:4-5, where Laban, Jacob's uncle, lived, and whither Jacob fled 27:43; 28:10 (both חָרָ֫נָה‎), 29:4; as conquered by fathers of Sennacherib 2Kin 19:12 = Isa 37:12; as place of trade Ezek 27:23; Assyrian –arrana (+?), SchrCOT on Gen 11:31; 27:43 ᵐ5‎ Χαρραν. It was an ancient seat of worship of moon-god (Sin), see SchrKG 355, 596; see further ChwolsohnSsabier i, 303 ff. WüstenfeldZMG 1864, 443 f. SchrKG 355, 536 SachauReise 217 ff. MezGeschichte. d. Stadt Harran, 1892.

II. חָרָן‎ proper name, masculine son of Caleb of Judah 1Chr 2:46 (twice in verse); ᵐ5‎ Αρραν, ᵐ5‎L Ωρων (Sabean proper name חרן‎ DHMEpigr. Dakam. 56).
