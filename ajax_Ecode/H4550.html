מַסַּע‎ (maççaʻ｜mas-sah'｜noun｜masculine｜pulling up)
[Heb] מַסַּע‎ ETCBC: מַסַּע‎ (subs｜breaking) OSHL: n.dz.ab TWOT: 1380a GK: H5023
Derivation: from נָסַע‎;
Strong's: a departure (from striking the tents), i.e. march (not necessarily a single day's travel); by implication, a station (or point of departure)
KJV: journey(-ing).

H4550. massa

מַסַּע‎ noun [masculine] pulling up, breaking camp, setting out, journey; — absolute ׳מ‎ Num 10:2; Deut 10:11; plural construct מַסְעֵי‎ Num 10:28; 33:1; suffix מַסָּעָיו‎ Gen 13:3, מַסְעֵיהֶם‎ Exod 17:1 6t.; —

1. a. pulling up, breaking camp (with accusative מַחֲנֶה‎; Ges§ 45e, 115d) Num 10:2.

b. setting out Num 10:6, compare 10:23.

2 station, stage, journey (by stages), לְמ ׳וַיֵּלֶךְ‎ Gen 13:3 and he went by (according to) his journeyings (compare הלך‎, p. 231 a above); לְמַסְעֵיהֶם‎ Exod 17:1; compare 40:36, 38; Num 10:12 (all P; all with נָסַע‎), 33:1-2,(|| מוֺצָאֵיהֶם‎), 33:2 (|| id.); לְמַסַּע לֵךְ קוּם‎ Deut 10:11 for journeying (see Dr).
