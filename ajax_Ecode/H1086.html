H1086. balah

[בָּלָה‎] verb become old and worn out (Arabic , Aramaic בְּלֵי‎, , Ethiopic id.) —

Qal Perfect בָּלְתָה‎ Deut 8:4, בָּלוּ‎ 29:4 +; Imperfect יִבְלֶה‎ Job 13:28 etc.; Infinitive with suffix בְּלֹתִי‎ Gen 18:12; — wear out (intransitive), especially of garments Deut 8:4; 29:4 (twice in verse), all with מֵעַל‎ pregnantly wear out (and fall) from upon... (hence Neh 9:21), Josh 9:13; figurative of the heavens (with simile of garment) Isa 50:9; Psa 102:27 יִבְלוּ כַּבֶּגֶד‎, the earth Isa 51:6 תִּבְלֶה כַּבֶּגֶד‎; the bones (through suffering) Psa 32:3; afflicted man Job 13:28 יִבְלֶה כְּרָקָב וְהוּא‎ (|| עָשׁ אֲכָלוֺ כְּבֶגֶד‎); of an aged and decrepit woman Gen 18:12 (J) בְלֹתִי אַחֲרֵי‎ after I am worn out.

Pi`el causative of Qal.

a. wear out (transitive), figurative Lam 3:4 וְעוֺרִי בְשָׂרִי בִּלָּה‎, Psa 49:15 and their form שְּׁאוֺל לְבַלּוֺת‎ is for Sh®°ôl to consume away (others read לִבְלוֺת‎ is for wasting away [Dr§ 204], connecting ׳שׁ‎ with following), 1Chr 17:9 לְבַלֹּתוֺ‎ to wear it (Israel) out (altered from לְעַנּוֺתוֺ‎ 2Sam 7:10), compare Dan 7:25 Aramaic

b. wear out by use, use to the full, Isa 65:22 and the work of their hands יְבַלּוּ‎ they shall use to the full, enjoy, Job 21:13 they wear out their days in prosperity (Qr here יְכַלּוּ‎ complete, which perhaps is the true reading in both passages; compare Exod 5:13; Job 36:11). — On ַבּלּוֺתֹּי‎ Psa 92:11, see below בָּלַל‎.
