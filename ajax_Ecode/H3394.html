H3394. yareach

יָרֵחַ‎ noun masculineJosh 10:13 moon, especially in poetry (Late Hebrew id.; Phoenician ירח‎; Assyrian iriµu, according to PinchesBOR Aug. 1888, 207; Ethiopic moon, month; compare below יֶרַח‎) — absolute יָרֵחַ‎ Gen 37:9 25t.; suffix וִיָֽרֵחֵךְ‎ Isa 60:20: — moon, usually named with sun Josh 10:12-13, (poem in J E), Psa 72:5 (יָרֵחַ וְלִפְנֵי עִםשָֿׁ֑מֶשׁ‎), 89:38 (in these two, a symbol of permanence), 121:6; Isa 60:19; Hab 3:11; Joel 3:4; object of idolatrous worship Job 31:26 (+ sun); in same sense also + sun and stars Deut 4:19; 17:3; 2Kin 23:5; Jer 8:2; as determiner of feast-times Psa 104:19 (|| sun); + stars, as shining, by night 136:9 (|| sun, by day), so יָרֵחַ חֻקֹּת‎ Jer 31:35; (|| id.); elsewhere + sun and stars Gen 37:9 (E), Isa 13:10; Ezek 32:7; Joel 2:10; Joel 4:15; Psa 148:3; Eccl 12:2; + stars Psa 8:4; Job 25:5; with neither sun nor stars only יָרֵחַ עַדבְּֿלִי‎ Psa 72:7.
