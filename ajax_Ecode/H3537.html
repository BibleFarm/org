כַּד‎ (kad｜kad｜noun｜feminine｜jar)
[Heb] כַּד‎ ETCBC: כַּד‎ (subs｜pitcher) OSHL: k.an.ab TWOT: 953a GK: H3902 Greek: ὑδρία
Derivation: from an unused root meaning to deepen;
Strong's: properly, a pail; but generally of earthenware; a jar for domestic purposes
KJV: barrel, pitcher.

H3537. kad

כַּד‎ noun feminine jar (Late Hebrew id.; Aramaic כַּדָּא‎) — כַּד‎ absolute 1Kin 17:12; Eccl 12:6; construct 1Kin 17:14, 16; כַּדֵּךְ‎ Gen 24:14, 17, 43 etc.; plural כַּדִּים‎ Judg 7:16 (twice in verse) + 3t.; — water-jar, carried on woman's shoulder Gen 24:14-15, 16, 17, 18, 20, 43, 45, 46 (all J), 1Kin 18:34; Eccl 12:6; empty (of water), containing lamps Judg 7:16, 19, 20; containing meal 1Kin 17:12, 14, 16. (On extra-Palestinian history of this word compare LagBN 104.)
