H2038. Harmon

[הַרְמוֺן‎] noun masculine meaning dubious; text perhaps corrupt; only Amos 4:3 הַהַרְמ֫וֺנָה וְהִשְׁלַכְתֶּנָֿה‎ and ye shall cast them (your אחרית‎, posterity, AE; Hi RV cast [yourselves], but read rather with Vrss as passive ׳וְהָשְׁל‎ shall be cast) into Harmon; if text be sound, some locality must be meant, though the nature of the allusion is lost (AV, into the palace, treats הרמון‎ improbable as = ארמון‎). ᵑ6 ᵑ7‎ have mountain (s) of Armenia (מוֺנָה הַר‎), Symm Armenia, compare LagGes. Abh. 172, HoffmZAW iii. 1882, 102, of exile; Hi-St for ׳ה‎ read הֲדַדְרִמּוֺנָה‎ to Hadadrimmon, proper name, of a location in plain of Jezreel, so Gunning; ᵐ5‎ εἰς τὸ ὄρος τὸ Ῥομμαν, whence Ew ingeniously רמונה ההר‎ and ye shall cast Rimmonah (name of idol, supposed feminine of Rimmon 2Kin 5:18) to the mountains.
