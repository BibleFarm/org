II. שָׁלַל‎ verb spoil, plunder (usually identification with I. ׳שׁ‎; Late Hebrew id. (Jastr, compare Dalm); Assyrian šalâlu, id.; HomChr 33 compare Sabean תַלל‎ plunder, connecting this with Arabic a flock of sheep (or goats); then שְׁלָלָא ᵑ7‎ booty, and (rare) Syriac are loan-words); —

Qal Perfect3masculine singular consecutive ׳וְשׁ‎ Ezek 26:12; 2masculine singular שַׁלּוֺתָ‎ Hab 2:8, etc.; Imperfect3masculine plural suffix יְשָׁלּוּךָ‎ 2:8; construct לִשְׁלֹ‎ Isa 10:6 +; Participle plural שֹׁלְלִים‎ Zech 2:12, etc.; — spoil, plunder (usually || בָּזַז‎), with accusative of congnate meaning with verb שָׁלָל‎ Isa 10:6; Ezek 29:19; 38:12-13, (twice in verse), accusative חַיִל‎ wealth 26:12; accusative of person, שֹׁלְלִים‎ Jer 50:10; Hab 2:8; Ezek 39:10, רַבּים גּוֺיִם‎ Hab 2:8, compare Zech 2:12.
