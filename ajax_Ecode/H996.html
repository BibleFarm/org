בֵּין‎ (bêyn｜bane｜preposition｜interval)
[Heb] בֵּין‎, בַּיִן‎, בַּיִת‎ ETCBC: בַּיִן‎ (subs｜interval) OSHL: b.bo.ac TWOT: 239a GK: H1068, H1075 Greek: μεσίτης, μέσος
Derivation: (sometimes in the plural masculine or feminine); properly, the constructive form of an otherwise unused noun from בִּין‎; a distinction; but used only as a preposition,
Strong's: between (repeated before each noun, often with other particles); also as a conjunction, either...or
KJV: among, asunder, at, between (-twixt...and), [phrase] from (the widest), [idiom] in, out of, whether (it be...or), within.

H996. bayin

[בַּ֫יִן‎] substantive properly interval, space between ( id.) — construct בֵּין‎ once Isa 44:4 (Baer) בֵּינוֺ בֵינֵךְ, ֵבּינְךָ, בֵּינִי, בֵּן,‎ (בֵּינָיו‎ Josh 3:4; 8:11 Qr); with plural suffix in plural form בֵּינֵינוּ‎ (בֵּנֵינוּ‎), etc.; also בֵּינוֺת‎ Ezek 10:2 (twice in verse); 10:6 (twice in verse); 10:7 (twice in verse) (+ 1:13 ᵐ5‎ Hi Ew etc. for דְּמוּת‎), בֵּינוֺתֵינוּ‎ Gen 26:28; Josh 22:34; Judg 11:10, בֵּינוֺתָם‎ Gen 42:23; 2Sam 21:7; Jer 25:16; dual בֵּנַיִם‎ (see below); —

1 always (except dual) as

preposition in the interval of, between (so Aramaic בֵּין‎, & plural בֵּינֵי‎, , ; ), as Gen 15:17 הַגְּזָרִים בֵּין‎ between the pieces, Exod 13:9 and elsewhere עֵינֶיךָ בֵּין‎ between thy eyes (see עַיִן‎), Isa 22:11 and elsewhere between the two walls (see חוֺמָה‎), Job 24:11; 30:7; Job 40:30 part him between merchants; rather more Generally among Hosea 13:15; Song 2:2-3, Ezek 19:2; 31:3; Job 34:37; less exactly within Prov 26:13 a lion is הָֽרְחֹבוֺת בֵּין‎ within the board places (compare 23:13 בְּתוֺךְ‎): once with a singular (unusual) Dan 8:16 אוּלַי בֵּין‎ between the Ulai, i.e. between its banks. When the space separating two distinct objects is to be indicated, this is done

a. most commonly by repeating בין‎, as Gen 13:3 הָעָ֑י וּבֵין בֵּיתְאֵל בֵּין‎ literally in the interval of Bethel, and in the interval of `Ai, i.e. between Bethel and `Ai, 16:14; 17:7; 31:50-51, +?

b. more rarely by לְ ֗֗֗ בֵּין‎, Gen 1:6 dividing לָמָ֑יִם מַיִם בֵּין‎ literally in the interval of waters with reference to waters, i.e. between the waters and the waters, Lev 20:25; 27:33; Deut 17:8 (3 t. in verse); 2Sam 19:36; 1Kin 3:9; Ezek 41:18; 42:20; 44:23 (twice in verse); Jonah 4:11; Mal 3:18 (twice in verse); 2Chr 14:10.

c. by וּלְ ֗֗֗ בֵּין‎ Joel 2:17.

d. by לְבֵין ֗֗֗ בֵּין‎ Isa 59:2. בֵּין‎ is used not only of actual locality, but also with verbs of dividing (figurative) Gen 1:14; Lev 10:10, and of judging, knowing, teaching, etc., if the idea of distinguishing be involved, as Gen 16:5; 2Sam 19:36 לְרָע טוֺב בֵּין הַאֵדַע‎ can I discern between good and evil ? 1Kin 3:9; Jonah 4:11; Gen 31:49 (watch between), Judg 11:10 (hear), Josh 22:27 (witness), Isa 2:4 and he shall judge (arbitrate) between the nations, 5:3; Lev 27:33; Ezek 44:23; Mal 3:18; 2Chr 14:10 (see RV); and in other metaphoric applications, as of a covenant or sign between two contracting parties, Gen 9:12, 15; Exod 31:13 +; or an oath Gen 26:28; 2Sam 21:7; enmity or strife Gen 3:15; 13:7; Deut 25:1; Prov 6:19; peace 1 Kings 5:26; good-will 14:9. — It is used of time in the phrase of P הָעַרְבַּיִם בֵּין‎ (see below עֶרֶב‎), & Neh 5:18 יָמִים עֲשֶׂרֶת בֵּין‎ during the interval of ten days, i.e. every ten days (unusual).With other prepositions: —

a. אֶלבֵּֿין‎, after a verb implying motion, in between, in among Ezek 31:10, 14. So לְ אֶלבֵּֿינוֺת‎ ib. 10:2.

b. בְּבֵן‎ Isa 44:4 in the midst of, amongst (ᵐ5‎ Ew Di Che חָצִיר מַיִם כְּבֵין‎).
