עָגוּר‎ (ʻâgûwr｜aw-goor'｜noun｜masculine｜crane)
[Heb] עָגוּר‎ ETCBC: עָגוּר‎ (subs｜bulbul-bird) OSHL: p.ao.ab TWOT: 1563a GK: H6315
Derivation: passive part (but with active sense) of an unused root meaning to twitter;
Strong's: probably the swallow
KJV: swallow.

H5693. agur

עָגוּר‎ noun [masculine] apparently understood by Masoretes as name of a bird; very uncertain; crane according to Saad (Isaiah), but note of crane not suitable (TristrNHB 239 f.); — וְעָגוּר וְסוּס וְתוֺר‎ Jer 8:7; עָגוּר כְּסוּס‎ Isa 38:14 (but here gloss Klo Brd Di Dn CheHpt); Thes and (in Jeremiah) Hi Gf Gie strike out וְ‎ and take ׳ע‎ as adjective of סוס‎ = twittering or the like; Thes assumes transpos. from גער‎, Ethiopic cry.
