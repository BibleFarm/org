H1389. gibah

I. גִּבְעָה‎ noun feminine hill — absolute גִּבְעָה‎ 2Sam 2:25 13t. + Ezek 6:13 (strike out Co q. v.) 1Sam 7:1; 2Sam 6:3-4, (compare Dr); construct גִּבְעַת‎ Josh 5:3 8t. + 1Sam 10:5 (compare Dr); suffix גִּבְעָתִי‎ Ezek 34:26 (but strike out Co), גִּבְעָתָהּ‎ Isa 31:4; plural absolute גְּבָעוֺת‎ Deut 12:2 35t.; construct גִּבְעוֺת‎ 33:15; Hab 3:6, גִּבְעֹת‎ Gen 49:26; suffix גִּבְעוֺתֶיךָ‎ Ezek 35:8; — hill, height, elevation, both high and low, compare Psa 148:9 גבעות וכל ההרים‎, 65:13; —

1 in ordinary prose, hill, lower than mountain Exod 17:9-10, (E) 2Sam 2:25; it may be proper name in 1Sam 7:1; 10:10; 2Sam 6:3-4, see also below II. גִּבְעָה‎.

2 especially as place of illicit worship כָּלגֿ ׳עַל רענן עץ כל ותחת גְּבֹהָה‎ 1Kin 14:23; 2Kin 17:10; Jer 2:20; compare Deut 12:2; 2Kin 16:4; 2Chr 28:4 (these two + במות‎); also Hosea 4:13; Jer 13:27; 17:2; Ezek 6:13 (see above).

3 very commonly || הר‎ in poetry & prophets Deut 33:15; Psa 72:3; 114:4; 114:6; 148:9; Song 2:8; 4:6; Isa 2:2, 14; 30:17, 25; 31:4; 40:4, 12; 41:15; 42:15; 54:10; 55:12; 65:7; Jer 3:23; 4:24; 16:16; 50:7; Ezek 6:3; 34:6; 35:8; 36:4, 6; Hosea 4:13; 10:8; Josh 4:18; Amos 9:13; Micah 4:1; 6:1; Nahum 1:5; Hab 3:6; rarely in prose Deut 12:2; sometimes as high and majestic (poetry) ׳ג עולם‎ Gen 49:26; Deut 33:15; Hab 3:6 ('everlasting hills'); compare also Job 15:7; Prov 8:25(with adjective high, compare above); see also Jer 49:16.

4 hills with special names, some nearly or quite = proper name, of a location, which see under the respective words: ׳ג מֹרֶה‎ Judg 7:1 teacher's hill, in valley of Jezreel; ׳ג הָעֲרָלוֺת‎ Josh 5:3 hill of the fore-skins; ׳ג הָאֱלֹהִים‎ 1Sam 10:5 (a designation of Gibeah); ׳ג הַחֲכִילָה‎ 23:19; 26:1, 3; ׳ג אַמָּה‎ 2Sam 2:24; ׳ג הַלְּבוֺנָה‎ Song 4:6 (i.e. hill where frankincense is grown); ׳ג גָּרֵב֑‎ Jer 31:39; ׳ג יְרוּשָׁלַם‎ Isa 10:32; compare 31:4 (where || הַרצִֿיּוֺן‎), Ezek 34:26 (see above) & הַגְּבָעוֺת‎ Zeph 1:10 hills on which Jerusalem stands.
