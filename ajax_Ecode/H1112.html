H1112. Beleshatstsar

ֵבּלְאשַׁצַּר‎ proper name, masculine (Bêl-šar-uƒur, Bel, protect the king COT Dan 5:1) 8:1; represented as king of Babylon, successor, and apparently son of Nebuchadrezzar (5:1-2, 11 etc.); in cuneiform inscription known only as prince, son of Nabonidus (last Shemitic king of Babylon), see COT 1.c.
