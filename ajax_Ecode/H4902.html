II. מֶ֫שֶׁךְ‎ proper name, of a people the Moschi (Greek Μοσχοι, see below; Assyrian Mušku, Muški, DlPar 250 E. SchrCOT Gen 10:2; KGF 155 ff.; compare Sabean משך‎ Hal243); — 'son' of Japheth 10:2 (P), between תֻּבָל‎ and תִּירָס‎, = 1Chr 1:5; also, מֶ֑שֶׁךְ‎, probably error, 1:17 (see מַשׁ‎); מֶ֑שֶׁךְ‎ Psa 120:5 (|| קֵדָר‎; here without תֻּבָל‎); וּמ ׳תּוּבַל‎ Ezek 27:13; compare 32:26; 38:2 (both + תֻּבָל‎); so also ᵐ5‎ Isa 66:19 (Lo StaJavan 8 Du CheHpt; see above מָשַׁךְ‎ 2); מ ראֹשׁ נְשִׂיא ׳גּוֺג וְתֻבָ֑ל‎ Ezek 38:3; 39:1. ᵐ5‎ Μοσοχ (Μεσοχ), Samaritan מושׁ(י)ך‎. — On identification compare Boch.; in Assyrian times they dwelt in west (or northwest) Armenia (compare Schrl.c.); in Persian times apparently farther northeast (southeast of Euxine Sea), compare Μοσχοι καὶ Τιβαρηνοι Herodiii. 94; vii. 78; also DiGen 10:2 and CheEncycl.
