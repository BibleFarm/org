אֵצֶל‎ (ʼêtsel｜ay'-tsel｜noun｜masculine｜conjunction)
[Heb] אֵצֶל‎ ETCBC: אֵצֶל‎ (subs｜side) OSHL: a.fj.ab TWOT: 153a GK: H725
Derivation: from אָצַל‎ (in the sense of joining);
Strong's: a side; (as a preposition) near
KJV: at, (hard) by, (from) (beside), near (unto), toward, with. See also בֵּית הָאֵצֶל‎.

H681. etsel

I. אֵ֫צֶל‎61 substantive conjunction, proximity; with suffix אֶצְלוֺ אֶצְלִי,‎ etc.; only used as a

preposition a. (as an implicit accusative) in proximity to (as though LagBN 68), beside: Gen 39:10, 15; 41:3 and stood הַמַּרוֺת אֵצֶל‎ beside the kine, 1Sam 5:2 דָּגוֺן אֵצֶל‎ beside Dagon, 1Kin 13:24-25, 21:1; Prov 8:30; Ezek 1:15, 19; 33:30; 39:15; often in phrase (׳מ ׳י‎) הַמִּזְבֵּחַ אֵצֶל‎ Lev 1:16; 6:3; Deut 16:21; 1Kin 2:29; Amos 2:8 +; of a locality (compare II. אֵת‎ 2) Deut 11:30 beside the terebinths of Moreh, 1Kin 1:9; 4:12; Jer 41:17. After a verb of motion (late) Dan 8:7, 17; compare 2Chr 28:15 b.

b. with מֵאֵצֶל מִן,‎ from proximity to, from beside (compare מֵעִם מֵאֵת,‎): 1Sam 20:41 (read with הָאַרְגָּב מֵאֵצֶל ᵐ5‎ from beside the mound); Ezek 40:7 contiguous to, beside (מִן‎ 3c); with suffix 1Sam 17:30 מֵאֶצְלוֺ וַיָּסָב‎ and he turned about from beside him, 1Kin 3:20; 20:36; Ezek 10:16.
