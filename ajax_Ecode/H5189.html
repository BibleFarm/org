H5189. netishah

[נְטִישָׁה‎] noun feminine twig, tendril of vine (as spreading, compare √ Niph`al 3); — only plural הֵתַז הֵסִיר הַנְּטִישׁוֺת‎ Isa 18:5 the tendrils he hath removed, compare נְטִישׁוֺתֶיהָ הָסִירוּ‎ Jer 5:10 (apparently cited from Isaiah; explanation by FinnJourn. Trans. Vict. Inst. xxii. 306 f. from use by shepherds of poterium spinosum, called nettsh, as defence of sheepfolds), — in both of destroying city (under figure of vineyard); יָם עָ֑בְרוּ נְטִישֹׁתַיִךְ‎ 48:32 thy tendrils went over the sea (figurative of prosperity of Moab).
