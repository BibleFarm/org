[מוֺסֵר‎]

noun masculineIsa 28:22 band, bond, in poetry & late (= מֹאסֵר‎; Ethiopic Syriac , compare Assyrian mêsiru, sheathing, plating, e.g. LyonSargontexte, p. 16, l. 65 & p. 80) — construct מוֺסַר‎ Job 12:18 (so Di Hoffm and others for מוּסַר‎); plural מוֺסֵרוֺת‎ Jer 5:5; 27:2; construct מֹסְרוֺת‎ Job 39:5; מוֺסְרֵי‎ Isa 52:2; suffix מוֺסֵרָי֑‎ Psa 116:16; מוֺסְרוֺתֶיךָ‎ Jer 30:8; מוֺסְרֵיכֶם‎ Isa 28:22; מוֺסְרוֺתֵי֑מוֺ‎ Psa 2:3 etc.; — bands accusative after פִתֵּהַ‎ Job 39:5 restraining-bands of wild ass; Psa 116:16 bonds of distress; Isa 52:2 ׳מ צַוָּארֵח‎ bonds of captivity of Zion, verb Hithpa`el; compare also Job 12:18 מִּתֵּחַ מְלָכִים מוֺסַר‎ (so read, see above & AV RV); Di understands bonds imposed by kings; Hoffm girdles of kings, & reads וַיָּסַר‎ in ||, for וַיֶּאְסַר‎; more often accusative after נִתֵּק‎ Psa 2:3 bonds imposed by ׳י‎ & his anointed, compare Jer 5:5 & 2:20 (ᵑ9 ᵐ5‎, see Commentaries); of Yahweh's breaking bonds of Israel 30:8; Nahum 1:13 (last four || עֹל שָׁבַר‎), bonds of oppressed Psa 107:14; מ ׳יֶחְזְקוּ‎ Isa 28:22, i.e. bonds imposed by Assyria; compare Jer 27:2 (|| מֹט‎) literal, symbolic of rule of Nebuchadrezzar.
