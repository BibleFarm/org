[Heb] מֹוצָא‎ ETCBC: מֹוצָא‎ (subs｜issue) OSHL: j.cq.ad TWOT: 893c GK: H4604 Greek: ἔξοδος, πηγή, πορεία, τόπος

Strong's: a going forth, i.e. (the act) an egress, or (the place) an exit; hence, a source or product; specifically, dawn, the rising of the sun (the East), exportation, utterance, a gate, a fountain, a mine, a meadow (as producing grass)

H4161. motsa

I. מוֺצָא‎ noun masculineHosea 6:3 place or act of going forth, issue, export, souce, spring; — absolute ׳מ‎ Job 28:1; Psa 75:7; construct ׳מ‎ Num 30:13 9t.; מֹצָא‎ Job 38:27; Dan 9:25; suffix מוֺצָאֲךָ‎ 2Sam 3:25; מֹצָאוֺ‎ Hosea 6:3; מוֺצָאוֺ‎ Psa 19:7; plural construct מוֺצָאֵי‎ Isa 41:18 5t.; suffix מוֺצָאָיו‎ Ezek 43:11;מוֺצָאֵיהֶם‎ Num 33:2 (twice in verse); מוֺצָאֵיהֶן‎ Ezek 42:11; —

1 a going forth:

a. the act, of ׳י‎ Hosea 6:3; of the sun = rising Psa 19:7 (see יצא‎ 1f); of man 2Sam 3:25 (opposed to מָבוֺא‎ Kt); ׳מ דָּבָר‎ Dan 9:25 going forth of a command.

b. concrete, גּוֺלָה מוֺצָאֵי‎ Ezek 12:4 goings forth of exile, i.e. those going forth into exile (in simile).

c. way out, exit: concrete, of chambers in temple Ezek 42:11; 43:11 (opposed to מוֺבָאָיו‎); הַמִּקְדָּשׁ מוֺצָאֵי‎ 44:5 (opposed to הַבַּיִת מְבוֺא‎).

2 that which goes forth:

a. utteranc of mouth or lips (especially of solemn or formal speech), פִייֿ ׳מוֺצָא‎ Deut 8:3; מ ׳שְׂפָתַי‎ Jer 17:16; Psa 89:35; so Deut 23:24; Num 30:13 (P).

b. export of horses 1Kin 10:23 2Chr 1:16.

3 place of going forth:

a. source or spring of water 2Kin 2:21; Isa 58:11 (in simile) Psa 107:33 (|| נְהָרוֺת‎), 107:35 (|| אֲגַםמֿים‎) = Isa 41:18 (|| id.); הָעֶלְיוֺן גִיחוֺן מֵימֵי מוֺצָא‎2Chr 32:30.

b. place of departure of Israel on marchNum 2Chr 33:2 (twice in verse) (P).

c. מוֺצָא‎ = east (place of sun's going forth) Psa 75:7 (opposed to מַעֲרָב‎); zeugmatically, 65:9 וָעֶרֶב בֹּקֶר מוֺצָאֵי‎ i.e. the east and the west (compare the two Orients, Qor 43:37).

d. place whence silver comes = mine Job 28:1; דֶשֶׁא מֹצָא וּלְהַצְמִיחַ‎ 38:27 and to cause the growing-place (others, the growth) of young grass to sprout.
