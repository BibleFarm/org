דִּמְעָה‎ noun feminine collective tears (chiefly poetic and late; frequently in Jeremiah and contemporary) (Arabic ; Assyrian dimu, dimtu HptKAT 2 Gloss i. ZimBP 23, 95; Aramaic דִּמְעָא‎, ; Late Hebrew דִּמְעָה דֶּמַע,‎) — absolute ׳ד‎ Psa 80:6 10t.; construct דִּמְעַת‎ Eccl 4:1; suffix דִּמְעָתִי‎ Psa 6:7 4t.; דִּמְעָתֶ֑ךָ‎ 2Kin 20:5 = Isa 38:5 + Ezek 24:16 (strike out ᵐ5‎ Co); דִּמְעָתָהּ‎ Lam 1:2; plural דְּמָעוֺת‎ Psa 80:6; Lam 2:11 — tears Psa 6:7; Eccl 4:1; Lam 1:2; 2:11; especially in Jeremiah in phrase ד ׳עֵינִי תֵּרֵד‎ etc. Jer 9:17; 13:17; 14:17, compare Lam 2:18; Jeremiah 8:23 ד ׳מְקוֺר וְעֵינִי‎; דִּמְעָתִי אֲרַיָּ֫וֶךְ‎ of weeping over, in behalf of one Isa 16:9; as appealing to God's compassion 2Kin 20:5 = Isa 38:5; Psa 39:13; 56:9 בְּנאֹדֶ֑ךָ ד ׳שִׂימָה‎ (compare Che); see also 116:8; Jer 31:16; Isa 25:8; opposed to רִנָּה‎ Psa 126:5; of hypocritical tears Mal 2:13; figurative tears as food Psa 42:4 (לחם דִּמְעָתִי‎); ד ׳לחם‎ 80:6; as drink 80:6, compare Babylonian dimtu maštîtî, tears (were) my drink (ZimBP 42; || bikîtum kurmatî, weeping (was) my sustenance). — Ezek 24:16 דִּמְעָתֶ֑ךָ תָבוֺא וְלֹא‎ strike out ᵐ5‎ Co compare 24:23.
