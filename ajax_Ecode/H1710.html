H1710. dagah

דָּגָה‎ noun feminine fish (Late Hebrew id.) — דָּגָה‎ Num 11:5 5t.; construct דְּגַת‎ Gen 1:26 4t. + Ezek 29:4b (strike out B Co); suffix דְּגָתוֺ‎ 47:10 ᵑ9 ᵐ5‎ Co; (דגתם ᵑ0‎), דְּגָתָם‎ Isa 50:2; Psa 105:29 + Ezek 47:10 ᵑ0‎ compare above — fish, almost always collective; fish of sea הַיָּם דְּגַת‎ Gen 1:26, 28 (P) Ezek 47:10 (in simile); in Nile (יְאֹר‎) Exod 7:18, 21 (E) compare Psa 105:29, יְאֹרֶיךָ דְּגַת‎ Ezek 29:4 (twice in verse) (compare above) 29:5; in sea and rivers Isa 50:2; Ezek 47:9-10, food in Egypt Num 11:5 (JE); image of fish forbidden in worship, as of beasts and birds Deut 4:18 (on sacredness of fish, and use in sacrifice compare SeldenDe Diis Syris, ii. 3. RSSern. i. 274); of single fish only Jonah 2:2 (2:1 (twice in verse); Jonah 2:11 דָּג‎) by late usage; compare conversely דָּאג‎ = דָּגָה‎collective Neh 13:16.
