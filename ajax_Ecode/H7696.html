H7696. shaga

[שָׁגַע‎] Pu`al be mad (Arabic coo, of male pigeon, utter long whinny, of camel (compare HoffmZAW iii (1883), 89 RSJPhil. Xiv. 119 f. GoldziherArab. Philol. i. 59 ff. LagBN 202); Ethiopic be mad (n insert, Di1055 BaES 50); Assyrian šegû, rage, howl; on meaning in Hebrew see especially WeSkizzen iii. 130); —

Pu`al Participle as adjective : predicate מְשֻׁגָּע וְהָיִיתָ‎ Deut 28:34 thou shalt be maddened (מִן‎ because of), figurative for driven to despair; ׳מ‎ of prophets, contemptuously Hosea 9:7, וּמִתְנַבֵּא מ ׳כָּלאִֿישׁ‎ Jer 29:26, as substantive 2Kin 9:11; plural מְשֻׁגָּעִים‎ 1 Samuel 21:16 madmen.

Hithpa`el shew madness; Participle מִשְׁתַּגֵּעַ אִישׁ‎ 1Sam 21:15; Infinitive construct לְהִשְׁתַּגֵּעַ‎ v.1 Samuel 21:16.
