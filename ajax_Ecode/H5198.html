H5198. nataph

I. [נָטָף‎] noun masculine drop; — נִטְפֵימָֿ֑יִם‎ Job 36:27 (|| מָטָר‎, rain).

II. נָטָף‎ noun [masculine] an odorific gum (gathered in drops), used in sacred incense Exod 30:34 (P). Identification dubious; ᵐ5‎ στακτή, ᵑ9‎ stacte, i.e. (Hesych.) a kind of myrrh, so Kn, compare RiHWB; Rosenm Thes Ke and others storax; Rabb opobalsamum (compare Dion the passage NowArchaeology ii. 64. 248).
