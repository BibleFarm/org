סְגֻלָּה‎ (çᵉgullâh｜seg-ool-law'｜noun｜feminine｜possession)
[Heb] סְגֻלָּה‎ ETCBC: סְגֻלָּה‎ (subs｜property) OSHL: o.ah.ab TWOT: 1460a GK: H6035 Greek: περιούσιος, περιποιέομαι, περιποίησις
Derivation: feminine passive participle of an unused root meaning to shut up;
Strong's: wealth (as closely shut up)
KJV: jewel, peculiar (treasure), proper good, special.

H5459. segullah

סְגֻלָּה‎ noun feminine possession, property (on formation see BaNB § 95 b Köii. 1, 168); — absolute ׳ס‎ Exod 19:5 5t.; construct סְגֻלַּת‎ Eccl 2:8; suffix סְגֻלָּתוֺ‎ Psa 135:4; —

1 valued property, peculiar treasure, which ׳י‎ has chosen (בחר‎) and taken to himself; always of people of Israel, first Exod 19:5 (E; ᵐ5‎ λαὸς περιούσιος = Titus 2:14; 1Pet 2:9 λαὸς εἰς περιποίησιν = περιποίησις Eph 1:14 compare BrMP 102; MA 52, 235); then ס ׳עַם‎ Deut 7:6; 14:2; 26:18; later ׳ס‎ alone, Mal 3:17; Psa 135:4.

2 treasure (very late), of kings 1Chr 29:3 (gold and silver), Eccl 2:8.
