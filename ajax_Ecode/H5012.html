נָבָא‎ (nâbâʼ｜naw-baw'｜verb｜prophesy)
[Heb] נָבָא‎ ETCBC: נבא‎ (verb｜speak as prophet) OSHL: n.ai.ac TWOT: 1277 GK: H5547 Greek: ἀποφθέγγομαι, λαλέω, προφητεύω
Derivation: a primitive root;
Strong's: to prophesy, i.e. speak (or sing) by inspiration (in prediction or simple discourse)
KJV: prophesy(-ing), make self a prophet.

H5012. naba

[נָבָא‎] verb denominative prophesy (in oldest forms, of religious ecstasy with or without song and music; later, essentially religious instruction, with occasional predictions); —

Hithpa`el Perfect2masculine singular הִתְנַבִּיתָ‎ 1Sam 10:16; 1singular הִנַּבֵּאתִי‎ Ezek 37:10; Imperfect יִתְנַבֵּא‎ 1Sam 10:10 7t.; 3 masculine plural יִתְנַבְּאוּ‎ Num 11:25 5t.; Imperative הִנַּבְּאוּ‎ Jer 23:13; Infinitive הִתְנַבּוֺת‎ 1Sam 10:13; Participle מִתְנַבֵּא‎ Jer 26:20 9t. 1 Prophesy under influence of divine spirit:

a. in the ecstatic state Num 11:25-26, 27 (J), with music 1Sam 10:5-6, 10, 13, in frenzy 19:20-21, (twice in verse); 19:23-24, excited to violence 18:10 (= מְשֻׁגָּע‎ mad 2Kin 9:11); Jer 29:26.

b. apart from ecstatic state, absolute Ezek 37:10, with לְ‎ 1Kin 22:8; Jer 29:27; על‎ 1Kin 22:18 2Chr 18:17, 7; 20:37; י ׳בְּשֵׁם‎ Jer 26:20.

3 of false prophets 1Kin 22:10 2Chr 18:9; Jer 14:14; Ezek 13:17.

נבא‎ (√ of following; see Biblical Hebrew id., but especially BewerAmos. J. Semitic Lang., Jan. 1902 who compare Assyrian nabû, tear away, lead forcibly, hence prophets as (figuratively) carried away by divine frenzy, ecstasy, compare 1Sam 10:6, 10; 19:20, 24).
