noun masculine god, God. (Samaritan id., Aramaic אֱלָהּ‎, , Arabic , Sabean אלה‎ DHMl.c.; — אֱלֹהַּ‎ as found in Hebrew probably a singular formed by inference from plural אֱלֹהִים‎: compare Nesl.c.) —

1 a heathen god, late usage; כָּלאֱֿלוֺהַּ‎2Chr 32:15; Dan 11:37; לאלהו כחו זוּ‎ whose power is his god Hab 1:11; 2Kin 17:31 (but Qr אֱלֹהֵי‎), בְּיָדוֺ אֱלוֺהַּ הביא אשר‎ who doth bring God in his hand Job 12:6 (Ew Di RVm, etc.)

2 God, used in ancient poems Deut 32:15, 17; Psa 18:32, and on their basis an archaism in later poetry Job 3:4 + (41 t. Job), Psa 50:22; 114:7; 139:19; Prov 30:5; Isa 44:8; Hab 3:3; Neh 9:17 (citing Exod 34:6 where אֵל‎ is used).
