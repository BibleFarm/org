דָּלַק‎ verb burn (intransitive), hotly pursue (Late Hebrew id., Aramaic דְּלַק‎, all intransitive burn, kindle; Hiph`il, Aph`el transitive) —

Qal Perfect2masculine singular דָּלַקְתָּ‎ Gen 31:36; 3masculine plural וְדָֽלְקוּ‎ consecutive Obadiah 18; suffix דְּלָקֻנִי‎ Lam 4:19; Imperfect יִדְלַק‎ Psa 10:2; Infinitive construct דְּלֹק‎ 1Sam 17:53; Participle active plural דֹּלְקִים‎ Psa 7:14; Prov 26:23; —

1 burn, בָהֶם וְדָֽלְקוּ‎ Obadiah 18 (figurative of Israel ravaging among Edomites, || וַאֲכָלוּם‎; on construction with בְּ‎ compare 2Kin 17:25); also in יִפְעָ֑ל לְדֹלְקִים חִצָּיו‎ Psa 7:14 his arrows he maketh burning ones (subject God) RV De Che and others; & דֹּלְקִים שְׂפָתַיִם‎ Prov 26:23 burning (fervent) lips (opposed to רָ֑ע לֵב‎).

2 hotly pursue Gen 31:36 (E; followed by אַחֲרֵי‎) 1Sam 17:53 (followed by id.); Psa 10:2; Lam 4:19 (both followed by accusative)

Hiph`il Imperfect suffix יַדְלִיקֵם‎ Isa 5:11; Imperative הַדְלֵק‎ Ezek 24:10; — inflame, with accusative, subject wine Isa 5:11; kindle object הָאֵשׁ‎ Ezek 24:10.
