בָּדָד‎ (bâdâd｜baw-dawd'｜adverb｜isolation)
[Heb] בָּדָד‎ ETCBC: בָּדָד‎ (subs｜alone) OSHL: b.af.ac TWOT: 201b GK: H970 Greek: χωρίζω
Derivation: from בָּדַד‎;
Strong's: separate; adverb, separately
KJV: alone, desolate, only, solitary.

H910. badad

בָּדָד‎ noun [masculine] isolation, separation: Isa 27:10 בָּדָד בְּצוּרָה עִיר‎ the fenced city is isolation, i.e. is solitary (substantive for adjective : Dr§ 189. 2); more usually as adverb accusative, to signify alone, Deut 32:12; especially with verbs of dwelling, Lev 13:46 יֵשֵׁב בָּדָד‎ he shall dwell alone (literally in isolation), Jer 15:17; Lam 1:1; 3:28: figurative of freedom from attack, security Deut 33:28 (of Israel), Jer 49:31 (Kedar); soלְבָדָד‎ Num 23:9 (Israel) Micah 7:14; Psalm 4:9 for thou תּוֺשִׁיבֵנִי לָבֶטַח לְבָדָד‎ makest me dwell solitarily, in safety (see Deut 33:28).
