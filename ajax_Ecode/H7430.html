רָמַשׂ‎ (râmas｜raw-mas'｜verb｜creep)
[Heb] רָמַשׂ‎ ETCBC: רמשׂ‎ (verb｜creep) OSHL: t.dc.aa TWOT: 2177 GK: H8253 Greek: κινέω
Derivation: a primitive root;
Strong's: properly, to glide swiftly, i.e. to crawl or move with short steps; by analogy to swarm
KJV: creep, move.

H7430. ramas

רָמַשׂ‎ verb creep, move lightly, move about (chiefly P) (Arabic touch Gently (Kam Frey); Late Hebrew רְמָשִׂים‎ creeping things (rare)); —

Qal Imperfect3feminine singular תִּרָמשֹׁ‎ Gen 9:2 2t.; Participle רֹמֵשׂ‎ 1:26 +; feminine singular רֹמֶ֫שֶׂת‎ Lev 11:46 2t.; — 1

subject ground, ת אֲשֶׁר ׳כֹּל הָאֲדָמָה‎ all with which the ground creeps (teems), i.e. all creeping things, Leviticus 20:55 (H), Gen 9:2 (P).

2 elsewhere subject animal:

a. creep, עַלהָֿאֲדָמָה‎ Deut 4:18; Gen 7:8 (P), עַלהָֿאָרֶץ‎ Lev 11:44 (H), Gen 1:30 (P); עַלהָֿאֲדָמָה הָרֹמֵשׂ הָרֶמֶשׂ‎ Ezek 38:20, עַלהָֿאָרֶץ‎ Gen 1:26; 7:14; 8:17 (all P).

b. move lightly, glide about, of water animals, כָּלנֶֿפֶשׂ‎ (בַּמַּיִם‎) הָרֹמֶצֶת הַחַיָּה‎ Lev 11:46 (H), Gen 1:21 (P), compare Psa 69:35.

c. in General move about, of all land-animals, עַלהָֿאָרֶץ‎ Gen 1:28; 7:21; 8:19 (all P); specifically of wild beasts prowling at night Psa 104:20.
