כָּרָה‎ (kârâh｜kaw-raw'｜verb｜get by trade｜give a feast)
[Heb] כָּרָה‎ ETCBC: כרה‎ (verb｜purchase) OSHL: k.da.aa, k.cz.aa TWOT: 1034 GK: H4126
Derivation: usually assigned as a primitive root, but probably only a special application of כָּרָה‎ (through the common idea of planning implied in a bargain);
Strong's: to purchase
KJV: buy, prepare.

H3739. karah

II. [כָּרָה‎] verb get by trade, trade (Late Hebrew כִּירָה‎ act of buying, purchase (so 'in den Küstenländern,' LevyNWB ii. 323 f.); Arabic let for hire); — only

Qal Imperfect1singular suffix וָאֶכְּרֶהָ‎ Hosea 3:2 (dagesh forte dirimens, si vera punctat., Ges§ 20. 2, b; but compare We); 3 masculine plural יִכְרוּ‎ Job 40:30; 2masculine plural תִּכְרוּ‎ Deut 2:6; Job 6:27; — get by trade, buy, with accusative Hosea 3:2; Deut 2:6; followed by -עַל‎ trade in, make trade of Job 6:27; Job 40:30.

III. [כָּרָה‎] verb give a feast (literally perhaps bring (guests), invite; compare Assyrian karû, bring, kirêtu, feast, kireti iškun, he gave a feast, Dl :HWB 352); — only

Qal Imperfect. with accusative of congnate meaning with verb, גְדוֺלָהוַיִּכְרֶה כֵּרָה לָהֶם‎ 2Kin 6:23 and he gave a great feast for them (the context requires this sense, but text dubious, NöZMG 1886, 724, compare Klo).
