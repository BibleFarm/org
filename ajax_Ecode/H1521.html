H1521. Gichon

גִּיחוֺן‎ proper name, of a river (a bursting forth) —

1 one of the rivers of Eden Gen 2:13 (on theories of identity see Commentaries; also SmithDict. Bible Schaff-HerzogArt. Eden SpurrellText of Gen. on the passage RiHWB).

2 spring of water near Jerusalem: גִּחוֺן‎ 1Kin 1:33, 38, 45, הָעֶלְיוֺן גִיחוֺן מֵימֵי‎2Chr 32:30, בַּנַּחַל לְגִיחוֺן‎33:14; — there are two main theories as to locality:

a. west of Jerusalem, connected with Birket Mamilla, and aqueduct into city RobBR i. 239, 345 ff. SurveyJerusalem and others;

b. east of Jerusalem = Fountain of the Virgin, FurrerSchenkel BL ii. 483 BdPal 101, or Siloah water-system GutheZPV, v., 1882, 359 ff.
