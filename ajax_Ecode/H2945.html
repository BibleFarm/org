טַף‎ (ṭaph｜taf｜noun｜masculine｜children)
[Heb] טַף‎ ETCBC: טַף‎ (subs｜[those unable to march]) OSHL: i.bj.ab TWOT: 821a GK: H3251, H3252 Greek: ἔκγονον, λαός, νήπιος, ὄχλος, παιδίον, συγγένεια, σῶμα, τέκνον
Derivation: from טָפַף‎ (perhaps referring to the tripping gait of children);
Strong's: a family (mostly used collectively in the singular)
KJV: (little) children (ones), families.

H2945. taph

טַף‎42 noun masculine collective children (as going with quick, tripping steps; Ethiopic Di1251) — טַף‎ Jer 41:16 11t.; טָ֑ף‎ 40:7 5t.; suffix טַמֵּנוּ‎ Gen 43:8 5t.; טַמְּכֶם‎ 45:19 10t.; טַמָּם‎ 34:29 6t.; never construct and never plural (in 47:24 omitted by ᵐ5‎, compare Di); — children, little ones, 34:29 (E) + 18 t. J E (Genesis Exodus Numbers), also Deut 1:39 8t. D (including Josh 1:14; 8:35); P only Num 31:9, 17, 18, where note בַּטָּ֑ף זָכָר‎ 31:17 = young boys, and בַּנָּשִׁים הַטַף‎ 31:18 = young girls; also Judg 18:21; 21:10; 2Sam 15:22; Jer 40:7; 41:16; 43:6; Ezek 9:6; 2Chr 20:13; 31:18; Ezra 8:21; Est 3:13; 8:11; the words וְהַטָ֑ף וְנָשִׁים מְתִים‎ Deut 2:34; 3:6 including all inhabitants; so וְהַטָּף וְהַנָּשִׁים אֲנָשִׁים‎ 31:12; Jer 40:7; 43:6; טַף‎ distinguished from וּבְּתוּלָה בָּחוּר‎ Ezek 9:6; also from בְּנֵיהֶם‎2Chr 20:13, and from וּבְנוֺתֵיהֶם בְנֵיהֶם‎31:18. **Note that the word includes (or implies) women as well as children, in following: Gen 47:12; Exod 12:37 (E), 10:10; 10:24 (J), Num 32:16; 32:17 (J E; compare Di on 32:26).
