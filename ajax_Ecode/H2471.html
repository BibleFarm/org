H2471. challah

חַלָּה‎ noun feminine a kind of cake (probably as perforated, see Di Lev 2:4) — ׳ח‎ absolute Num 15:20; Lev 24:5; construct חַלַּת‎ Exod 29:23 4t.; plural חַלּוֺת‎ Lev 2:4 2t.; חַלֹּת‎ Exod 29:2-3t. — used in offerings:

1 at the sacrif. of David on removal of ark, given to each person as part of peace-offering 2Sam 6:19.

2 elsewhere only P (H Lev 24:5 (twice in verse)):

a. of the 12 cakes of the table of the holy place, made of סלת‎ Lev 24:5 (twice in verse).

b. made of ערסת ראשׁית‎ as an offering of first-fruits Num 15:20.

c. of שׁמן לחם‎, to be burnt with the עוֺלָה‎ Exod 29:23 = Lev 8:26.

d. of מַצָּה‎ burnt on the altar with

c. Lev 8:26, but usually to be eaten by participants in the several forms of peace- offering Num 6:19, and such mingled with oil Exod 29:2; Lev 2:4; 7:12 (twice in verse); Num 6:15.

e. ׳ח חמץ לחם‎ to be used by the offerer in connection with the unleavened cakes of

d. Lev 7:13.
