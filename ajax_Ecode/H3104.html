יֹובֵל‎ (yôwbêl｜yo-bale'｜noun｜masculine｜ram)
[Heb] יֹובֵל‎, יׄובֵל‎ ETCBC: יֹובֵל‎ (subs｜ram) OSHL: j.ah.ah TWOT: 835e GK: H3413 Greek: ἄφεσις, ἱερός, σάλπιγξ
Derivation: or יֹבֵל‎; apparently from יָבַל‎;
Strong's: the blast of a horn (from its continuous sound); specifically, the signal of the silver trumpets; hence, the instrument itself and the festival thus introduced
KJV: jubile, ram's horn, trumpet.

H3104. yobel

יֹבֵל יוֺבֵל,‎ noun masculineNum 36:4 ram, ram's horn, cornet (Phoenician יבל‎ ram; compare Di Lev 25:10 DlPr 124) — absolute יוֺבֵל‎ Josh 6:5 7t.; יֹבֵל‎ Exod 19:13 13t.; plural יוֺבְלִים‎ Josh 6:4 2t.; יֹבְלִים‎ 6:13; —

1 ram, only in combination: הַיּ בְּקֶרֶן ׳בִּמְשֹׁךְ‎ Josh 6:5 the ram's horn, as wind-instrument; so שׁוֺפְרוֺת(ה)י(ו)בלים‎ 6:4, 6, 8, 13 rams' horns (see BenzArchäol. 276); יֹבֵל‎ alone, הַיֹּבֵל בִּמְשֹׁךְ‎ Exod 19:13 at the sounding of the ram ('s horn).

2 designation of 50th year, marked by blowing of cornets, AV 'jubile' (so Late Hebrew יובֵילָא ᵑ7 יוֺבֵל,‎, as loan-word); originally no doubt הַיּוֺבֵל שְׁנַת‎ year of the ram ('s horn), as Lev 25:13, 28, 40, 50, 52, 54 (all H), 27:17-18, 23, 24 (all P), but then, without שׁנת‎, as לָכֶם תִּהְיֶה הִוא יוֺבֵל‎ 25:10 a ram ('s horn blowing) shall it be to you; so 25:11; 25:12; 25:15; 25:28; 25:30; 25:31; 25:33 (all H), 27:18; Num 36:4 (both P).
