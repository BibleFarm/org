H3474. yashar

יָשַׁר‎ verb be smooth, straight, right (Late Hebrew id., and derivatives; Arabic be Gentle, tractable easy, II. make easy; manageable, easy, but Sabean יתֿרן אהל הותֿר, ותֿר,‎ OsZMG 1865, 270 CISiv, 29, 2; Assyrian Išâu, be or go straight, right, mêšêru, justice, mêšêriš, righteously, DlHWB 310) —

Qal Perfect יָשַׁר‎ Jer 18:4 2t.; 3 feminine יָשְׁרָה‎ Judg 14:3; Hab 2:4; 3masculine plural יָָֽשְׁרוּ‎ 1Kin 9:12; Imperfect יִישַׁר‎ Num 23:27 2t.; יִשַׁר‎ 1Sam 18:20, 26; 3feminine singularתִּישַׁר‎ Judg 14:7; 3feminine plural יִשַּׁרְנָה‎ 1Sam 6:12 (Ges§ 47, n. 3; 71) —

1 go straight, בַּדֶּרֶךְ‎ in the way 1Sam 6:12.

2 figurative, be pleasing, agreeable, right (בְּעֵינֵי‎ in the yees of); said of a woman Judg 14:3, 7; a man Jer 27:5; of matters and things (הַדָּבָר‎) 1Sam 18:20, 26; 2Sam 17:4; 1Chr 13:4; 2Chr 30:4; of cities 1Kin 9:12; a place הָאֱלֹהִים בְּעֵינֵי‎ Num 23:27 (E); with כַּאֲשֶׁר‎ Jer 18:4.

3 in ethical sense, straightforward, upright; בּוֺ נַפְשׁוֺ לֹאיָָֽשְׁרָה‎ Hab 2:4 his soul is not upright in him (opposed to עֻמְּלָה‎ swollen).

1 make smooth, straight, accusative מְסִלָּה‎ Isa 40:3; הֲדוּרִים‎ 45:2; figurative, with דֶּרֶךְ‎ make smooth or straight, i.e. free from obstacles, successful 45:13; Prov 11:5, אֹרַח‎ 3:5; in ethical sense, אָרְחוֺתָם הַמְיַשְּׁרִים‎ 9:15 those making straight their paths, going straightforward on their paths; יְיַשֶּׁרלָֿ֑כֶת‎ 15:21 maketh straight his going, goeth straightforwards.
