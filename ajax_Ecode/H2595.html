H2595. chanith

חֲנִית‎ noun feminine spear (as flexible? see Thes); — absolute חֲנִית‎ 2Sam 13:19 26t.; construct id. 1Sam 26:16 (so 26:22 Qr, where Kt has article); suffix חֲנִיתָ֑ךָ‎ Hab 3:11; חֲנִיתוֺ‎ 1Sam 17:7 (twice in verse) + 11 t. + 2Sam 23:8, see below; plural חֲנִיתִים‎2Chr 23:9; חֲנִיתוֺתֵיהֶם‎ Isa 2:4 = חֲנִיתֹתֵיהֶם‎ Micah 4:3; —

1 spear, as hurled 1Sam 18:10-11, 19:9-10, (twice in verse); 20:33; held by king 22:6; sign of king's presence 26:7-8, 11, 12, 16, 22 (see RSK 171); also 2Sam 1:6; 2:23; 21:19; 23:18, 21 (3 t. in verse); 1Chr 11:11 = 2Sam 23:8, where read אֶתחֲֿנִיתוֺ עוֺרֵר הוּא‎ (for הָעֶצְנִו֯ עֲדִינוֺ הוּא‎) according to Ew Th Ke Klo Dr (not We), 1Chr 11:20, 23 (3 t. in verse); plural 2Chr 23:9; often || חֶרֶב‎ 1Sam 13:19, 22; 17:45, 47; 21:9; Isa 2:4 = Micah 4:3; Nahum 3:3; || שְׁלָטִים‎ 2Kin 11:10; || צִנָּה‎ 1Chr 12:35 (Baer); מַסָּע‎ Job 41:18; סְגֹד‎ Psa 35:3; || קֶשֶׁת‎ 46:10.

2. a. shaft of spear is ח ׳עֵץ‎ 2Sam 23:7; 1Chr 20:5 + 1Sam 17:7 Qr (doubtless right; Kt חץ‎).

b. spear-head ח ׳לַהֶבֶת‎ 1Sam 17:7, compare ח ׳לַהַב‎ Job 39:23; ח ׳בְּרַק‎ Nahum 3:3; Hab 3:11.

c. butt of spear ח ׳אַחֲרֵי‎ 2Sam 2:23.

3 metaphor of teeth of lions Psalm 2Sam 57:5 (|| חצים‎; figurative of Psalmist's enemies).
