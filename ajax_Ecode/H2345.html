חוּם‎ (chûwm｜khoom｜adjective｜darkened)
[Heb] חוּם‎ ETCBC: חוּם‎ (adjv｜ruttish) OSHL: h.bh.ab TWOT: 625a GK: H2569 Greek: λευκός, ποικίλος
Derivation: from an unused root meaning to be warm, i.e. (by implication)
Strong's: sunburnt or swarthy (blackish)
KJV: brown.

H2345. chum

חוּם‎ adjective darkened, dark brown or black, only of colour of sheep (in Genesis 30, J); — בכשׂבים וכלשֶֿׂהחֿוּם‎ Gen 30:32, בכ ׳וְחוּם‎ 30:33, 35, בְּצאֹן וכלחֿוּם‎ 30:40 (clause probably interpolated Ol Hup De We Di).
