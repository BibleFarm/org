חוּט‎ (chûwṭ｜khoot｜noun｜masculine｜thread)
[Heb] חוּט‎ ETCBC: חוּט‎ (subs｜thread) OSHL: h.be.ab TWOT: 621a GK: H2562
Derivation: from an unused root probably meaning to sew;
Strong's: a string; by implication, a measuring tape
KJV: cord, fillet, line, thread.

H2339. chut

חוּט‎ noun masculineJosh 2:18 thread, cord, line (Late Hebrew id.; Arabic , Aramaic , חוּטָא‎) — ׳ח‎ absolute Gen 14:23 2t.; construct Josh 2:18+ 3t.; —

1 thread, as easily broken Judg 16:12 (simile); as composing a rope or cord; ח ׳תִּקְוַת הַזֶּה הַשָּׁנִי‎ Josh 2:18 (JE) this cord of scarlet thread (literally the cord of this thread of scarlet); probably also נַעַל שְׂרוֺךְ וְעַד מִחוּט‎ Gen 14:23 from a thread to a sandal-thong; in simile הַשָּׁנִי ׳כְּח‎ Song 4:3 like a thread of scarlet are thy lips.

2 cord, ׳הַח הַמְּשֻׁלָּשׁ‎ Eccl 4:12 a three-fold cord.

3 line, as measure of length 1Kin 7:15 and a line of twelve cubits surrounded (i.e. would surround) it (compare קָו‎ 7:23), so Jer 52:21.
