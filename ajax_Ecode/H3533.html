H3533. kabash

[כָּבַשׁ‎] verb subdue, bring into bondage (Late Hebrew id., press, oppress; Aramaic כְּבַשׁ‎, tread down, beat or make a path, subdue; Arabic press, squeeze, knead (body or limb, as in the bath, message), also attack, assault) —

Qal Perfect3plural וְכָֽבְשׁוּ‎ consecutive Zech 9:15; Imperfect יִכְבּוֺשׁ‎ Micah 7:19; וַיִּכְבְּשׁוּם‎ Jer 34:11 Qr (Kt Hiph), etc.; Imperative plural suffix כִּבְשֻׁהָ‎ Gen 1:28; Infinitive כְּבוֺשׁ‎2Chr 28:10; Est 7:8; Participle כֹּבְשִׁים‎ Neh 5:5; —

1 bring into bondage, followed by accusative + לַעֲבָדִים‎ Jer 34:11, 16; 2Chr 28:10; Neh 5:5.

2 (late) subdue, force, a woman Est 7:8 (= earlier עִנָּה‎).

3 subdue, dominate, the earth Gen 1:28 (P), Zech 9:15 (of conquest); tread down, followed by אַבְנֵיקֶֿלַע‎; figurative subdue, followed by עֲוֺנֹתֵינוּ‎ Micah 7:19.

Niph`al Perfect3feminine singular נִכְבְּשָׁה‎ Josh 18:1 3t.; Participle feminine plural נִכְבָּשׁוֺת‎ Neh 5:5; — passive of Qal 1, subject בְּנוֺתֵינוּ‎ 5:5; of Qal 3, subject הָאָרֶץ‎ (of conquest) Num 32:22, 29; Josh 18:1 (all P), 1Chr 22:18.

Pi`el Perfect כִּבֵּשׁ‎ 2Sam 8:11, object גּוֺיִם‎ (of conquest).

Hiph`il Imperfect bring into bondage, followed by accusative + לַעֲבָדִים‎ Jer 34:11 Kt (Qr Qal).
