Derivation: or (shortened); אַתָּ‎; or אַת‎; feminine (irregular) sometimes אַתִּי‎; plural masculine אַתֶּם‎; feminine אַתֶּן‎; or אַתֵּנָה‎
Typo: lemma אתֵּנָה missing vowel, corrected to אַתֵּנָה‎
; or אַתֵּנָּה‎; a primitive pronoun of the second person;
Strong's: thou and thee, or (plural) ye and you
KJV: thee, thou, ye, you.

אָ֑תָּה אַתָּ֫ה,‎ (so regularly; but 26-7 t., with different disjunctive accents, ׃אַ֑תָּה‎ see FrMM 228; Sta§ 178 a (read 8 for 18)

pronoun 2 singular masculine thou (for anta, see above; compare נָתַתָּ‎ for נָתַנְתָּ‎) Gen 3:11, 19 +?אַתָּ‎ 1Sam 24:19; Psa 6:4; Job 1:10; Eccl 7:22; Neh 9:6. Appended to a verb for emphasis, Exod 18:19 (twice in verse); 1Sam 17:56 אַתָּה שְׁאַל‎ inquire thou, 20:8; 22:18; Isa 43:26 אַתָּה סַמֵּר‎. Added for the purpose of strengthening a Genitive or accusative suffix 1Kin 21:19; Prov 22:19 (Ges§ 135.2).

אתי‎, i.e. אַתִּי‎, the older & more original form of אַתְּ‎ thou (feminine), preserved, probably dialectically, 7 t. in Kt, Judg 17:2; 1Kin 14:2; 2Kin 4:16, 23; 8:1; Jer 4:30; Ezek 36:13. (V. above as in Syriac, the י‎ may not have been fully sounded: the Masoretes direct אַתְּ‎ to be everywhere read.)

אָ֑תְּ אַתְּ,‎ pronoun 2 singular feminine thou (feminine) (shortened from אַתִּי‎ (q. v.); in Syriac the two Genders are written differently, , , but, the not being sounded, are pronounced alike: in ᵑ7‎ both are written אַתְּ‎ or אַנְתְּ‎) Gen 12:11, 13; 24:23 +? Num 11:15; Deut 5:24; Ezek 28:14 used as a masculine (as in Aramaic of ᵑ7‎); but probably אַתָּ‎ (see below אַתָּה‎) should be here read.

אַתֶּם‎ pronoun 2 masculine plural you (masculine) (for antem, see above) Gen 9:7; 26:27; 29:4 +?הָאַתֶּם הֲ,‎ Judg 6:31; following the verb for emphasis 15:12; Jer 34:15; construed inaccurately with a feminine Ezek 13:20.

אַתֵּן‎ Ezek 34:31 (many editions אַתֶּן‎), אַתֵּ֫נָה‎ Gen 31:6; Ezek 13:11, 20 (editions אַתֵּ֫נָּה‎) 34:17

pronoun 2 feminine plural you (feminine)
