בְּהֵמָה‎ (bᵉhêmâh｜be-hay-maw'｜noun｜feminine｜beast)
[Heb] בְּהֵמָה‎ ETCBC: בְּהֵמָה‎ (subs｜cattle) OSHL: b.al.ab TWOT: 208a GK: H989 Greek: θηρίον, κτῆνος, τετράπους
Derivation: from an unused root (probably meaning to be mute);
Strong's: properly, a dumb beast; especially any large quadruped or animal (often collective)
KJV: beast, cattle.

H929. behemah

בְּהֵמָה‎187 noun feminine beast, animal, cattle (Arabic ) — ׳בּ‎ Gen 1:24 137t.; construct בֶּהֱמַת‎ Num 3:41 (twice in verse) + 10 t.; suffix בְּהֶמְתְּךָ‎ Lev 19:19 4t.; בְּהֶמְתֶּ֑ךָ‎ Exod 20:10 4t.; בְּהֶמְתּוֺ‎ Gen 36:6; Prov 12:10; plural absolute בְּהֵמוֺת‎ Deut 32:24 6t. + Psa 73:22 (see below); construct בַּהֲמוֺת‎ 8:8 5t.; — beast, & collective beasts (Gen 8:1 + very often)

1 of living creatures other than man (אָדָם‎) Gen 8:1; Exod 8:13; 8:14; 9:9-10, 22; 22:18; Lev 18:23 (twice in verse) (where ׳בּ‎ is male, so 20:16) Deut 27:21; Psa 36:7 etc.; בּ וְעַד ׳מֵאָדָם‎ (׳בּ‎ including all the larger animals) Exod 9:25; 12:12; Psa 135:8; Jer 50:3 etc.; as inferior to man Job 18:3; Psa 49:13; Psalm 49:21, so also Eccl 3:18-19, (twice in verse); 3:21; opposed to also birds & reptiles Gen 6:7; 7:23; 8:17 compare Ezek 44:31 etc.; also to fishes 1Kin 5:13; Job 12:7 compare 12:8, Psa 8:9; Zeph 1:3.

2 opposed to also to wild beasts ׳ח הַשָּׂדֶה הָאָרֶץ, חַיַּת‎ Gen 1:24-25, 26; 2:20; 3:14; 7:14, 21; 9:10; Psa 50:10; 148:10; Isa 46:1 etc.; especially therefore cattle, as owned and used by man, Gen 47:18 (הַבּ ׳מִקְנֵה‎) Exod 20:10 = Deut 5:14; Lev 19:19; 26:22; Num 3:41 (twice in verse); 3:45 (twice in verse); Deut 2:35; 11:15; Jonah 4:11; Zech 2:8; 8:10; 14:15; Ezra 1:4, 6; Neh 9:37; 10:37 etc.; in one (late) passage of animal for riding (horse ? mule ?) 2:12 (twice in verse); 2:14.

3 rarely of wild beasts, especially carnivora, ׳בּ יַעַר‎ Micah 5:7; ׳בּ הָאָרֶץ‎ Deut 28:26; Isa 18:6 (twice in verse); Jer 7:33; 15:3; 16:4; 19:7; 34:20; ׳בּ הַשָּׂדֶה‎ 1Sam 17:44; alone, Prov 30:30; שֶׁןבְּֿהֵמוֺת‎ Deut 32:24; בּ ׳שֹׁד‎ Hab 2:17. — On בְּהֵמוֺת‎ Psa 73:22 compare below
