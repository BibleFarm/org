Derivation: a primitive root;
Strong's: to collect; hence, to enfold
KJV: gather (together), heap up, wrap self.

H3664. kanas

[כָּנַס‎] verb gather, collect (Late Hebrew id.; Aramaic כְּנַס‎ (see also כנשׁ‎); Ethiopic in derived conjugation assemble for worship, etc.; Arabic is sweep, sweep away, destroy) —

Qal (late) Perfect1singular כָּנַסְתִּי‎ Eccl 2:8; Imperative כְּנוֺס‎ Est 4:16; Infinitive construct 1Chr 22:2-3t.; Participle כֹּנֵס‎ Psa 33:7; — gather people 1Chr 22:2; Est 4:16; ׳כ תְּהוֺמוֺת בְּאוֺצָרוֺת נֹתֵן הַיָּ֑ם מֵי כַּנֵּד‎ Psa 33:7 (read perhaps, for כַּנּאֹד כַּנֵּד,‎ as in a wine skin, see נד נאד,‎); gather, collect portions of harvest for priests and Levites Neh 12:44; stones Eccl 3:5 (opposed to השׁליך‎); silver and gold = amass wealth 2:8; absolute 2:26 (|| אסף‎).

Pi`el Perfect1singular אֶתְכֶם וְכִנַּסְתִּ֫י‎ Ezek 22:21 and I will gather you together (for punishment; Co translates to 22:20 and reads for וְהִנַּחְתִּ֫י‎; || קבץ‎ 22:19; 22:20); of restoration אֶלאַֿדְמָתָם וְכִנַּסְתִּים‎ 39:28, and יְכַנֵּס‎ Psa 147:2.

Hithpa`el Infinitive construct צָרָה כְּהִתְכַּנֵּסוְהַמַּסֵּכָה‎ Isa 28:20 and the covering is (too) narrow when one gathers oneself together (|| הַמַּצָּע מֵהִשְׂתָּרֵעַקָצַר‎), i.e. the bed is too short to stretch oneself at full length, and when one (perforce) draws up the feet, the covering becomes too narrow.
