[מָּרָה‎] verb bear fruit, be fruitful (Late Hebrew = Biblical Hebrew 1, מְּרִי‎ = Biblical Hebrew; Phoenician פר‎ fruit; Ethiopic , blossom, bear fruit; Assyrian pir'u, posterity; compare also מֵּירָא ᵑ7‎ Syriac fruit, and BaZMG xii (1887), 604): —

Qal Perfect3plural מָּרוּ‎ Exod 1:7; 1plural consecutive וּפָרִינוּ‎ Gen 26:22, etc.; Imperfect3masculine singular יִפְרֶה‎ Isa 11:1, etc.; Imperative masculine singular מְּרֵה‎ Gen 35:11, masculine plural מְּרוּ‎ 1:22 +; Participle active מֹּרֶה‎ Deut 29:17, feminine מֹּרִיָּה‎ Ezek 19:10; Isa 17:6, מֹּרָת‎ (Ges§ 80g; for *מֹּרַיַת‎ LagBN 81) Gen 49:22 (twice in verse); —

1 of men and animals, Exod 23:30 (E) Gen 26:22 (J); especially + רבה‎ Jer 3:16; 23:3; Ezek 36:11; Gen 35:11; 47:27 (P), and שָׁרַץ‎ (P) 8:17; 9:7; Exod 1:7, וגו וּמִלְאוּ וּרְבוּ ׳מְּרוּ‎ Gen 1:22, 28; 9:1.

2 of vine, מֹּרִיָּה גֶּפֶן‎ Isa 32:12 fruitful vine, so Psa 128:3 (simile of wife); compare (in figurative of Israel) Ezek 19:10; in Messianic prediction Isa 11:1 a shoot from his [Jesse's] roots shall bear fruit; with accusative פ ׳שֹׁרֶשׁ ראֹשׁ‎ figurative, Deut 29:17 a root bearing gall (as its fruit); יֶשַׁע יִפְרוּ‎ Isa 45:8 is dubious, read perhaps 3 feminine singular תֵּפֶר‎ let earth be fruitful in salvation (Oort and others); Participle f., as substantive, הַמֹּרַיָּה‎ the fruit-bearer, poetic for tree, 17:6 (compare [סָעִיף‎]

2); so מֹּרָת בֵּן‎ Gen 49:22 (twice in verse) Joseph is son of a fruit-bearer, i.e. a fruitful bough.
