H3791. kethab

כְּתָב‎ noun masculineEzra 4:7 writing (late Aramaic; Biblical Aramaic כְּתָב‎ Syriac ); — ׳כ‎ absolute 1Chr 28:19 4t.; construct Ezek 13:9 4t.; suffix כְּתָבָהּ‎ Est 1:22 2t.; כְּתָבָם‎ Ezra 2:62 3t.; — writing:

1 register, enrolement, Ezek 13:9; Ezra 2:62 = Neh 7:64.

2 = mode of writing, character, letter, ׳וּכ אֲרָמִית כָּתוּב הִנִּשְׁתְּוָן‎ Ezra 4:7 and the writing of the letter was written in Aramaic (characters; so probably, see BeRy Ryle, compare Late Hebrew HoffmZAW i.(1881), 334 ff.); compare Est 1:22; 3:12; 8:9 (twice in verse).

3 = letter, 2Chr 2:10; Est 9:27.

4 of a royal enactment, edict2Chr 35:4 (|| מִכְתָּב‎), Est 3:14; 8:8, 13; more fully כְּתָבהַֿדָּת‎ 4:8.

5 of a writing with divine authority 1Chr 28:19; ׳כ אֱמֶת‎ Dan 10:21, i.e. a book of truth.
