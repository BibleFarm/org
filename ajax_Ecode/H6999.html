קָטַר‎ (qâṭar｜kaw-tar'｜adjective｜incense｜incense-altar｜make sacrifices smoke)
[Heb] קָטַר‎, מֻקְטָר‎, מְקַטֶּרֶת‎ ETCBC: קטר‎ (verb｜smoke), מְקַטְּרֹות‎ (subs｜incense altars) OSHL: s.bg.ai, s.bg.ak, s.bg.af TWOT: 2011 2011e 2011g GK: H5231, H5232, H7787 Greek: ἀναφέρω, ἐπιτελέω, ἐπιτίθημι, θυμίαμα, θυμιάω, θύω, προσφέρω
Derivation: a primitive root (identical with through the idea of fumigation in a close place and perhaps thus driving out the occupants);
Strong's: to smoke, i.e. turn into fragrance by fire (especially as an act of worship)
KJV: burn (incense, sacrifice) (upon), (altar for) incense, kindle, offer (incense, a sacrifice).
