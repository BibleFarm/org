מִּילֶ֫גֶשׁ מִּלֶ֫גֶשׁ,‎37

noun feminine concubine (Late Hebrew id.; ᵑ7‎J מִּילַּקְתָּא (מַּלְקְתָא)‎, perhaps influenced by Greek παλλακή, παλλακίς (properly young girl), Latin pellex; originally Greek word according to StaG. i. 380, compare also LewyFremdw. 66f; on possibly Hittite origin see JenZMG xiviii (1894), 468 ff.); — ׳מִּ‎ absolute 2Sam 3:7, construct 21:11, ׳מִּי‎ absolute Gen 36:12; Judg 19:1, construct Gen 35:22 4t.; suffix מִּילַגְשׁוֺ‎ 22:24 +, שֵׁהוּ‎- Judg 19:24; שִׁי‎- 20:4 2t.; plural מִּלַגְשִׁים‎ 2Sam 5:13 2t., ׳מִּי‎ Gen 25:6 5t.; construct מִּלַגְשֵׁי‎ 2Sam 16:21-22, suffix מִּילַגְשָׁיו‎2Chr 11:21, מִּלַגְשֶׁיךָ‎ 2Sam 19:6, מִּלַגְשֵׁיהֶם‎ Ezek 23:20; —

1 concubine Gen 22:24; 25:6; 35:22; 36:12; Judg 8:31; 19:1 10t. 19:20; 2Sam 3:7 (twice in verse) + 7 t. 2Samuel; 1Kin 11:3; 1Chr 1:32 6t. Chronicles; Est 2:14; Song 6:8-9,.

2 either = paramour (perhaps contempt.; ὁ πάλλαξ = youth), or < concubinage Ezek 23:20 (figurative of Jerusalem doting on Babylon).
