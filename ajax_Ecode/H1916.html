H1916. hadom

הֲדֹם‎ noun masculine stool, footstool, always joined with רַגְלַיִם‎; only in poetry and late writings, (properly something cast down, low) — ׳ה‎ absolute Psa 110:1 (or construct before לְ‎, compare Ges§ 130, 1); construct Lam 2:1+ 4t. — footstool, never literal, usually of ׳י‎ Isa 66:1 ה וְהָאָרֶץ כִּסְאִי ׳הַשָּׁמַיִם רַגְלַי‎; elsewhere with reference to sanctuary ׳ה רַגְלָיו‎ (compare רַגְלַי מְקוֺם‎ 60:13) Lam 2:1, or perhaps of ark (as place on which God rests) Psa 99:5; 132:7, אֱלֹהֵינוּ בְּרִיתיֿ לַאֲרוֺן מְנוּחָה ׳בֵּית ׳וְלַה רַגְלֵי‎ 1Chr 28:2; of conquest of enemies of Messianic king by Yahweh's agency לְרַגְלֶיךָ הֲדֹם אֹיְבֶיךָ אָשִׁית‎ Psa 110:1.
