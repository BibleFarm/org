H584. anach

[אָנַח‎] verb only

Niph`al sigh, groan, mostly in poetry & late (Aramaic אֲנַח‎, Ethpe`el compare Assyrian [anâ—u], inh—u, sigh ZimBP 12, 30) — Perfect3feminine singular נֶאֶנְחָה‎ Lam 1:8; Joel 1:18; 3plural נֶאֶנְחוּ‎ Isa 24:7; Imperfect יֵאָנַח‎ Prov 29:2 etc.; Imperative הֵאָנַ֯ח֑‎ Ezek 21:11; Participle נֶאְנָ֑ךְ‎ 21:12; נֶאֱנָחָה‎ Lam 1:21 etc.; —

1 sigh, in token of grief Isa 24:7; Prov 29:2; Lam 1:4 (|| נוּגוֺתּ‎) 1:21; Ezek 21:11 (twice in verse); of Jerusalem Lam 1:8; mostly absolute but followed by אַלֿ‎ Ezek 9:4 (|| נֶאֱנָק‎); followed by עַלֿ‎ & אֱלֿ‎ 21:12.

2 in physical distress Lam 1:11 (|| לֶחֶם בִּקֵּשׁ‎); Exod 2:23 followed by מִן‎ by reason of (|| זָעַק‎).

3 groan of cattle (בְּהֵמָה‎) Joel 1:18.
