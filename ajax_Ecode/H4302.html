מַטָּע‎ (maṭṭâʻ｜mat-taw'｜noun｜masculine｜place)
[Heb] מַטָּע‎ ETCBC: מַטָּע‎ (subs｜planting) OSHL: n.db.ae TWOT: 1354c GK: H4760 Greek: φυτεία
Derivation: from נָטַע‎;
Strong's: something planted, i.e. the place (a garden or vineyard), or the thing (a plant, figuratively or men); by implication, the act, planting
KJV: plant(-ation, -ing).

H4302. matta

מַטָּע‎ noun masculine place, or act of planting; plantation; — absolute ׳מ‎ Ezek 34:29; construct מַטַּע‎ Isa 61:3; suffix מַטָּעוֺ‎ 60:21 (Kt; Qr מַטָּעַי‎); מַטָּעָהּ‎ Ezek 17:7, also 31:4, but read ᵐ5 מַטָּעֹה‎ Hi Co Berthol Toy; plural construct מַטָּעֵי‎ Micah 1:6; —

1 planting-place, כָ֑רֶם מַטָּעֵי‎ Micah 1:6 planting-place for a vineyard, so Ezek 31:4 (read מַטָּעֹה‎, see above); probably also ׳מ לְשֵׁם‎ < read ᵐ5 ׳מ שָׁלֹם‎ Co Berthol Toy i.e. peaceful (fruitful) planting-place; compare מ ׳עֲרֻגוֺת‎ 17:7 = beds where it was planted.

2 act of planting מ ׳נֵצֶר‎ Isa 60:21 shoot of his (my) planting (figurative of people).

3 ׳מ ׳י‎ Isa 61:3 plantation of ׳י‎ (id.).
