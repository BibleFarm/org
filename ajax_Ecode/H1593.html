H1593. gannah

גַּנָּה‎ noun feminine garden (compare foregoing) — גַּנָּה‎ Isa 1:30; 61:11; construct גִּנַּת‎ Song 6:11 3t.; suffix גַּנָּתוֺ‎ Job 8:16; plural גַּנֹּת‎ Num 24:6, גַּנּוֺת‎ Eccl 2:5-6t.; suffix גַּנּוֺתֵיכֶם‎ Amos 4:9; garden, orchard, 4:9 (|| זַיִת תְּאֵנָה, כֶּרֶם,‎), 9:14 (fruit-garden; || כֶּרֶם‎), Jer 29:5, 28; Eccl 2:5 (|| מַּרְדֵּס‎), אֱגוֺז גִּנַּת‎ Song 6:11 nut-garden; compare גִּנַּתבִּֿיתָן‎ Est 1:5; 7:7-8, in simile, of prosperous Israel Num 24:6 (poem in J E; עֲלַינָהָר׳ג‎), of chastised Israel Isa 1:30 (ג לָהּ אין מים ׳אשׁר‎), of Yahweh's blessing 61:11; in Job 8:16 עַלגַּֿנּוֺ‎ in figure of prosperity of wicked, as a luxuriant plant; gardens as places of idolatrous worship Isa 1:29 (|| אֵילִים‎), 65:3 (|| לְבֵנִים‎ ), 66:17 (i.e. groves).
