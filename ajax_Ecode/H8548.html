H8548. tamid

תָּמִיד‎ noun masculineDan 12:11 continuity (perhaps originally extent; Late Hebrew as Biblical Hebrew, Ecclus תמיד‎ Ecclesiasticus 45:14); — ׳ת‎ always absolute; —

1 earliest and oftenest as adverb, continually:

a. of going on without interruption = continuously, Hosea 12:7; Jer 6:7; Nahum 3:19; Isa 21:8; 49:16; 51:13 (+ כָּלהַֿיּוֺם‎), 52:5 (+ id.), 60:11 (|| וָלַיְלָח יַוֺמָם‎, 62:6 (+ וְכָלהַֿלַּיְלָה כָּלהַֿיּוֺם‎), 65:3; Obadiah 16; Hab 1:17; Deut 11:12; 1Kin 10:8 2Chr 9:7; 1Chr 16:11, 37; especially (sometimes hyperbole) in Psalms : Psa 16:8; 25:15; 34:2 (|| בְּכָלעֵֿת‎), 35:27; compare 40:17; 70:5; 38:18; 40:12; 50:8; 51:5; 69:24; 71:6; 71:14; 72:15; 73:23; 74:23; 105:4; 109:15; 109:19; 119:44 (+ לְעוֺלָם וֶָעך׃‎), Psalm 40:109; v<Bible/VERSE ONLY:119:117 (so also 16:5, for תומי ᵑ0‎, Bi Che; תּוֺסיף‎ Ol Bae); Prov 5:19 (|| בִּכָלעֵֿת‎), 6:21; 15:15; 28:14; also Ezek 38:8 (strike out ᵑ6‎ Co ToyHpt. Berthol), Lev 6:6 (P; of fire, || תִכְבֶּה לֹא‎), and Lev 46:14 (but ᵐ5 תּ ׳חֻקַּת‎ Co Berthol); also in ritual, 24:8 (H), compare Exod 25:30 (P; shew-bread), Lev 24:2-3, 4 (H), compare Exod 27:20 (P; of lamp), 28:29-30, 38.

b. of regular repetition: meals 2Sam 9:7, 10, 13; 2Kin 25:29 = Jer 52:33; journeys 2Kin 4:19; compare Num 9:16; Psa 71:3; of ritual: sacrifice, תָּמִיד לַיּוֺם‎ Exod 29:38; compare 1Chr 16:40; 23:31; 2Chr 24:14.

2 as substantive:

a. of uninterrupted continuity, ת ׳אַנְשֵׁי‎ Ezek 39:14 men of continuity, i.e. men continually employed for the purpose; הַתּ ׳לֶחֶם‎ Num 4:7 the bread of continuity, i.e. the bread that is always there, so ת ׳מַעֲרֶכֶת‎2Chr 2:3, and perhaps ת ׳חֲצֹצְרוֺת‎ 1Chr 16:6.

b. of regular repetition מ ׳אֲרֻחַת‎ 2Kin 25:30, i.e. a regular allowance, = Jer 52:34; especially of ritual: ת ׳קְטֹרֶת‎ Exod 30:8 (P); most often ת ׳עֹלַת‎ Ezek 46:15 (every morning), Exod 29:42 (morning and evening, so) Num 28:6; Ezra 3:5, so read also Num 28:3 (Di) for ת ׳עוֺלָה ᵑ0‎ (לַיּוֺם‎); הַת ׳עֹלַת‎ 28:10, 15, 23, 24, 31; 29:6, 11, 16, 19, 22, 25, 28, 31, 34, 38; Neh Neh 10:35; rarely ת ׳מִנְחַת‎ Num 4:16 (P), Neh 10:34; as appositive, ת ׳מִנְחָה‎ Lev 6:13 meal-offering as a regular observance (compare Ges§ 131, 2 (b) DaSynt.§ 29 (b); on text see Di, NowArchaeology ii. 124 f.), (and Num 28:3 ᵑ0‎, but see above)

c. (late) ׳הַת‎ alone = daily (morning and evening) burnt-offering (NowArchaeology ii. 222 f.) Dan 8:11-12, 13; 11:31; 12:11 (so Talmud, even in plural תְּמִידִין‎).
