הֶ הַּ , הַ, הֲ,‎ (on the different forms, see Ges§ 100. 4: on Deut 32:6, see הַל‎),

interrogative particle (Biblical Aramaic and הֲ ᵑ7‎, Arabic ), prefixed, as a rule, to the first word of a sentence (or clause).

1 in direct questions:

a. as a simple interrogative, where the answer expected is uncertain, Exod 2:7 הַאֵלֵךְ‎ shall I go and call thee a nurse ? 1Sam 23:11 הֲיַסְגִּרֻנִי‎ will the men of Keilah deliver me into his hand ? שָׁאוּל הֲיֵרֵד‎ will Saul come down ? Job 1:8; and frequently.

b. often in questions, expressed in a tone of surprise, or put rhetorically, to which a negative answer is expected (= Latin num ?): Gen 4:9 אָנֹכִי אָחִי הֲשֹׁמֵר‎ Amos I my brother's keeper ? 18:17 shall I hide from Abraham that which I am about to do ? 30:2; 50:19; Num 11:23 a Deut 4:33 עַם֗֗֗ הֲשָׁמַע‎ Did a people ever hear the voice of God speaking out of the midst of the fire, . . . and live ? 20:19 (read with ᵑ9 ᵑ6 ᵑ7 ᵐ5‎ Ew Ke Di etc. הֶאָדָם‎ for הָאָדָם‎), 2Sam 7:5 בַיִת תִּבְנֶהלִּֿי ַהאַתָּה‎ (altered in 1Chr 17:4 to the negative וג תִּבְנֶה אַתָּה ׳לֹא‎), 2Kin 6:22 ֗֗֗ שָׁבִיתָ הַאֲשֶׁר‎ those whom thou hast taken captive with thy sword and with thy bow, wilt thou smite ? Isa 28:24; 36:12; a 57:6; 58:5; Jer 15:12; 16:20; Amos 5:25; Psa 50:13; Job 8:11; 15:7-8, 11; 38:12, 16, 17, 22 etc.; before an infinitive absolute (Ew§ 328 a) Jer 7:9; Job 40:2 and probably Micah 2:7 (read הֶאָמוֺר‎: ). After a protasis, הִנֵההֲ֗֗֗‎ Num 22:38; 2Kin 7:2, 19; Jer 32:27; Ezek 17:10; after הֵן‎ Jer 3:1; Hag 2:12: compare after זוּלַי‎ Gen 24:5; after אִם‎ Job 14:14 הֲיִחְיֶה גֶּבֶר יָמוּת אִם‎ a man dieth, shall he live ? Occasionally, one or more words precede הֲ‎ (in the same clause) for special emphasis: 2Sam 7:7; Job 34:31 הֶאָמַר אֶלאֵֿל כִּי‎ for to God did one ever say. . .. Neh 13:27; Jer 22:15 (הֲלֹא‎), compare 23:26.

c. it is used in questions which, by seeming to make doubtful what cannot be denied, have the force of an impassioned or indignant affirmation: Gen 27:36 ('dubitantis speciem prae se fert Esavus, ut eo acerbius affirmet,' Maurer, cited by Ges), 1Sam 2:27 וגי נִגְלֵיתִי הֲנִגְלהֹ‎ Did I reveal myself to the house of thy father, when they were in Egypt ? etc. (i.e. of course I did, although thy sons, by their actions, appear to belie it), 1Kin 16:31 (expressing astonishment), 21:19 וְגַםיָֿרָ֑שְׁתָּ הֲרָצַחְתָּ‎ Hast thou slain, and also taken possession ? Jer 31:20; Job 20:4; 41:1 (compare Hi); also Gen 16:13, and the phrases הַרְּאִיתֶם‎ do ye see ? 1Sam 10:24; 2Kin 6:32; הֲרָאִיתָ‎ dost thou see ? 1Kin 20:13; Ezek 8:12, 15, 17 and elsewhere

d. in disjunctive questions, the first question being introduced usually by הֲ‎, the second by אִם‎ or (more rarely) וְאִם‎: see examples under אִם‎

2 (p. 50). The disjunctive question may express a real alternative (as Josh 5:13), or (as especially in poetry) the same thought may be repeated in a different form, in two parallel clauses (e.g. Isa 10:15): in the latter case, the answer No is usually expected. Only very rarely is the second question introduced by הֲ‎ Judg 14:15 (where, however, הֲלֹם‎ ought no doubt to be read, with MSS., ᵑ7‎ Be and others, for הֲלֹא‎), or אוֺ‎ 18:19; Job 16:3; 38:28, 31.

2 In indirect interrogation, whether (German ob. . . .?) —

a. singly, after verbs of seeing Gen 8:8 הֲ לִרְאוֺת‎ to see whether, 18:21; Exod 4:18; Song 6:11, telling Gen 43:6, trying Deut 13:4, knowing Judg 3:4; Eccl 3:21 (read הַעֹלָה‎ and הֲיֹרֶדֶת‎: see De or Wr on the passage.)

b. disjunctively, — usually אִם ֗֗֗ הֲ‎, Num 13:18 end, 13:19a (and see the land) ֗֗֗ רָעָה אִם הִיא הֲטוֺבָה‎ whether it be good or bad, 13:19 b; 13:20; 13:20; לֹא אִם ֗֗֗ הֲ‎ Gen 24:21; 27:21; 37:32; Exod 16:4 that I may try it (the people) לֹא אִם בְּתוֺרָתִי הֲיֵלֵךְ‎ whether it will walk in my law or not, Num 11:23; Deut 8:2; Judg 2:22; הֲ ֗֗֗ הֲ‎ Num 13:18 (by side of אִם ֗֗֗ הֲ‎); אוֺ ֗֗֗ הֲ‎ Eccl 2:19; 11:6. It is prefixed to other particles, as הֲלֹא הֲכִי, הֲגַם, הַאַף, הַאִם,‎: see אַף אִם,‎,etc. p. 49, etc.
