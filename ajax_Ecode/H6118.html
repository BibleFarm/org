עֵקֶב‎ (ʻêqeb｜ay'-keb｜adverb｜consequence)
[Heb] עֵקֶב‎ ETCBC: עֵקֶב‎ (subs｜end) OSHL: p.eu.ah TWOT: 1676e GK: H6813 Greek: ἀνταπόδοσις, πλήν
Derivation: from עָקַב‎ in the sense of עָקֵב‎;
Strong's: a heel, i.e. (figuratively) the last of anything (used adverbially, for ever); also result, i.e. compensation; and so (adverb with preposition or relatively) on account of
KJV: [idiom] because, by, end, for, if, reward.

H6118. eqeb

עֵ֫קֶב‎ noun [masculine] consequence, usually as

adverb accusative as a consequence of, because (that) also reward, end (see below עָקַב‎; and cf, heel figurative consequence, result); —

1 Isa 5:23 שֹׁחַד עֵקֶב רָשָׁע מַצְדִּיקֵי‎ (adverb accusative) in consequence of a bribe. Psa 40:16 (= 70:4) בָּשְׁתָּם עֵקֶב על‎ (pleon.) according to the consequence of their shame, i.e. in consequence of the disgrace falling upon them. Hence as

conjunction אֲשֶׁר עֵקֶב‎ as a consequence of (the fact) that, because, Gen 22:18 ע ׳בְּקֹלִי שָׁמַעְתָּ אֲשֶׁר‎ 26:5; 2Sam 12:6; so כִּי עֵקֶב‎ 12:10; Amos 4:12 ׳ע לך אעשׂה זאת כִּי‎; עֵקֶב‎ alone, Num 14:24, and (followed by imperfect) Deut 7:12 ׳ע תשׁמעון‎ in consequence of your hearkening, etc., 8:20.

2 consequence = gain, reward, Psa 19:12 רָב עֵקֶב בְּשָׁמְרָם‎, Prov 22:4 י (וְ)יִרְאַת עֲנָוָה ׳עֵקֶב וגו ׳עשֶׁר‎.

3 end (of time), adverb accusative, Psa 119:33 ע ׳וְאֶצֳּרֶנָּה‎ to the end, 119:112 ׳ע לְעוֺלָם‎ (compare Ecclus 16:3; עִקְבָּא ᵑ7‎ (rare)).
