H2554. chamas

[חָמַס‎] verb treat violently, wrong (Late Hebrew id., act violently, treat violently; Aramaic חֲמַס‎ (rare) violently seize; Arabic is be hard, strict, rigorous) —

Qal Perfect3plural חָֽמְסוּ‎ Ezek 22:26; Zeph 3:4; Imperfect יַחְמֹס‎ Job 15:33, וַיַּחְמֹס‎ Lam 2:6; תַּחְמֹ֑סוּ‎ Job 21:27; Jer 22:3; Participle חֹמֵס‎ Prov 8:36; — treat violently, wrong; —

1 of physical wrong: ׳תַּחְ‎ Jer 22:3 (|| תֹּנוּ‎) of wrong to widows and orphans; ׳וַיַּח שֻׂכּוֺ כַּגַּן‎ Lam 2:6 and hath done violence to his pavilion as to a garden (|| מֹעֲדוֺ שִׁחֵת‎); so, figurative, of vine, wrong, i.e. fail to nourish, kill בִּסְרוֺ ׳יַח כַּגֶּפֶן‎ Job 15:33.

2 of ethical wrong, object תּוֺרָה‎ Zeph 3:4; Exod 22:26 (both || קֹדֶשׁ חִלֵּל‎); compare תַּחְמֹ֑סוּ עָלֵי מְזִמּוֺת‎ Job 21:27 the devices (wherewith) ye do me violence.

3 both physical and ethical נַפְשׁוֺ חֹמֵס חֹטְאִי‎ Prov 8:36.

Niph`al Perfect3plural נֶחְמְסוּ‎ Jer 13:22 thy heels suffer violence (|| שׁוּלַיִךְ נִגְלוּ‎).
