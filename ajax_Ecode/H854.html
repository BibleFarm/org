אֵת‎ (ʼêth｜ayth｜preposition｜with)
[Heb] אֵת‎, מֵאֵת‎ ETCBC: אֵת‎ (prep｜together with) OSHL: a.gn.aa TWOT: 187 GK: H907, H4425 Greek: ὀπίσω
Derivation: probably from אָנָה‎;
Strong's: properly, nearness (used only as a preposition or an adverb), near; hence, generally, with, by, at, among, etc.
KJV: against, among, before, by, for, from, in(-to), (out) of, with. Often with another prepositional prefix.

H854. eth

II. אֵת‎, preposition with — with makkeph אֶתֿ‎, with suffix אִתְּכֶם אִתְּךָ, אִתִּי,‎ etc. (also, however, אוֺתוֺ אוֺתְךָ,‎, and similarly מֵאוֺתוֺ מֵאוֺתְךָ,‎ etc.), first in Josh 10:25; 14:12, next 2Sam 24:24; then repeatedly (but not exclusively) 1 Kings 20 — 2 Kings 8, & in Jeremiah, Ezekiel, e.g. 1Kin 20:25 (but 20:23 אִתָּם‎) 22:7-8, 24 (beside מֵאִתִּי‎) 2Kin 1:15; 3:11-12, 26; 6:16 (beside מֵאִתּוֺ‎) 8:8; Jer 2:35; 10:5; 16:8; 19:10; 20:11; Ezek 2:6; 10:17; 23:23; 37:26 (see below 1d; also Isa 59:21, contr. Gen 17:4: on אֹתָהּ שָׁכַב‎ 34:2 and elsewhere, see below שָׁכַב‎, & compare DrSm ii. 13, 14) preposition denoting

proximity (synonym עִם‎; Phoenician את‎, e.g. CISi. 3, 8 רפאם את משכב לם יכן לא‎ let there not be for them a resting-place with the shades; Assyrian itti (perhaps akin to ittu 'side,' DlPr 115 Hpt KAT2. 498; but compare NöZMG '86, 738 f.). Not found as yet in the other cognate languages: but compare Ethiopic 'enta, towards, which supports the view; that אֵת‎ is for 'int [compare תִּתִּי תֵּת,‎], perhaps from √ אנה‎ to meet Olp. 431 PrätZMG '73, 643 LagM i. 226).

1 Of companionship, together with: Gen 6:13 behold, I destroy them אֶתהָֿאָרֶץ‎ together with the earth, 11:31; 12:4 +?verbs of dwelling, abiding, going, etc., as Judg 1:3; 14:11; 19:4, & in the phrase אִתּוֺ אֲשֶׁר הָעָם‎ 4:13; 7:1; 9:33, 48; 1Sam 14:20; 30:4 etc.; thou, and thy sons ֗֗֗ ׃ אִתָּֽךְ‎ with thee Gen 6:18; similarly (3rd person) 7:7, 13; 8:18; 9:8 and elsewhere (character of P: DrIntr 124); אֶתהָֿאֱלֹהִים הִתְהַלֵּךְ‎ to walk with God, i.e. to have him as a companion (that is, by adopting a course of life pleasing to him) 5:22, 24; 6:9 (compare אֵת הִתְהַלֵּךְ‎ literal 1Sam 25:15); — by the side of, like Isa 45:9, equally with Lev 26:39, in common with Jer 23:28b (compare עִם‎ 1e, f). Hence, in particular —

a. with for the purpose of help: Num 14:9 ׳וי אִתָּנוּ‎, Josh 14:12 (אוֺתִי‎, as Jer 20:11) Judg 1:19; 2Kin 6:16; 9:32 מִי אִתִּי מִי‎ who is on my side, who ? אֲנִי אִתְּךָ כִּי‎ Isa 43:5; Jer 1:18-19, +; Isa 63:3; Psa 12:5 our lips are with us, on our side; in the phrase מּ יַד ׳(הָֽיְתָה) אֵת‎ 2Sam 14:19; 2Kin 15:19; Jer 26:24; אֵת נָשָׂא‎ to bear together with, i.e. to assist Exod 18:22; Num 11:17. Exceptionally, = with the help of: Gen 4:1 for I have gotten a man ׳אֶתיֿ‎ with the help of ׳י‎ (compare עם‎ 1Sam 14:45) Gen 49:25 (where, however, the parallelism, & ⅏ ᵑ6 ᵑ7‎ favour שַׁדַּי וְאֵל‎ for שַׁדַּי וְאֵת‎) Micah 3:8; compare Est 9:29.

b. beside (German neben): Gen 39:6 מְאוּמָה אִתּוֺ יָדַע לֹא‎ he knew not with him, beside him, aught (i.e. Joseph managed everything), 39:8; Exod 20:23 אִתִּ֑י תַעֲשׂוּן לֹא‎ ye shall not make (aught) beside me.

c. beside = in the presence of (rare): Gen 20:16b and before all thou shalt be righted, Isa 30:8; Micah 6:1. In this sense מּ ׳אֶתמְּֿנֵי‎ is more frequently, see below מָּנִים‎

d. of intercourse of different kinds with another, e.g. after verbs of making a covenant or contract, or (less often) of speaking or dealing: (α) Gen 9:9; 15:18; 17:4 (Ezek 16:60; Isa 59:21 -אוֺת‎) Josh 10:4; 1Kin 3:1 etc.; compare 1Sam 2:13 (but here מֵאֶתֿ הַכֹּהֵן‎ is probably to be read with ᵑ7 ᵑ6 ᵐ5‎ Ke We etc., compare Deut 18:3). (β) Gen 17:3; 42:30 קָשׁוֺת אִתָּנוּ דִּבֶּר‎, 1Kin 8:15; Psa 109:2, & especially in Jeremiah and Ezekiel (as Jer 1:16; 4:12 [52:9 אִתּוֺ‎] 5:5; 12:1; Ezek 2:1; 3:22, 24, 27; 14:4; 44:5 — all -אוֺת‎); Gen 24:49 to perform kindness אֵת‎ (עִם‎ is here more Genl.), 2Sam 16:17 אֶתרֵֿעֶךָ֑ חַסְדְּךָ זֶה‎, Ruth 2:20; Zech 7:9; Jon 11:27 רָעָה אִתִּי עשֶֹׁה וְאַתָּה‎, Deut 1:30; 10:21; 1Sam 12:7 b, (-אוֺת‎) Jer 21:2; 33:9; Ezek 7:27; 16:59; 22:14; 23:25, 29; 39:24; absolute 17:17; 20:44; Psa 109:21; Zeph 3:19; (γ) in a pregnant sense, (in dealing) with, i.e. towards (rare): Isa 66:14; Psa 67:2 אִתָּנוּ מָּנָיו יָאֵר‎ make his face to shine with (= toward) us (varied from אֶל‎ Num 6:25) Deut 28:8; faithful with Psa 78:8 (compare 78:37 עִם נָכוֺן‎); Ezek 2:6 (אוֺתָ֑ךְ‎); Judg 16:15 אִתִּי אֵין וְלֹבְּךָ‎. (δ) often with verbs of fighting, striving, contending, as Gen 14:2, 8, 9; Num 20:13; Isa 45:9; a 50:8; Psa 35:1; Prov 23:11; with בְמִשְׁמָּט בָּא‎ Psa 143:2 (Isa 3:14 and elsewhere עִם‎).

2 Of localities, especially in the phrase אֵת אֲשֶׁר‎ describing a site: Judg 3:19; 4:11 אֶתקֶֿדֶשׁ אֲשֶׁר‎ which is near Kedesh, 1Kin 9:26; 2Kin 9:27 (compare עם‎ 2, which is commoner in this sense); Ezek 43:8; Exod 33:21 אִתִּי מָקוֺם הִנֵּה‎. Perhaps, anomalously, 1Sam 7:16 at or by all those places (but see Dr); in 2Sam 15:23 אֵת אֶתהַֿמִּדְבָּר, דֶרֶךְ עַלמְּֿנֵי‎ = towards is against analogue: read with ᵐ5‎L בַּמִּדְבָּר אֲשֶׁר הַזַּיִת דֶּרֶךְ עַלמָּֿנָיו‎; 1Kin 9:25 אִתּוֺ‎ beside it (that is, the altar); but עָלָיו‎ 13:1 etc. would be idiomatic, & for אֲשֶׁר אִתּוֺ‎ Klo proposes plausibly אֶתאִֿשֹּׁוֺ‎ (see Exod 30:20).

3 מּ ׳אֵת‎ denotes specially,

a. in one's possession or keeping: Gen 27:15; 30:29 thou knowest ֗֗֗ אִתִּי מִקְנְךָ הָיָה אֲשֶׁר אֵת‎ how thy cattle fared with me, 30:33; Leviticus 5:23; Lev 19:13; Deut 15:3; Judg 17:2; 1Sam 9:7 אִתָּנוּ מָה‎ = what have we ? 25:29; Isa 49:4 my right is with Jehovah (contr. 40:27), Jer 8:8; Psa 38:11 the light of mine eyes also אִתִּי אֵין‎ i.e. is gone from me, Prov 3:28; 8:18; in his power, Jer 10:5 אוֺתָם אֵין הֵיטֵיב‎ is not in their power, perhaps Psa 12:5. A dream, or the word of ׳י‎, is said to be אֵת‎ with a prophet, 2Kin 3:12; Jer 23:28; 27:18. Metaph. of a mental quality, Prov 11:2; 13:10.

b. in one's knowledge or memory: Isa 59:12 אִתָּנוּ מְּשָׁעֵינוּ‎ our transgressions are with us, i.e. present to our minds (|| יְדַעֲנוּם וַעֲוֺנֹתֵינוּ‎), Job 12:3 כְּמוֺאֵֿלָּה אֶתמִֿיאֵֿין‎ with whom are not (i.e. who knoweth not ? τίς οὐ σύνοιδε;) things like these ? 14:5 אִתָּךְ‎ i.e. known to thee, Prov 2:1; Gen 40:14; Jer 12:3 (Ew Gf towards thee, as 1d γ). So אֶתנַֿפְשְׁכֶם אִםיֵֿשׁ‎ Gen 23:8 [2Kin 9:15 ׳נ‎ alone], אֶתלְֿבָבְךָ‎ 10:15. compare עם‎ 4b, which is more frequent in this sense.

4 מֵאֵת‎ (מֵאִתִּי‎, etc.; also -מֵאוֺת‎) from proximity with (like Greek παρά with a Genitive, From de chez; in Syriac, Arabic , corresponding synonym מֵעִם‎; see below): coupled almost always with persons (contrast מֵעִם‎, a). Thus

a. with קָנָה‎ to buy Gen 25:10 + often (compare 17:27); לָקַח‎ 42:24; Exod 25:2; Lev 25:36; Numbers 17:17 + often; נָשָׂא‎ Psa 24:5; שִׁלַּח‎, as Gen 8:8 מֵאִתּוֺ אֶתהַֿיּוֺנָה וַיְשַׁלַּח‎ and he sent forth the dove from with him 26:27; הָלַךְ‎ 26:31; 1Kin 18:12; 20:36; Jer 9:1, of a wife deserting her husband Judg 19:2 מֵאִתּוֺ וַתֵּלֶךְ‎, Jer 3:1 (compare Isa 57:8); with simile words Gen 38:1; Deut 2:8; 1Kin 11:23; Jer 2:37 (see Exod 5:20); Isa 54:10; Psa 66:20; with שָׁאַל‎ Judg 1:14; 1Kin 2:16; Psa 27:4 +, דָּרַשׁ‎ 1Kin 22:7 and elsewhere, שָׁמַע‎ 1Sam 2:23. — מּ מְּנֵי ׳מֵאֵת‎ Gen 27:30; 43:34; Exod 10:11; Job 2:7; Lev 10:4 (הַקֹּדֶשׁ‎), 2Kin 16:14 (הַבַּיִת‎). Hence

b. of rights or dues, handed over from, given on the part of, any one: Gen 47:22 מַּרְעֹה מֵאֵת חֹק‎; often in P, as 23:20; Exod 27:21 a perpetual due יִשְׂרָאֵל בְּנֵי מֵאֵת‎ from, or on the part of, the children of Israel, Lev 7:34; b 24:8; Num 3:9; 7:84 +; Deut 18:3; 1Sam 2:13 (ᵐ5‎, etc.; see 1d) 2Sam 15:3 הַמֶּלֶךְ מֵאֵת אֵיןלְֿךָ וְשֹׁמֵעַ‎ but there is none to hear thee deputed of the king, 1Kin 5:14.

c. expressing origination: 1Kin 1:27 נִהְיָה הַמֶּלֶךְ אֲדֹנִי מֵאֵת אִם‎. Especially י ׳מֵאֵת‎ — of a concrete object proceeding from him: Gen 19:24 (brimstone), Num 11:31 (a wind), 16:35 (fire), 1Sam 16:14 (evil spirit), Isa 38:7 (a sign), Jer 51:53 (wasters), Micah 5:6 (dew); of wrath Zech 7:12 (compare Num 17:11), teaching Isa 51:4, the word of prophecy Jer 7:1 (so 11:1; 18:1 +? Jeremiah) 37:17; Ezek 33:30; with 'have I((we) heard' Isa 21:10; 28:22; Jer 49:14 (= Obadiah); of an event, or phase of history Josh 11:20 י ׳מֵאֵת הָֽיְתָה‎ it came of ׳י‎ to..., 1Kin 12:24; Hab 2:13; Psa 118:23 י ׳מֵאֵת זאֹת הָֽיְתָה‎ (ᵐ5‎ παρὰ Κυρίου) Ezra 9:8; Neh 6:16; of trouble (רָעָה‎) 2Kin 6:33; Micah 1:12 (יָרַד‎); of a good or evil lot, having its source in ׳י‎ Jer 13:25; Isa 54:17; Psa 109:20, compare Job 2:10; Psa 22:26 תְהִלָּתִי מֵאִתְּךָ‎ from thee cometh my praise (thou art the source of it); Isa 44:24 Qr מֵאִתִּי‎ of myself (compare ἀπ’ ἐμαυτοῦ John 5:30; Kt is אִתִּי מִי‎ who was with me ?), John 54:15 מֵאוֺתִי אֶפֶס‎ not at my instance (compare מִנִּי לֹא‎ John 30:1, מִמֶּנִּי לֹא‎ Hosea 8:4). d. of a place 1Kin 6:33 (corrupt: read with ᵑ9 ᵑ6 ᵐ5‎ [partly] רְבֻעוֺת מְזוּזֹת‎, & compare Ezek 41:21).Note. אֵת‎ expresses closer association than עִם‎: hence while מֵעִם‎ sometimes denotes hardly more than from the surroundings or belongings of, מֵאֵת‎ expresses from close proximity to. Thus Saul asks, מֵעִמָּנוּ הָלַךְ מִי‎ who has gone from (those) about us ? but Jacob, speaking of the loss of Joseph, says, Gen 44:28 מֵאִתִּי הָאֶחָד וַיֵּצֵא‎ and the one is gone from with me. מֵאֵת‎ is accordingly preferred to מֵעִם‎ in the sense of origination or authorship; מאת‎ is not usual in the sense of מֵעִם‎
