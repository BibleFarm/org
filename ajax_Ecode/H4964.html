H4964. metheg

מֶ֫תֶג‎ noun masculine bridle (Aramaic (ᵑ7‎) מִתְגָּא‎; Late Hebrew מֶתֶג‎ of secondary accent ָֽ‎; מִתֵּג‎ Pi`el denominative put on a bridle); —

1 literally bridle, for animals; לַחֲמוֺר מֶתֶג‎ Prov 26:3 a whip for the horse, a bridle for the ass, etc.; ׳בְּמ וָרֶסֶן‎ Psa 32:9 with bridle and halter (on text of verse compare Che); of ׳י‎'s birdle for Sennach. (under figure of beast), בִּשְׂפָתֶיךָ וּמִתְגִּי‎ 2Kin 19:28 and I will put (וְשַׂמְתִּי‎) my hook in thy nose and my bridle in thy lips = Isa 37:29 (see DrIs. ed. 2, 220).

2 figurative = control, authority, only in ׳מ הָאַמָּה‎ 2Sam 8:1authority of the mother city, according to MV Buhl, of especially We Dr; Bu omit as corrupt (see I. אַמָּה‎, above); see, further, HPS.
