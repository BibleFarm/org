כַּר‎ (kar｜kar｜noun｜masculine｜basket-saddle｜he-lamb｜pasture)
[Heb] כַּר‎ ETCBC: כַּר‎ (subs｜basket-saddle), כַּר‎ (subs｜ram), כַּר‎ (subs｜pasture) OSHL: k.cx.af, k.df.ab, k.ax.ab TWOT: 1046a GK: H4119, H4120, H4121 Greek: ἐρίφιον, ἔριφος, ἑρπετόν, τόπος, χάραξ
Derivation: from כָּרַר‎ in the sense of plumpness;
Strong's: a ram (as full-grown and fat), including a battering-ram (as butting); hence, a meadow (as for sheep); also a pad or camel's saddle (as puffed out)
KJV: captain, furniture, lamb, (large) pasture, ram. See also בֵּית כַּר‎, כָּרִי‎.
