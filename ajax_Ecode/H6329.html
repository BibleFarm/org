פּוּק‎ (pûwq｜pook｜verb｜bring out)
[Heb] פּוּק‎ ETCBC: פוק‎ (verb｜totter) OSHL: q.at.aa TWOT: 1748 GK: H7048, H7049 Greek: δίδωμι, ἑτοιμάζω, εὑρίσκω, κινέω, λαμβάνω
Derivation: a primitive root (identical with פּוּק‎ through the idea of dropping out; compare נְפַק‎);
Strong's: to issue, i.e. furnish; causatively, to secure; figuratively, to succeed
KJV: afford, draw out, further, get, obtain.

H6329. puq

II. מּוּק‎ verb Hiph`il bring out, furnish, promote (Late Hebrew id.; apparently akin to Aramaic נְפַק‎ go forth, Aph`el bring forth, produce); —

1 produce, furnish: Imperfect (jussive) לָרָעֵב וְתָפֵק‎ Isa 58:10 with נַפְשְׁךָ‎ (read perhaps לַחְמְךָ‎ see CheHpt); Participle אֶלזַֿן מִזַּן מְפִיקִים‎ Psa 144:13 (see זַן‎).

2 bring out, elicit, obtain, with accusative; יָפִיק‎ Prov 3:13, + מִן‎ person, וַיָּפֶק‎ 8:35; 18:22 (all || מצא‎), 12:2.

3 אַלתָּֿפֵק זְמָמוֺ‎ Psa 140:9 his device do not promote.
