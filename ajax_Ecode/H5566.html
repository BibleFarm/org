[Heb] סֶמֶל‎ ETCBC: סֶמֶל‎ (subs｜image) OSHL: o.bt.ad TWOT: 1515 GK: H6166
Derivation: or סֵמֶל‎; from an unused root meaning to resemble;
Strong's: a likeness
KJV: figure, idol, image.

H5566. semel

סֶ֫מֶל‎ noun masculineEzek 8:5 perhaps originally proper name, of divinity, then Generally image, statue (Phoenician סמל‎ id., feminine סמלת‎, Lzb151, 329, also פנסמלת‎ Id329; BauRel. i. 88 understands ׳ס‎ to be designation of foreign god); — absolute ׳הַסּ‎2Chr 33:7, 15, סָ֑מֶל‎ Deut 4:16; construct סֵ֫מֶל‎ Ezek 8:3, 5; — image, figure of anything, כָּלסֿ תְּמוּנַת ׳מֶּסֶל‎ Deut 4:16; idol-image הַסּ ׳מֶּסֶל‎2Chr 33:7 and (מֶּסֶל‎ omitted) 33:15; הַקִּנְאָה סֵמֶל‎ Ezek 8:3 the statue of jealousy, i.e. that rouses ׳י‎'s jealousy, = 8:5.

סמם‎ (√ of following; meaning dubious; if akin to Arabic smell Lane1593, any fragrant plant Id1594, then ס‎ must be for שׂ‎; in that case ׳ס‎ perhaps loan-word in Hebrew; Late Hebrew id., spice, drug (Ecclus ׳שׂ‎ Ecclesiasticus 38:4 m.), Aramaic סַמָּא‎ id.; drug, pigment; Arabic poison is apparently loan-word Frä262).
