H4966. mathoq

or mathuq מָתוֺק‎ adjective sweet, and

noun [masculine] sweetness (on form see Lag BN 30, 60, compare BaNB 13, 36); — absolute ׳מ‎ Judg 14:14 9t.; feminine מְתוּקָה‎ Eccl 5:11; plural מְתוּקִים‎ Psa 19:11; —

1 sweet, of honey Judg 14:14 (where ׳מ‎ = substantive a sweet thing), 14:18 (both Samson's riddle), Prov 24:13; so = sweetness, Ezek 3:3; more Generally, מ ׳כָּלמַֿר‎ Prov 27:7 every bitter thing is sweet to one hungry; figurative Psa 19:11 (God's commandments, sweeter than honey), compare Prov 16:24 (simile of pleasant words) מ ׳וּפִרְיוֺ לְחִכִּי‎ Song 2:3 (i.e his caresses); לְמ מַר ׳שָׂמִים ׳וּמ לְמָ֑ר‎ Isa 5:20 making bitter into sweet and sweet into bitter (obscuring moral distinctions; || רָ֑ע וְלַטּוֺב טוֺב לָרַע הָאֹמְרִים‎).

2 = pleasant, sleep of the toiler Eccl 5:11; light 1:17 (|| אֶתהַֿשֶּׁמֶשׁ לִרְאוֺת לַעֵינַיִם טוֺב‎).
