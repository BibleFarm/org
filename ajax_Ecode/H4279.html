מָחָר‎ (mâchâr｜maw-khar'｜noun｜masculine｜to-morrow)
[Heb] מָחָר‎ ETCBC: מָחָר‎ (subs｜next day) OSHL: m.bq.ab TWOT: 1185a GK: H4737 Greek: αὔριον, ἐπαύριον
Derivation: probably from אָחַר‎;
Strong's: properly, deferred, i.e. the morrow; usually (adverbially) tomorrow; indefinitely, hereafter
KJV: time to come, tomorrow.

H4279. machar

מָחָר‎52 noun masculine, used often as

adverb, tomorrow, in time to come (Aramaic מְחַר‎ ; probably properly time in front, Köii. 263, compare √, and Assyrian ma—ru, front, often of time, though always of former time, of old, DlHWB 402); —

1 to-morrow (the day following the present day),

a. as substantive Exod 16:23 לְי קֹדֶשׁ שַׁבַּת ׳שַׁבָּתוֺן מָחָר‎ to-morrow is a sabbath, etc., 1Sam 20:5 מָחָר חֹדֶשׁ‎ to-morrow is the new moon, 20:18; in מָחָר יוֺם‎ Gen 30:33; Prov 27:1; Isa 56:12; in the idiomatic phrase מָחָר כָּעֵת‎ at the like of the time, (being) to-morrow, = about to-morrow (see כְּ‎ 1a) Exod 9:18; 1Sam 9:16; 20:12; 1Kin 19:2; 20:6; 2Kin 7:1, 18; 10:6; and in לְמָחָר‎ Exod 8:19 הַזֶּה הָאֹת יִהְיֶה לְמָחָר‎, = for, against, to-morrow, 8:6; Num 11:18; Josh 7:13; Est 5:12.

b. as adverb accusative Exod 8:25; 9:5 ׳י הַזֶּה הַדָּבָר יַעֲשֶׂה מָחָר‎, 10:4 אַרְבֶּה מָחָר מֵבִיא הִנְנִי‎, 17:9; 32:5; Num 14:25; 1Sam 11:9 etc., Josh 11:6 הַזּאֹת כָּעֵת מָחָר‎; opposed to הַיּוֺם‎ Exod 19:10 וּמָחָר הַיּוֺם‎, Josh 22:18; 2Sam 11:12a; rhetorically, Isa 22:13 נָמוּת מָחָר כִּי‎.

2 = in future time, Exod 13:14 לֵאמֹר מָחָר בִנְךָ יִשְׁאָֽלְךָ כִּי‎ = Deut 6:20; Josh 4:6, 21; 22:24, 27, 28; so מָחָר בְּיוֺם‎ Gen 30:33.
