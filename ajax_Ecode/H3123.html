יֹונָה‎ (yôwnâh｜yo-naw'｜noun｜feminine｜dove)
[Heb] יֹונָה‎, יׄונָה‎ ETCBC: יֹונָה‎ (subs｜dove) OSHL: j.ba.ab TWOT: 854a GK: H3433 Greek: περιστερά
Derivation: probably from the same as יַיִן‎;
Strong's: a dove (apparently from the warmth of their mating)
KJV: dove, pigeon.

H3123. yonah

I. יוֺנָה‎ noun feminine dove (Late Hebrew id.; Aramaic id., ) — absolute יוֺנָה‎ Gen 8:8 20t.; construct יוֺנֵת‎ Psa 56:1; suffix יוֺנָתִי‎ Song 2:14 2t.; plural יוֺנִים‎ Nahum 2:8 6t.; construct יוֺנֵי‎ Ezek 7:16 (see below); — dove Gen 8:8-9, 10, 11, 12 (all J); often of offerings, בֶּןיֿוֺנָה‎ Lev 12:6 (P; an individual of the species; || תֹּר‎); (ה)יוֺנִים בְּנֵי‎ || תֹּרִיםׅ‎ 1:14; 5:7, 11; 12:8; 14:22, 30; 15:14, 29; Num 6:10 (all P); יוֺנִים חֲרִי‎ dove's dung 2Kin 6:25 (Kt, yet see GeiUrschrift 409); in various similes: Ephraim is מּוֺתָה כְּיוֺנָה‎ like a silly dove Hosea 7:11 (allowing itself to be snared); simile of return of exiles, like eager flight of doves 11:11, of ships with white outspread sails Isa 60:8; כַּיּוֺנָה אֵבֶר‎ Psa 55:7; see also יוֺנָה כַּנְפֵי‎ 68:14; simile of fugitive Moab Jer 48:28; also הַגֵּאָיוֺת כְּיוֺנֵי‎ Ezek 7:16 like the doves of the valleys (but on text see Co, who emends הוֺגִיּוֺת כיונים‎, but regards phrase as gloss): simile of mourning יוֺנִים כְּקוֺל‎ Nahum 2:8; without כיונים הגה קוֺל,‎ Isa 38:14; 59:11 (from mournful note); figurative of beauty (only Canticles): יוֺנִים עֵינַיִךְ‎ thine eyes are (those of) doves Song 1:15; 4:1, see also 4:12; term of endearment, יוֺנָתִי‎ my dove 2:14; 5:2; 6:9. Elsewhere only Psa 56:1 (title) רְחֹקִים אֵלֶם יוֺנַת (עַל)‎ probably name of melody: To 'the dove of distant terebinths' (read אֵלִים‎ for אֵלֶם‎; see אֵלֶם‎ above, and BaePsalms, p. xvii). — On sanctity of dove among Shemites, see RSSemitic i. 202, 275.
