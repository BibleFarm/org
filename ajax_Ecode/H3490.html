H3490. yathom

יָתוֺם‎ noun [masculine] orphan (Late Hebrew id.; Aramaic יַתְמָא‎, ; Phoenician יתם‎; Arabic pupil, orphan) — יָתוֺם‎ Exod 22:21 29t.; plural יְתוֺמִים‎ 22:23 8t.; suffix יְתֹמֶיךָ‎ Jer 49:11; יְתֹמָיו‎ Isa 9:16; לִיתוֺמָיו‎ Psa 109:12; — orphan, i.e. fatherless (|| אַלְמָנָה‎) Exod 22:23; Psa 109:9; Lam 5:3 (|| אָב אַין‎); Job 24:9; Psa 109:12; probably also Exod 22:21; Deut 10:18; 14:29; 16:11, 14; 24:17, 19, 20, 21; 26:12-13, 27:19; Psa 94:6; 146:9; Jer 7:6; 22:3; Zech 7:10; Mal 3:5 (in all these, mentioned as helpless, exposed to injury, || אַלְמָנָה‎ and גֵּר‎); similarly, || אַלְמָנָה‎ Job 22:9; 24:3; 29:12; 31:17; Psa 68:6; Isa 1:17, 23; 9:16; 10:2; Jer 49:11; Ezek 22:7; without אלמנה‎ Hosea 14:4; Job 6:27; 31:21; Psa 10:14; 10:18; Prov 23:10; Jer 5:28 (in no case clear that both parents are dead).
