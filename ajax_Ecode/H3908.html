[Heb] לַחַשׁ‎ ETCBC: לַחַשׁ‎ (subs｜whispering) OSHL: l.bk.ab TWOT: 1107a GK: H4318 Greek: ἀκροατής, ψιθυρισμός
Derivation: from לָחַשׁ‎;
Strong's: properly, a whisper, i.e. by implication, (in a good sense) a private prayer, (in a bad one) an incantation; concretely, an amulet
KJV: charmed, earring, enchantment, orator, prayer.

H3908. lachash

לַ֫חַשׁ‎ noun [masculine] a whispering, charming; — ׳ל‎ Isa 26:16; לָ֑חַשׁ‎ Eccl 10:11; Isa 3:3; Jer 8:17; plural לְחָשִׁים‎ Isa 3:20. —

1 serpent-charming Eccl 10:11; לָ֑חַשׁ נְבוֺן‎ Isa 3:3 (probably more General).

2 charms, amulets, worn by women, Isa 3:20.

3 whisper of prayer, לַחַשׁ צָקוּן‎ Isa 26:16 they pour forth a whisper of prayer (but Koppe Bö Gr Di Du read לַחַשׁ צְקוֺן‎ 'Zauberzwang,' compulsion of (by) magic).
