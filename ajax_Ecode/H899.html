H899. beged

I. בֶּ֫גֶד‎ noun [masculine] treachery, Isa 24:16; Jer 12:1.

II. בֶּ֫גֶד‎ noun masculine (except Lev 6:20 עָלֶיהָ‎, but Samaritan עליו‎, compare Di in loco Köi. 182) garment, covering — Gen 28:2 36t.; suffix בִּגְדוֺ‎ Ezek 9:3 + (14 t. without dag. lene GesLgb. 94); plural בְּגָדִים‎ Lev 6:4 32t.; construct בִּגְדֵי‎ Gen 27:15 39t.; suffix בְּגָדֶיךָ‎ 1Kin 22:20 81t.; בִּגְדֹתֶיךָ‎ Psa 45:9; —

1 garment, clothing, raiment, robe of any kind, from the filthy clothing of the leper to the holy robes of the high priest, the simplest covering of the poor as well as the costly raiment of the rich and noble, used throughout Hebrew Literature: Gen 24:53 (J), 28:20 (E; 14 t. J E), Exod 28:2 + (P 90 t.), Deut 24:17; Judg 8:26 4t., 1Sam 19:13 10t., 1Kin 1:1 23t., 2Chr 18:9 + (Chronicles 9 t.), Est 4:1, 4; Job 13:28; 22:6; 37:17; Psa 22:19; 45:9; 102:27; 109:19; Prov 6:27; 20:16; 25:20; 27:13; Eccl 9:8; Isa2 Isa 24:16; 36:22; 37:1; Isa3 50:9-10t.; Jer 12:1 3t., Ezek 16:16 13t.; Joel 2:13; Amos 2:8; Hag 2:12; Zech 3:3-4, 5; 14:14; בִגְדוֺ מְלֹא‎ his lapful 2Kin 4:39.

2 covering, wrapping, of furniture of tabernacle Num 4:6-13 (6 t.); coverlet of a bed 1Sam 19:13.
