גָּאַל‎ (gâʼal｜gaw-al'｜verb｜defile)
[Heb] גָּאַל‎ ETCBC: גאל‎ (verb｜pollute) OSHL: c.ad.aa TWOT: 301 GK: H1458 Greek: μολύνω
Derivation: a primitive root, (rather identified with גָּאַל‎, through the idea of freeing, i.e. repudiating);
Strong's: to soil or (figuratively) desecrate
KJV: defile, pollute, stain.

H1351. gaal

II. [גָּאַל‎] verb defile, late (compare גָּעַל‎) —

Niph`al Perfect3masculine plural נְגֹאֲלוּ‎ Isa 59:3; Lam 4:14, on form see Ges§ 51, 2, Köi. 265; Participle נִגְאָלָה‎ Zeph 3:1; — be defiled, hands with blood בַּדָּם‎, Isa 59:3 compare Lam 4:14; participle as substantive defiled, polluted one Zeph 3:1 of Jerusalem (|| מֹרְאָה‎; appositive הַיּוֺנָה הָעִיר‎).

Pi`el Perfect1plural suffix גֵּאַלְנוּךָ‎ Mal 1:7 pollute, desecrate, object ׳י‎ (desecrated in his altar).

Pu`al Imperfect מִןהַֿכְּהֻנָּה וַיְגֹאֲלוּ‎ Ezra 2:62 = Neh 7:64 construct pregnant and they were desecrated out of the priesthood, i.e. deposed, as desecrated one; Participle מְגֹאָל‎ of bread laid on Yahweh's altar Mal 1:7; of Yahweh's altar (table, שֻׁלְחָן‎) 1:12.

Hiph`il Perfect1singular אֶגְאָ֑לְתִּי‎ Isa 63:3 (on Aramaic form see Köi. 27 Ges§ 53 R. 6 but) read perhaps גֵּאָ֑לְתִּי‎ Pi`el, see GFMThLZ 1887, 292 compare also Gesl.c. N. Ol§ 255 b Sta§ 159 b, 3; — I have polluted, i.e. stained all my raiment.

Hithpa`el Imperfect יִתְגָּאַל‎ Dan 1:8; יִתְגָּאָ֑ל‎ 1:8; — defile himself.
