H4616. maan

[מַ֫עַן‎] substantive purpose, intent, only with לְ‎, in לְמַ֫עַן‎271

preposition and

conjunction for the sake of, on account of, to the intent or in order that (abbreviated from מַעֲנֶה‎: compare מַ֫עַל‎; Köii. 116); — with suffix לְמַעַנְךָ לְמַעֲנִי,‎, and לְמַעַנְכֶם‎; — 1. preposition: —

a. Gen 18:24 wilt thou ... not spare the place הציקים חמשׁים למען‎ for the sake of the 50 righteous? Deut 30:6 ׳ל חַיֶּיךָ‎ for thy life's sake, 1Kin 8:41 the foreigner who comer from afar ׳ל שְׁמֶ֑ךָ‎, Isa 43:14 בָבֶ֫לָה שִׁלַּחְתִּי לְמַעַנְכֶם‎, 45:4; 62:1 ׳ל ציון‎, 63:17 ׳ל עבדיך‎, 65:8; Ezek 36:22 (twice in verse); Job 18:4; Psa 122:8; 122:9; (ד ׳עַבְדִּי‎) דָּוִד לְמַעַן‎, i.e. for the sake of David's memory, and the promises given to him, 1Kin 11:12-13, 32, 34; 15:4; 2Kin 8:19; 19:34 (= Isa 37:35), 20:6 (all D2); לְמַעֲנִי‎ (of ׳י‎) for my own sake, i.e. to vindicate my name, 2Kin 19:34 (= Isa 37:35), 2Kin 20:6; Isa 43:25; 48:11 אֶעֲשֶׁה לְמַעֲנִי לְמַעֲנִי‎, so לְמַעַנְךָ‎ Dan 9:19, ׳ל אֲדֹנָי‎ 9:17; ׳י‎ is said (or entreated) to act (שְׁמִי שִׁמְךָ,‎) שְׁמוֺ לְמַעַן‎ i.e. to maintain his reputation, or character, Psa 23:3; 25:11; 31:4; 79:9; 106:8; 143:11; Jer 14:7, 21; Ezek 20:9, 14, 22, 44; Isa 48:9; simile ׳ל חַסְדְּךָ‎ (i.e. to maintain it consistently) Psa 6:5; Psalm 44:27, ׳ל טוּבְךָ‎ 25:7, ׳ל צִדְקוֺ‎ Isa 42:21.

b. In view of, on account of (but not expressing causation distinctly, like מִן‎ 2f, or מִמְּנֵי‎), Deut 3:26 י ׳ויתעבר לְמַעַנְכֶם בי‎ on your account, 1Kin 11:39 ׳ל זֹאת‎ in view of this, ׳ל שׁוֺרֲרַי‎ on account of my watchful foes, Psa 5:9; 27:11, ׳ל צורריך‎ 8:3, simil. 69:19; 48:12 (97:8) ל ֗֗֗ ציון הר ׳ישמח משׁפטיך‎ in view of thy judgments.

c. Sq. infinitive Gen 18:19 י הָבִיא ׳לְמַעַן‎ for the purpose of J.'s bringing = to the intent that J. might bring, 37:22 ׳ל הַצִּיל‎ in order to rescue, 50:20; Exod 1:11; 9:16; 10:1, etc., Deut 2:30; Deuteronomy 6:28, etc., 2Kin 10:19; Jer 7:10, 18; 11:5; 50:34 (on הִרגיע‎, see עַד‎

2a b), Ezek 14:5; 21:5; 22:6 etc., 36:5, + often (Köiii.595 f.). Once, pleonastic, לֵב לָמוּג לְמַעַן‎ 21:20 (compare לְ כַּעֲבוּר‎ 1Chr 19:3; but read probably הִמּוֺג‎).

2. conjunction (followed by imperfect): —

a. אֲשֶׁר לְמַעַן‎ Gen 18:19 I have known him ׳ל יְצַוֶּה אשׁר‎ to the end that he might command, etc., Deut 27:3; Josh 3:4; 2Sam 13:5; Jer 42:6; more often

b. without אשׁר‎, as Gen 12:13 לִי יִיטַב לְמַעַן‎, 27:25; Exod 4:5 ׳ל יאמינו‎, 8:6; 8:18; 9:29; 10:2; Isa 5:19; 23:16; Psa 9:15; Psalm 30:13; 48:14 + often (Köiii.571).

c. Sq. לֹא‎ (a) ׳ל לֹא אשׁר‎ Num 17:5; Deut 20:18; Ezek 31:14; 36:30; 46:18; (b) ׳ל לֹא‎ 14:11; 19:9; 25:10; 26:20; Zech 12:7; Psa 119:11; 119:80; 125:3 (Köiii.574. Less common than לְבִלְתִּי‎ or מִן‎ with infinitive, or מֶּןֿ‎).

Schr. i. 397 f.).

Note 2. — In Josh 4:24 for the anomalous ׳ל יְרָאתֶם‎, read infinitive ׳ל יִרְאָתָם‎ in order that they might fear (Dr§ 14 Obs.): Neh 6:13 אִירָא לְמַעַן הוּא שָׂכוּר לְמַעַן‎ (si vera 1.) the first ׳ל‎ points forwards, to this intent was he hired, to the intent that I should be afraid. — On Prov 16:4, see מַעֵנֶה‎ above
