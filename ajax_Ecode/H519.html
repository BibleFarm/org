H519. amah

אָמָה‎ noun feminine maid, handmaid (Phoenician אמת‎, Arabic , Sabean אמת‎ in proper name SabDenkm20, Aramaic , Assyrian amtu ZimBP 67) — ׳א‎ absolute Gen 21:10 +; suffix אֲמָתִי‎ 30:3 +, etc.; plural absolute אֲמָהֹת‎ 31:33; 2Sam 6:22; construct אַמְהוֺת‎ 6:20; אַמְהֹתַי‎ Job 19:5 etc. — maid, handmaid.

1 literally maidservant (= שִׁפְחָה‎ q. v., where however sometimes more servile; rarely P in Hexateuch) Gen 30:3; 31:33 (|| שִׁפְחָה‎ 29:24, 29; P 30:4f. J etc.) Exod 2:5 (all E); 2Sam 6:20, 22; Job 19:15; Nahum 2:8 (suffix refers to mistress, except Job 19:15 compare 2Sam 6:20, 22); in legisl. (|| עֶבֶד‎) Exod 20:10, 17; 21:20, 26, 27, 32 (all E) Lev 25:6, 44 (twice in verse) (H) Deut 5:14 (twice in verse); 5:18; 12:12, 18; 15:17 (compare see 12) 16:11, 14; compare also Job 31:13; Ezra 2:65; Neh 7:67; applied to concubine (suffix of master) Gen 20:17; 21:12; Exod 23:12 (all E) Judg 9:18; 19:19; also Gen 21:10 (twice in verse); 21:13 (16:1f. P שִׁפְחָה‎ q. v.) Exod 21:7 (all E), see on this StaGesch i: p. 380.

2 figurative in address, אֲמָֽתְךָ‎ etc., referring to speaker, in token of humility; Ruth 3:9 (twice in verse) (|| שׁפחה‎ 2:13) 1Sam 1:16 (|| id. 1:18) 25:24 (twice in verse); 25:25, 28, 31, 41 (|| id. 25:27) 2Sam 14:15-16, (|| id. 14:6; 14:7; 14:12; 14:15; 14:17; 14:19); 20:17; 1Kin 1:13, 17; 3:20; in addressing God (never שׁפחה‎) 1Sam 1:11 (3 t. in verse), compare ׳בֶּןאֿ‎ Psa 86:16; 116:16.
