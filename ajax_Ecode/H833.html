אָשַׁר‎ (ʼâshar｜aw-shar'｜verb｜go straight)
[Heb] אָשַׁר‎ ETCBC: אשׁר‎ (verb｜be happy), אשׁר‎ (verb｜walk straight) OSHL: a.gj.aa TWOT: 183 GK: H886, H887 Greek: ἀσφαλής, ζηλόω, κατευθύνω, μακαρίζω, πλανάω
Derivation: or אָשֵׁר‎; a primitive root;
Strong's: to be straight (used in the widest sense, especially to be level, right, happy); figuratively, to go forward, be honest, proper
KJV: (call, be) bless(-ed, happy), go, guide, lead, relieve.

H833. ashar

[אָשַׁר‎] verb go straight, go on, advance (Assyrian ašâru ZimBP 11; Arabic, Aramaic in derivatives) —

Qal Imperative אִשְׁרוּ‎ Prov 9:6 בִּינָה בְדֶרֶךְ אִשְׁרוּ‎ go straight on in the way of understanding.

1 intensive go straight on, advance Prov 4:14.

3 set right, righten Isa 1:17.

4 pronounce happy, call blessed Gen 30:13 (J) Job 29:11; Psa 72:17; Prov 31:28; Song 6:9; Mal 3:12, 15.

Pu`al Imperfect יְאֻשַּׁר‎ Psa 41:3; Participle מְאֻשָּׁר‎ Prov 3:18; Isa 9:15.

1 be led on Isa 9:15.

2 be made happy, blessed Psa 41:3; Prov 3:18.
