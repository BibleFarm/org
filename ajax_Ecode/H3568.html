H3568. Kuwsh

I. כּוּשׁ‎ proper name, masculine person, of a people and

territory (Egyptian Kœš, SteindBAS i, 593, Assyrian Kûsu, Idib. DlPa 251; Kaši in Tel Amarna tablets, see WklTel Am. 39*); —

1 1st 'son' of חָם‎ Gen 10:6-7, (P) = 1Chr 1:8-9, ᵐ5‎ Ξους, ᵑ9‎ Chus, from whom descended according to these verses the southernmost peoples known to Hebrews.

2 land and people of southern Nile-valley, or Upper Egypt, extending from Syene (Ezek 29:10) indefinitely to the south, ᵐ5‎ Αιθιοπια, Αιθιοπες:

a. the land Isa 11:11; 18:1; Zeph 3:10; Ezek 29:10; Job 28:19; Est 1:1; 8:9.

b. the people Isa 20:4; Jer 46:9; Ezek 38:5; personified Psa 68:32.

c. indeterminate, either land or people, or including both: Isa 20:3, 5; 43:3; 45:14; 2Kin 19:9 = Isa 37:9; Nahum 3:9; Ezek 30:4-5, 9; Psa 87:4 (ᵐ5‎ λαὸς Αιθιοπων).

3 in Gen 10:8 (J) = 1Chr 1:10 כּוּשׁ‎ is error for כַּשׁ‎ = Babylonian Kaššu, according to SchrCOT on Gen 10:6, DlPa 51 ff. 72 f. and most Assyriologists; so perhaps also 2:13 (J), yet see HptÜber Ld. u. Meer, 1894-5, No. 15.

II. כּוּשׁ‎ proper name, masculine a Benjamite, Psa 7:1 (title), ᵐ5‎ Χουσει υιὁῦ Ιεμενει.
