כֶּסֶל‎ (keçel｜keh'-sel｜noun｜masculine｜loins)
[Heb] כֶּסֶל‎ ETCBC: כֶּסֶל‎ (subs｜loin), כֶּסֶל‎ (subs｜imperturbability) OSHL: k.cj.ab TWOT: 1011a GK: H4072, H4073 Greek: ἐλπίς, μηρός, σκάνδαλον
Derivation: from כָּסַל‎;
Strong's: properly, fatness, i.e. by implication (literally) the loin (as the seat of the leaf fat) or (generally) the viscera; also (figuratively) silliness or (in a good sense) trust
KJV: confidence, flank, folly, hope, loin.
